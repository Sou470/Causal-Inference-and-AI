ICEBOAT WANTED
TO SAVEPROPERTY
Board of Trade Secretary
Suggests Change Needed
in Present Craft.
Believing that a new Are and tee
boat on the Potomac would moan sav
ing of much property on the river
above the Aqueduct bridge. J. Harry
Cunningham, secretary of the Botird
of Trade, has written to Commissioner
Ix>uis Brownlow uriring that steps be
taken looking to the construction of
such a boat. Portions of the letter
follow:
"In October, 1914. the Washington
Board of Trade, through its commit
tee on river and harbor improvement,
recommended that an appropriation
be made for the construction of a
new Are and ice boat, with a hinged
smokestack, said boat to be located
in Georgetown. Since then our oom
mittee has on several occasions re
iterated this recommendation.
"The great necessity for this Are
and ice boat was again brought most
forcibly to our attention because of
the serious ice gorges, which did so
much damage to the boathouses and
other property adjacent to the Aque
duct bridge two years ago.
"The same condition prevailing on
the river at this time again confronts
us today. The present iceboat, located
at the 7th street wharf, can only go as
far as the Aqueduct bridge, but can
not go under the bridge because of its
towering smoke-stack. If the Fire
fighter was able to go under the bridge
it would mean that the heavy Ice now
in the river could be broken up and
thus avoid the gorge which is bound
to come with the first moderation of
weather.
"In view of the urgent necessity of
something being done in this connec
tion, we are writing to ask if there is
a fund available which would permit
having the smokestack of the Fire
lighter hinged so that this boat could
go under the Aqueduct bridge and
other bridges if necessary."
It is said that proprietors of the
different boathouses are now moving
their canoes and other craft awayjrom
the river in anticipation of a new jam
when the weather moderates.

Charles Dathrop J'ack, president of
the American Forestry Association of
Washington, in presenting the tree
seeds to the consuls of Belgium,
France and Great Britain.
Glory of Those Who Heard Call.
"They will tell the glory of those
who heard that call from across three
thousand miles of water. We on this
side are planting memorial trees and
roads of remembrance, hut this gift
to help reforest the battle areas and
the areas in Great Britain which were
sacrificed to war's demands will, I be
lieve. do more than anything else to
ward cementing the friendship that
was born of war and baptized in
blood.

"The American Forestry Association

this gift to your governments. May
every tree seed prosper and grow

FISH BOYCOTT PUT ON UNIONS
Supply in Two Cities Cut One-Half,
Ship Owners Say.
BOSTON, January 15.?A reduction
of approximately 50 per cent in the
amount of fish supplied to New York
and Boston markets resulted from an
alleged boycott by unions, according to
bills in equity filed in the supreme court
here yesterday.
The plaintiffs, who are owners or
captains of vessels engaged in fishing
off Cape Cod and the islands of Nan
tucket and Marthas Vineyard, ask the
court to enjoin the fishermen's union
of the Atlantic and Fish Handlers'
Union, No. 15,753 from further con
tinuing the boycott, the purpose of
which is said to be to prevent trans
portation, handling and sale of any fish
caught by the plaintiffs.
The plaintiffs say that the principal
objects for which the unions were
formed were to acquire union domina
tion and monopolistic control of the
fishing industry along the Atlantic
coast, and to compel all journeymen
engaged in catching or handling fish to
join one union or the other.

The joint commission is meeting in
the Forth Street Methodist Episcopal
Church South, from which announce
ment came seventy-four years ago
that owing to a disagreement on the
slavery question a breach had com'
about and thenceforth there would be
northern and southern branches of
the church.

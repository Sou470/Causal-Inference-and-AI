Before filling'the cooling: system with non-freezing: mixture, it is well
to be certain that the radiator passages are not partially stopped up with

pure water way be trapped in some of the passages, which will not be
replaced by the an ti-freeze mixture. This may freeze and cause leaks to

commonly used for cleaning out clogged radiators. Usually the instruction

a j.1. measurement of the water drawn out of the system from a full
condition checks up with the instruction book statement, the radiator
piping: and so forth are probably fairly clear. If however, considerably
less water can be drained from the system than should be. it is likely that
the radiator is holding back some dead water and that it needs an "internal
bath of alkali. On some cars the engine should be run while water is
being drawn off, so as to keep the pump in action. Knowing the total
capacity of the cooling system, for liquid, either by measurement or from
the instruction book, it is very easy to prepare the denatured alcoholic

clutch is somewhat worn, hut seem
ingly not enough to cause this trou
ble. What is the remedy?
Answer: In the case of a leather
lined cone clutch such as this, about
the only thing that can cause the
"dragging" that you experience is
lost motion in the throwout mechan
ism. As the movement of the throw
out collar is very slight in propor
tion to that of the pedal, in order
to give the required leverage, a very
slight amount of wear at the collar,
at the lingers which act upon it or
in any other part of the mechanism
will reduce the separation between
the clutch cone and the flywheel re
cess to such an extent as to make
the releasing action incomplete. In
our opinion, the replacement of all
worn parts of the disengaging
mechanism is the proper remedy for
vour difficulty. Lack of lubrication
of the clutch shaft bearing may also
cause the clutch to keep spinning
when in the released position.

Electric Tall-Light on Ford.
H. N. asks: How can I connect an
electric tail-lamp on jny Ford, so that
it shall light when the headlamps are
put on?
Answer: On Fords of recent model
the tail-lamp bulb should be of two
candlepower and eighteen volts. A
wire should be run from the connec
tion of the regular lighting switch, to
which the headlight wire is connected,
back to the. tail-lamp. If the lamp is
arranged to use a single contact bulb
it will be necessary to so mount the
lamp that it shall make an electrical
connection with the frame of the car.
If a double contact bulb is used, one
of its contacts should be connected to
the wire from the switch and a wire
from the other contact should be run
to a firm electrical connection on the
car frame. With this arrangement
the tail-light will burn whenever the
headlights are on.

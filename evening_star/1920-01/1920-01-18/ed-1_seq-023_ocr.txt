BY WALTER CAMP.
There is an opportunity for a really

by this country to many of otlr allies,
and that opportunity has come before
I us now with renewed emphasis since
the war. The United States have car
j ried the matter of training in athletic

any other country?in fact, with the
[exception of Great Britain this sci
I ence has been neglected in all these
i foreign countries save where an occa

The organisation vthat has come
I closest in contact with this fact has
been the T. M. C. A., and during the
war hundreds of their directors made
names for themselves abroad in this
paiticular branch. This is all now
bearing fruit, and th;s organization
is besieged with requests for athletic

AVe ought to send more men and
more highly advanced men. A few of
our best have gone, but any school or

Editorial
The other day Mr. In A.
Hurry rushed into a garage
with an inner tube over his
arm. and demanded that it
be fixed in a hurry. It was.
The next day one of his
tires got punctured way out
in the country. He yanked
the tire off, inserted the
tube, fixed in a hurry,
pumped it up and was off.
He hadn't gone a mile
when the patch (put on in
a hurry) blew off. and he
had to run five miles on a
flat tire, ruining a casing,
tube and injuring a' rear
wheel. Aside from this he
lost two hours.
All because he was in a
hurry.
If it were only possible to
save all the time that is
wasted by things being
done in a hurry, how many
worth-while accomplish
ments would be ours.
The only way to save
time is to spend enough
time to do whatever you
wish to do RIGHT.

TAKING OFD. C. CENSUS
MAY END FEBRUARY 1
The fourteenth decennial census in
the District of Columbia will probably
not be completed until the end of Jan
uary, announced Supervisor Robert E.
Mattingly last, night.
Nearly 100 portfolios, however,
were turned in to the central census
office from the office of the District
supervisor yesterday and last night,
making- a total of more than 175 from
the District.
The supervisor said he hoped Btlll
that Washing-ton would be the first
city to turn in complete census re
turns, but that speed in finishing up
the job will not be permitted to inter
fere with accuracy and completeness.
New York and Chicago have already
turned in boxes of portfolios to the
central office.
Washington was the first city of
25,000 or more to turn in final census
.returns in the last two censuses. Vari
ous causes have contributed to slow
ing up this census in the District. Mr.
Mattingly said last night, chief of
which is failure to catch at their resi
dences the persons who are to be enu
merated. Even when individual cen
sus slips are left at homes they are
[not turned in for many days after
ward and in some cases not at all.
War workers, as explained by the
supervisor, have caused ^considerable I
slowing up in the taking of the cen
sus, but delay has not been laid at
the door of th?se alone. Other resi- j
dents of the District of Columbia
have been equally as negligent, Mr.
Mattingly said, and an equal share
of the responsibility rests with them.
Assistant Director Steuart an
nounced last night that the census in
New York had been slowed up by
inability of the enumerators to find
people living in hotels or apartment
houses at home. Nevertheless, he
said. New York city will send to the
central bureau at least 100 portfolios
per day. Less difficulty has been ex
perienced than was expected in the
foreign language sections of the me
tropolis, Mr. Steuart said, and these
districts have been very nearly com
pleted.
The director of the census last
night ruled that a former bartender who
insists that his occupation still is bar
tender should be classed as "without
occupation." If the figures in this
class come anywhere near those of
the census of 1910 there will be a
large number of people in the United
States out of work, for in 1910 there
were 169,449 persons in the United
States engaged in the selling of
spirituous liquors. This number was
augmented by 78,779 persons engaged
in the manufacture of such liquors.

DINNEWUSSED
Talented Artists Will Enter
tain at Affair to Be Held
February 3.
Plans for the dinner and cabaret
of the membership committee of the
Washington Board of Trade, to be
given at Wardman Park Hotel Feb
ruary 3. are sbeinK completed rapidly,
according to C. J. Waters, chairman
of the entertainment committee.
"An evening of clean, wholesome
entertainment is being planned," said
Mr. Waters. "The committee in
charge of arrangements is bringing
some of the best dancers from New
York for this affair, and we expect to
make it a great success."
The dinner is given to stimulate in
terest in the 2,500 membership mark
set by President Oyster. At present
the membership of the board totals
1,300, with an early prospect of reach
ing the figures set by its president.
Talented Artists Will Entertain.
Some of the features that have been
planned are as follows:
From the studio of Luigi Albertieri,
former maitre de ballet of the Metro
politan Opera Company of New York,
the following will appear: Miss Su
zana Legnani. solo, music from the
"Barcarole" from Offenbach's opera,
"Tales of Hoffman"; Miss Aline Mar
low and Natal Carosio. Hungarian
dance; Miss Nina Penn and Miss
Suzana Legnani, Norwegian dance;
Miss Marlow, "Les Patineurs," and
Miss Nina Penn and Natal Carosio,
"La Ciociara."
Some of the best talent from Wash
ington will also have a feature pl^ce
on the program. They are as follows:
Miss Camille Little, Scotch sword
dance; Miss Estelle Murray, Spanish
dance; Miss Bessie Burch Haycock,
classical song numbers.
Number Limited to 000.
As the accommodations for the din
ner are limited to 600, Mr. Waters
urges all members to be prompt in
sending in their applications for
places. It has been definitely stated
that after the 600 places have been
filled applications will be returned.
Members are privileged to bring with
them one or more guests, but early
reservations are requested, as more
than half the tickets have already
been sold.

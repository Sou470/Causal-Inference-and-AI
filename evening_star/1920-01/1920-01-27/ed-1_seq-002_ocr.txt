WATER IN GASOLINE
TANKS HALTS WORK
Post Office Trucks Are Affected by
Conditions Which Operators
Report to Officials.
Officials of the city post office were
mystified Sunday morning when re
ports came in from their automobile
truck operators that the Post Office
trucks would not run. It appeared
that they ran for a few minutes and
then stopped. The operators examin
ed them and found that there was
plenty of spark and that the carbu
retors were full of substance which
looked like gasoline, but which would
not vaporize enough to fire in the
cylinders.
On further inspection they found
this sjbstance to be water?pure wa
ter. Gasoline is lighter in specific
gravity than water, and when the
two are mixed the water goes to the
bottom.
In modern automobiles the gasoline
is fed to the engine through a vacuum
tank, which draws it from the main
tank. The water, being heavier than
gasoline, went to the bottom and
came to the engine first. Therefore
the engine would not start.
Ten trucks in the service of the
city post office were tied up. unable
to run. Sunday, and fifteen trucks
were tied up yesterday on account of
the defective gasoline. Post office
officials are making an investigation
of the source of their supply.

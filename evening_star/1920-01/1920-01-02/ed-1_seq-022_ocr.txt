CHRISTIANIA, Norway, December
30.?Reports received from Baltic
sources in the past few days and now
seemingly confirmed by private mes
sages indicate that the British govern
ment. operating through a syndicate
of British bankers. has completed
negotiations by which Britain assumes
control of the financing of Ksthonia
and Latvia. A banking syndicate ex
pects to place the currency of these
states upon the oound sterling basis,
to extend credit to private enterprises
building up public utilities, and
supply a certain amount of raw ma
terials in exchange for hemp, flax,
mineral ores, grain and manufactured
produrts which the two states are
capable of providing for export.
This reported undertaking by
Britain represents a master stroke
In her commercial diplomacy, which
not only gives her access to one of the
richest markets in the world, but
places her in an advantageous posi
tion to exploit all Russia when normal
conditions are restored in the por
tions now under red rule. Further, it
will strengthen Britain's hand when
the time comes for making agree
ments with the Russian governments
regarding the trade routes toward
India. Now if it were possible to have
the same agreements with the Baltic
states as were made with Syria it
would open a new line of communica
tion, making possible the direct trans
port of all products from the Baltic
states for the near east through
countries either financed by or under
British control.
Rail Communication Dlirnmrd.
There has been much discussion
lately regarding the immediate open
ing of communicating railways be
tween the Baltic states from Reval
to Ukrainia. If this is accomplished
British communication will be much
improved, as the proposed line will
be made with British capital, thus in
suring preferred terms for British
freight. Some Americans who have
watched the 'operation of the Ameri
I can relief corps in the Baltic states,
feeding hundreds of thousands _ of
women and children and giving
clothing and medical supplies, are
pointing out that treasure is accumu
lating by the direct taxation of
American citizens and are wondering
I what, if any. reward will he forth

1 actual payment for charitable acts.
but they might conceivably expect a

American* llnd Opportunity.
Some enterprising American manu
facturers might have followed up the
relief work and taken advantage of
the sentiment created in favor of the
Americans. British capitalists lost no
time in making use of the splendid
work by their army and navy in help
ing the Baltic states during the past
year.
They have closely watched events
and then with commercial enterprise
have grasped the earliest opportunity
of seeking trade agreements and un
dertaken projects which mean tre
mendous advantages in the future.
It is impossible to overestimate the

to Britain's government. Events in

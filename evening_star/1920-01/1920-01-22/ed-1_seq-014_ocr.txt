Hopes for Final Retain* Monday.
Hope was expressed by Mr. Mat
tingly today that the work of enumer
ation in the District of Columbia will
be completed by next Saturday night
and that final returns will be made
from the District by next Monday.
While he did not predict such a sched
ule would be followed. Mr. Mattingly
said if results are as good as those
obtained in the last few days he will
complete the census count, here by the
end of the week. In this event Wash
ington will undoubtedly be the first
city to make complete census returns,
for census officials eay no oth?r city
is as far along in the census work
as Washington.
Few Districts Remain.
There were nearly 250 complete
portfolios turned in to the central
office by enumerators here up to noon
today, leaving a few more than 120
to be completed. The uncompleted
districts are few in number, and in
them there are not many names left
to be enumerated, so it is expected
portfolios will be turned in at the
rate of about forty a day for the next
three days.
In many cases enumerators are
doubling up, supplementing ths work
of others, in order to expedite the
work and the twelve census Inspec
tors In the city are at the same time
going the rounds as enumerators in
the attempt to clean up those per

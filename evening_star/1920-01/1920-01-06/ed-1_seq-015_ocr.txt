PROTECT FORESTS,
DEMAND IK REPORT
Federal regulation to prevent the
devastation of privately owned forest
lands in the United States, now be
ing- carried on to a disastrous extent,
is proposed in a report made public
by the Society of American Foresters, of
which Frederick E. Olmsted is presi
dent.
The report is the work of a com
mittee for the application of forestry,
headed by Gifford Pinchot.
It is proposed by the committee that
a federal commission be created by
congressional legislation, consisting
of the Secretary of Agriculture, the
Secretary of Labor and the chairman
of the Federal Trade Commission, and
that this .commission be authorized
to establish regional administrative
organizations coincident with the
principal forest regions of the coun
try; to fix standards and promul
gate rules to prevent the devastation
and provide for the perpetutation of
forest growth and production of for
est crops on privately owned timber
lands, operated for commercial pur
poses.
The report says, however, that the
commission should not enforce its
rules and standards upon farm wood
lets or upon other areas which in its
judgment may be exempted with due
regard to the public interest.
The commission would be authoriz
ed also to control production when
ever such action is necessary for the I
public good or in times of economic
stress: to acquire for the United
States thp title or control of forest
lands, both timbered and cut over.
The proposed legislation also pro
vides for the establishment of ade
quate forest insurance agencies and
for the establishment of a national
forest loan board and forest loan
banks.
The enforcement of the law is to be
assured through provisions preventing
the cutting or removal of forest
products from commercial forest
lands contrary to the provisions of
the law. the standards and regula
tions; by requiring a federal license
to be obtained by concerns doing an
interstate business; by means of a
tax on incomes of those who cut or
remove forest products in violation
of the law.

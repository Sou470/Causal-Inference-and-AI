One Half-and-Half Issue.
The issues in respect to repeal of j
the half-and-half law are now (lis- j
tinctly outlined and will be fought i
t.. a finish on their merits on the j
floor of the House and of the Senate, j
The majority report of the House
District committee adopts as its j
standard of measuring comparative
tax burdens the tax rate modified
b\ the application to it of the re- j
ported relations of assessed to true
value. The tax rate and the full
valuation of taxable property are j
thus the two factors in this standard
oi measurement.
Washington replies that "full val
uation" docs not mean the same
thing in any two states of the Union,
or with few exceptions in the cities
and counties of the same state;
that laws requiring full valuation by
assessors, which prevail in most of
the states, are confessedly disre
garded in many of them and in prac
tice are, through widely varying in
terpretations. violated in nearly all
of them; and that the standard of
measurement which has as a vital
factor the false reports or the j
guesses concerning the "full valua
tion" of taxable property is a yard
stick of fluctuating length in the dif-
ferent states and is absolutely unre-
liable and fantastically misleading j
if used to measjire comparative tax
burdens.
Utilizing this delusive standard of
measurement, the majority report finds,
that Washington is taxed only about
one-half as much as comparable cities.
Viewing the dollars actually paid in
taxes as the real tax burden and com
paring the aggregate taxes of Wash
ington with those of other cities ap
proximating it in size, and comparing
per capitas in measuring the relative
tax burdens of cities much larger or
much smaller than Washington, we find
in all the facts made available by the
census conclusive proof of our conten
tion that Washington is now taxed as
heavily as equity demands in compari
son with other American cities.
Washington welcomes the opportu
nity to fight out this issue openly,
fully and to an equitable conclusion.
Checking Fire Dangers.
Accumulation of waste material at
the bottom of service shafts is be
lieved to have been the cause of two
fires in apartment houses in this city
within the last few days. A thorough
inspection of all such premises has
been ordered and will be continued
regularly as far as the available force
will permit. Fortunately, neither of
the two fires in which this case is
suspected resulted in heavy damage
or in loss of life, but tragic conse
quences might easily have come from
these blazes. A fire in a thickly popu
lated building is always to be dreaded
as potential of the gravest disaster.
It is evident that the ordinary janitor
service cannot be depended upon for
eleaning the places where waste can
gather and where fires may start. If
the District must undertake this
house to house visitation in the in
terest of public security it should have
a special force for that purpose larger
than at present. It is now proposed
to take members of fire companies
from their regular duties for this
work, an expedient that should be
rendered unnecessary by larger pro
vision for the fire marshals patrol.

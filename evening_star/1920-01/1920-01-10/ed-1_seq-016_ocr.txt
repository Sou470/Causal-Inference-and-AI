Fifteen District* "Cheeked Ip."
About fifteen census districts in
the District of Columbia have been
completed and checked up. and the
rest are well on the load to comple
tion. Robert E. Mattingly, the super
visor. announced today. "We expect
practically all of the census districts
to be completed by Wednesday of next
week. Checking up will probably
take two days and then we will be
ready to make our final return. We
hope to be the first city to make final
census returns^in order that the peo
ple of the District of Columbia may
know the exact population of the
District as soon as possible."
Several census enumerators have
reported that they have had to leave
individual census slips for deaf mutes
to fill in and return to the census
bureau, thus saving the time it would
take to write the answers out while
the census enumerator is present.
"These people are all highly intelli
gent and willing to help us." an
enumerator said today, "but it would
take a long time for them to write
out the answers to the questions we
ask. so we leave them individual slips
to fill out and mail in to the central
office."

In telling of the varied experiences

District Mr. Mattingly said today that
frequently the enumerators have been
asked to stay to dinner in the even

with a less cordial reception. "Al
most universally our enumerators
have met with the best possible re
sponse." Mr. Mattingly said, "but
sometimes they are forced to show
their authority for taking the census.
We have not yet had any unpleasant

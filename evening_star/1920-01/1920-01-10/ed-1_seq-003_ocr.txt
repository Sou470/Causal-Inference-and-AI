Present Statute.
Miss Jessie Haver of the Consum
ers' League urged the Mothers' Club
of the Dent School, at a meeting Thurs
day afternoon. to support impor
tant amendments to the present Dis
trict child-labor law. Mrs. P.
Alonaco. president of the club, spoke
briefly on the splendid spirit of co-
operation between parents and teach
ers of children in the school.
"Under the present statute," said
Miss Haver, "any child can leave
school at the age of fourteen years, no
matter what grade he has reached,
and no matter whether he is going to
work or stay at home. The only edu
cational requirement for obtaining
an employment certificate is ability to
read and write legibly simple sen
tences in English.
"Many children are allowed to leave
school at the age of twelve years.
They may leave school at any age to
work for the Senate. There is no com
plete school census or records of all
children of school age in the District.
Many children are 'lost,' and not
reached by, attendance officers. Any
child may go to work at the age of
sixteen years, no matter how little
schooling he has acquired. Only four
states besides the Distric' allow the
employment of children under four
teen years on proof of poverty."

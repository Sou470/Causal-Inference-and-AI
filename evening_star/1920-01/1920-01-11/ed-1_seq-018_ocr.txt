IN COLORADO NATIONAL PARK
Or. J. Walter Fewkes, chief of the
bureau of American ethnology, has
recently resumed his field work on
the Mesa Verde National Park, Colo
rado.
This park is the only one of its char
acter on this continent that la re
served by the United States govern
ment for the protection of the aborig
inal buildings, and in recent years
the Depa rtment of the Interior and
the Smithsonian Institution have co

be preserved for generations yet un
born. after having been preserved to

The field work of last summer was
devoted to a cliff dwelling, called a
square tower house, from a high tow
er Situated midway in its length.
This to is forty feet high and is
the highest building constructed of
masonry by Indians north of Mexico
long before the white man reached
that section of the southwest. It
adds to this unique feature the best
known specimen of prehistoric ma
aonry, shown in the construction of
the roofs of two circular rooms. The
original rafters are still in place,
showing the marks of stone Imple
ments used by the builders.
The whole vein, which measures
lit feet in length, is very conspicu
ous and has already become one of
the greatest attractions of the park.
One of the most unexpected results
of Dr. Fewkes' summer work was the
discovery of many inconspicuous
buildings among the cedars on the
top of the plateau. The evidenee of these
buildings before excavating were
very obscure, but they are so nu
merous in certain areas that there is
scarcely a square quarter mile in
which one of them does not appear.
One of these small buildings when

PLANS TO TRAIN YOUTHS
FOR BIG NAVAL RESERVE
Secretary Daniels Will Lay Pro
posal for Summer Courses Be
fore Congress.
Plans for building- up a great naval
reserve force by the establishment of
summer training courses for high
school and college youths at the naval
training- stations will be laid before
Congress by Secretary Daniels in the
near future.
Announcing the plan. Secretary
Daniels said it was proposed to put
it into effect next summer. The
Great Lakes, Hampton roads and Pa
cific coast training stations would be
used.
Transportation and subsistence would
be provided for those who take the
training, the Secretary said, and the
tentative plan contemplates short
cruises on naval training ships as part
of the course, which probably will be
fixed at two months.
Those completing the training would
become members of the naval reserve
force, unless desiring to enter the
active naval service. Mr. Daniels said
he would like to see a class of 10,000
placed in training at the Great Lakes
station alone next summer.

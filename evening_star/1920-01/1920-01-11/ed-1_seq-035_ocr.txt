ENICE. December 26 (Correspond- I
ence of the Associated Press).?The
four bronze horses which adorned the
facade of St. Mark's Cathedra! ha^e
again been put in place after an ab- j
sence of more than two years due to
their transfer to Rome, where they
had been kept to avoid capture by
the Austrians or destruction by air
bombs.
The horses are regarded as the
finest specimens of ancient bronzes 1
and. according to tradition, graced the
triumphal arch of Nero. In the reign
of Constantine they were taken by j
that emperor to Constantinople. There
they stood until 1204, w-hen they
were brought to Venice and placed on
St. Mark's Cathedral. There the horses
remained for almost six centuries.
Napoleon, after he had conquered
Venice, took them to Paris to adorn
the arch of triumph in the Place du
Carrousel. After his defeat at Water
loo they were returned to their ori
ginal place in St. Mark's, where they
remained undisturbed for a century.
The horses stand five feet in height
and are the only ones in existence j
representing an ancient chariot quad
riga.

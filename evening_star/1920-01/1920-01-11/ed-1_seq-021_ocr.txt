Commissioner Roper Sends Letter
in Response to Requests From
Organizations.
Suggestions for organizations that
hope ot aid in the enforcement of
national prohibition are containied in
a letter sent out by Commissioner
Daniel C. Roper of the bureau of in
ternal revenue "in response to numer
ous requests."
Four major suggestions are made
by the commissioner as follows:
Organize and work for the develop
ment of a strong, healthy public sen
timent in favor of obedience to law
in general and the prohibition law
in particular.
Support of the local officers in the
performance of their duties under the
law and vigorously condemn those
who fail to perform their duty.
In states and municipalities where
local laws do not permit co-operation
between local and federal officers, work
to have the .statutes changed so that
such co-operation may be required.
Keep in close touch with the federal
prohibition director of the state, ad
vising him of enforcement results and
helping him correct defective adminis
tration.
"Be assured that the bureau of
internal revenue" welcomes construc
tive criticism made directly to respon
sible officers." says Mr. Roper in con
clusion, "and earnestly solicits the
co-operation of every organized moral
and civic agency in awakening the
public conscience to the great im
portance of this enforcement work."

educated at the College Sainte-Barbe

city, where he was enrolled as a

EX-SOLDIERS SEEK JOBS.
K. of C. Striving to Locate Places.
Waiting List Is Long.
An acute stage of unemployment has
been reached among the ex-service men
in Washington during the past week and
about 2,000 more are expected to be job
less within the next two weekB, owing
to discharge from temporary positions in
i the government, according to word re
ceived from the directors of the Knights
of Columbus employment bureau for for
mer soldiers, sailors and marines, lo
cated in Victory Cottage, 7th street and
Pennsylvania avenue northwest.
The sudden Increase in unemployment
is believed due to the discharge of many
workers who had found temporary work
during the Christmas holiday rush. Now
that the season is over they are no
longer needed. An appeal has been
made to the employers of the District to
aid these veterans through volunteering
positions and to register their needs
with the Knights of Columbus. It is
guaranteed that capable workers will be
supplied them. An average of eighty
men have been placed in well paying
positions for which they were fitted by
the K. of C. daily since Christmas day.
A waiting list of several hundred has
been maintained. The majority of the
men listed now are laborers, who are
looking for hard manual work as a pref
erence. No opening will be found for
them until building starts, unless posi
tions are listed requiring men of their
caliber.

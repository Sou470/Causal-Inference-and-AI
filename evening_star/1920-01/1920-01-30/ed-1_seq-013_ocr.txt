"SIX-BY-SIX MIRROR" AID
TO TRAFFIC POLICEMEN
Attached to "Go-Go" Sign
Stanchion Gives Safety Guardian
View of Vehicles Behind Him.
Washington has another innovation
in the matter of safeguarding street
traflic. It is an ordinary 6 by 6 mir
ror attached to a "go-go" sign stan
chion, which enables the traffic po
liceman to see vehicles in two direc
tions at a time, and the first one of
these mirrors to be given rial is
being used at Pennsylvania^avenue
and 14?h street.
Maj. Pullman, superintendent of po
lice. has always impressed upon
traffic policemen the necessity for not
giving Bignals unless they were cer
tain of the location of vehicles about
them, and at a recent conference with
Capt. Albert J. Headley, head of the
traffic squad, it was decided to try
the mirror.
The mirror in use at Pennsylvania
avenue and 14th street enables the
traffic policeman to have full view of
vehicles approaching from two di
rections, and, Maj. Pullman stated,
should make it easy to signal those
desiring to make a left-hand turn
without the possibility of a mix-up
between vehicles.
With the mirror in operation only
a short time, it is stated, its value
has been fully demonstrated, and they
will be put in operation at every,
point where a "go-go" sign is ueed.

BRANCH OF W. C. T. U.
Bev. Earle Wilfley Lauds Achieve
ments and TTrgfes Members to
Continue Working.
At a meeting of the Northwest
Union of the W. C. T. U. held at
Vermont Avenue Christian Church
yesterday afternoon Rev. Earle Wil
fley spoke on the pioneer work of
the white ribboners and eulogixed
their achievements in the abolition
of the saloons. He urged them to
continue to work ancl pray for the
enforcement of the law, as that was
going to prove a long, hard fleht.
He also spoke on the desecration of
the Sabbath in the capital, and said
no other city in the United States
is entirely without some laws of re
striction. He requested the earnest
co-operation of the Woman's Chris
tion Temperance Union in bringing
about a more holy Sabbatn.
Mrs. Sara Smith, who had served
as treasurer for the past twenty
five years, tendered her resignation,
to the regret of all members present.
Mrs. S. E. Henry consented to fill her
place. Mrs. MacSpeiden, chairman of
music, gave a solo, "Lead Me All the
Way."
Mrs. M. C. Hall, president, brought
the meeting to a close with a talk
on pioneer work and'the victory thus
far won.

The following communication from Stanley W. Wisnioski is self
explanatory
"Sporting Editor, Star:
"I have read your article concerning the Stecher-Caddock 'cham
pionship match in The Star. and. being a close follower of sport in
general and especially interested in wrestling, I wish to inform you
that I read this article with great interest.
"In substance. 1 agree with your views and opinions. Knowledge
of the subject matter is ouite evident and I am delighted to note that
you, too, are disposed to criticise the unscrupulous tactics of the faking
performers and of the 'get-rich-quick promoters.'
"However, I disagree with you on the point that the winner of
this match will be acknowledged a 'worthy successor to the late Frank
Gotch.' No doubt you have heard of Stanislaus Zbyszko, if not, you
can, by consulting the records, ascertain that he defeated every man
but Gotch. He defeated Hackenshmidt, Lurich. Mahmout, Cutler,
Americus, Lewis, etc. The older Zbyszko wrestled Gotch and was de
feated ia six.aeconds, through th? medium of a very neatly executed,
but very unsportsmanlike trick, and not by superior skill or strength.
I shall not relate the accounts or delve into the merits of that trick, as
I trust you are acquainted with same. After that meeting Gotch was
offered a return match with the older Zbyszko and for a consideration
of $35,000. but Gotch refused to listen to any such a thing as a match
with Zbyszko.
"Being unable to force Gotch to meet him, and having defeated all
the best wrestlers, Stanislaus Zbyszko went to Europe at the outset of
the world war, but has been heard from lately. He is scheduled to
arrive in this country within the next three weeks. In my opinion, he
is the best wrestler in the world today, much better than his younger
brother Wladek, and. therefore, to acclaim the winner of the Stecher
Caddock match the world champion is entirely too premature. The
writer has seen all of the above-mentioned wrestlers in action, and
from good authority learns that Lewis, Stecher and Caddock are all
members of Jack Curley's 'stable.'
"Wrestling is once again flourishing, and has taken a good hold
on the public. The performers and the promoters realize that the
public will not stand for any fakes. The sporting editors, through
the medium of their columns, also must be fair with the public, and
not furnish news of fake 'championship matches.'"

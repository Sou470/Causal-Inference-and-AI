WILL HELP FIND HOMES
FOR SHIPPING EMPLOYES
All the resources of the housing
bureau of the Y. M. C. A in Washington
have been placed at the disposal of the
United States Shipping Board, which is
endeavoring to find living quarters for
1,700 employes of the board and their
families, shortly to be brought to this
city from Philadelphia.
E. M. Davis, in charge of the asso
ciation's housing activities, has turned
over to the board a list of 195 houses
where thera are vacancies, and will con
tinue to supply that office with lists of
furnished rooms, vacant apartments and
residences as rapidly as such informa
tion comes to hand.
?There are many desirable places for
rent in Mount Pleasant, North Capitol
street and Georgetown," said Mr. Davis,
"but the majority of applicants at pres
ent want to settle in the heart of the
District, where conditions are already
congested and rents are high. The aver
age single room brings from $20 to $35
per month, although there are a few
small hallrooms at $15. One of the
difficulties that we are forced to con
tend with is the forgetfulness of the
owner or landlord of a house to notify
us when a vacancy no longer exists."

BAPTIST EDUCATIONAL
PLAN IN CHINA IS HUGE
Five New High Schools for Girls
Is Part of Extensive
Campaign.
Furthering- a general plan of educa
tion with particular emphasis on aca
demic training for women, the North
ern Baptist convention has planned
to establissh in China at least five new
high schools for girls and provide thein
with adequate buildings and with
equipment necessary to meet the de
mands of modern education.
According to the program mapped
out. the denomination will not only
establish new high schools for girls,
but will Increase the number of pri
mary schools" until there is at least
one in every village where there is
a church or an established preaching
station.
Complete Educational Coarse.
Ultimately it is proposed to build
up a system of schools in each of the
three mission fields so co-ordinated
and graded that boys and girl? from
Christian homes, beginning at the pri
mary grades, and on through the uni
versity course, may secure a complete
education and training, conducted un
der Christian auspices.
Plans have been approved for send
ing out trained instructors whose ex
perience in American colleges has fit
ted them for the g.gantic task of
standardizing and correlating the mis
sions' educational system in China.
Teachers qualified to serve as super
visors are particularly needed, it is
said.
Northern Baptists Co-Operating.
The Northern Baptists are co-oper
ating with other denominations in the
development of institutions of higher
education, such as colleges, univer
sities and medical schools.
The Baptist schools in China num
ber 265 with an enrollment of 8,455
pupils. The government is gradually
raising its standards of education as
its system develops and the mission
schools are assuming increasing re
sponsibilities.

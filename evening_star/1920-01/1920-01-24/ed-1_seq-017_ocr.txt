Quickly following the advocacy by

I Isaac Bacharach of New Jersey, a re

I bill along this line, on which he has
I been working for several months.
I Representative Bacharach feels that
his bill will:
First?Cut the cost of living.
Second?Increase the revenues at a
time when they are desperately need
ed.
Third?Do away with objectionable
forms of taxation.
Declared Aid to Poor People.
It is declared an aid to poor people
because it would not tax anything
under 50 cents. Beyond that the tax
is one cent on each dollar's worth of
goods purchased for consumption.
Anything above $500 would be t&xed
5 per cent, including player-pianos,
automobiles, yachts, jewelry, art

estate??one half of 1 per cent. He
proposes to make this levy on gross
sales above $500. which, he believes,
will prevent "washing," from which
the government has been getting no i
return.
There would be a tax of 1 per
cent on all raw materials from mines,
woodlands, waters, ctc.
Mr. Bacharach estimates that this
measure will raise approximately
$:i 000,000.000, which, he calculates, is
three-fifths of the total cost of run
ning the government during the next

It is proposed in this measure to
repeal the soda water and ice cream
tax and the excess profits tax. It
would repeal also the stamp tax on
all documents.
Repeals Lnwr Taxes.
It is declared It will prove popular
because it repeals the so-called "lux
ury taxes," which the House has al
ready voted to repeal in -a bill now
before the Senate finance committee.
Members of the ways and means
committee, including Representative
Joseph W. Fordney, the chairman,
have approved the action of Repre
sentative Bacharach in Introducing
this bill as a working basis for revision
of irksome taxes. It is generally con
ceded by all members of the commit
tee that some new form of taxation
must be devised, and this bill, they
think, accomplishes two good pur
poses by repealing objectionable
taxes and substituting another system

Representative Bacharach expects

Question of Bank Coercion.
The Senate, through a resolution
has called upon the Federal Reserve
Board to inform it whether the board
or any federal reserve bank has re
sorted to any method of coercion to
compel state banks to join the federal
reserve system. Senator Norris of Ne
braska was the author of the resolu
tion.
Charges that efforts have been made
by the federal reserve banks to force
the state banks into the reserve sys
tem have reached Senator Norris in
considerable number and also mem
bers of the House.
Senator Norris said he did not care
to discuss these charges until he had
1 heard from the reserve board. It is
understood, however, that the federal
reserve banks have sought to make it
so inconvenient for state banks out
side the reserve system to do business
that they would be compelled to join.

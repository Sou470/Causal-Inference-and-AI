Enumerators Meeting With

I Washington is co-operating heartily
and with good will in the immense
ta.sk of taking the 1920 decennial
census in the National Capital.
Everywhere census enumerators
have pone during the past few days
they have met with the best of co
operation, and have in many cases
found the answers to their questions
already written out and awaiting
them, according to statements made
by several of the enumerators when
they reported last night to Director
Kobert E. Mattingly. Nowhere in the
District of Columbia have any of the
367 enumerators met with rebuffs or
any unkind act, Mr. Mattingly said
last night, and to date the census
taking in Washington has run o(T
very smoothly.
The enumerators recounted last
night on finishing their work in
Washington many of the usual funny
census stories concerning the answers
put to their questions. Mr. Mattingly
reported that one woman was so
solicitous regarding correct enumera
tion of herself and family that she
telephoned in her new place of ad
dress after having been enumerated
Friday. It turned out that she had
moved Friday afternoon, after she
had been enumerated in her former
home.
Some Annwrr* lJiflldent.
Several enumerators reported diffi
dent answers to several of the ques
tions Some of them said colored per
sons who were nearly white had re
ported "white" when asked their color
and race. In this case the enumera
tor is authorized to use his discre
tion. but must report such cases to
the director. Others founTl difficulty
in making themselves understood
where they enumerated persons of
foreign birth who are unfamiliar
with the English language. Al
though anxious to help the enumera
tors and willing to answer questions,
these persons in some cases wished
to know what it was all about.
"I almost had to draw a blue print
of the whole census for one Chinese j
whom I enumerated," one enumerator j
said, recounting an experience with j
an oriental laundryman. Another said
site had found women who did not
know their correct age, but who, on i
being questioned closely and told the
purpose of the census, found their
senses and gave correct answers. No
cases were recorded yesterday or Fri
day of reluctance to answer the ques
tions asked, and in no case was re
course had to the warning authorized
by the census bureau that a maximum
fine of 5100 may be levied on persons
who refuse to give answers to the
questions put by enumerators. There
is also a fine, following conviction,
for giving incorrect answers.
Enumerator a Work at Mich*.
Other enumerators pointed out the
factor of loss of time while persons
being enumerated are counting up
their ages and in getting the exact
age of all children in a household,
which they are required to get. One
enumerator reported last night that
on interrogating the head of a house
hold she learned that the daughter
had been married Friday. This young
woman was reported as single, as
census regulations require that per
sons single on January 1 shall be so
recorded for census purposes.
Scores of the enumerators who are
working on a piecework basis'?that
is, 4 cents for each name enumerated
?continued their work last night.
They were so authorized by Director
Mattingly, who recognized that in
many cases there is a better chance
to secure full information in the
evening, and particularly on a. cold
evening. Enumerators have been di
rected by Mr. Mattingly to hurry
their work as much as possible, in
order that the District mav he one of

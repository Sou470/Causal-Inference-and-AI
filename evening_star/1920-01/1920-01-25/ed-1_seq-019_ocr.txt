Renewed effort to have the Chesa
peake and Potomac Telephone Com
pany restore its former practice of
allowing 600 calls a year on limited
phones irrespective of the number
used in any one month will be made
this week by William McK. Clayton,
representing the Federation of Citi
zens' Associations.
Mr. Clayton is arranging for a con
ference with officials of the com
pany, at which he will point out that
in the interest of good will between
the public and the corporation the
too calls a "year rule should be re
stored.
The rule prevailed before the tele
phone company was taken over by
the Post Office- Department during the
war. It is now claimed that the com-,
pany has built up an added source
of revenue by charging five cents a
call for all calls used in excess of
fifty a month, and not giving credit
for calls falling below fifty a month.
The telephone company claims that
it would be put to considerable ex
pense to install a bookkeeping sys
tem that would keep a record of the
calls per month and give credit in
accordance with the citizens' desire.
As the question is a rate question
there is no likelihood of the utilities
commission taking it up as a special
issue, inasmuch as the commission
has continued the existing telephone
rates for a period of six months.
Mr. Clayton believes that the mat
ter can be adjusted satisfactorily
through conferences of representa
tives of the citizens' bodies and offi
cials of the company, and will insti
tute efforts to that end this week.

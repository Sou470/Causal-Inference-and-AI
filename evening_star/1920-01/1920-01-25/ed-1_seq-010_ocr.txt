DECLINES APPOINTMENT
OF BRIGADIER GENERAL
Gen. Henry P. McOain's Emer
gency Commission as Major Gen
eral Expires June 30.
Gen. Henry P. McCain, commanding
Camp Devens, Majss.. has declined the
appointment of brigadier general in
the Regular Army. He now holds the
"emergency" rank of major general,
and his commission in that grade will
terminate June 30 next, if not before,
and under ordinary conditions lie
would revert to his former grade of
colonel in the adjutant general's de
partment.
In the early part of the world war
Gen. McCain held the office of adju
tant general of the Army, with the
rank of major general. He was re
lieved of that duty by Gen. Harris,
the incumbent, and assigned to the
command of Camp Devens. with the
temporary rank of major general. He
is eligible for retirement as a major
general, by virtue of his former serv
ice as adjutant general, and it is pre
dicted that he will apply for retire
ment, unless favorable action is taken
on a bill pending in Congress to ad
vance him to the grade of major gen
eral in the Regular Army on the ac
tive list.
Gen. McCain is from Mississippi
and was graduated from the Military
Academy in 1885. After fifteen years'
service in the infantry arm he was
transferred to the adjutant general's
department in 1900 and was attached
to that department until 1918. when
he was sent to the Massachusetts
camp.

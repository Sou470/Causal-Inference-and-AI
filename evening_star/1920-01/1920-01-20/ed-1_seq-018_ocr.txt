A society of engineers and agricul
turists was organized last night at
the Y. M. H. A. headquarters which is
to be a chapter of the Zionist Society
of Engineers and Agriculturists. B.
H. Hirschenson was elected chairman
and M. Perkiss, secretary-treasurer.
The main object of the organization
will be to study the ways and means
of developing the arid regions of
Palestine. Other engineering prob
lems of that country will also be i
studied, such as harbor improvements
and the development of the chemical
resources of the Dead sea. Emile I
Berliner, president of the Washington
Zionist District, gave a lecture on
this subject which he illustrated with
a topographical map of Palestine.
One project he discussed was the con
struction of a canal from the Mediter
ranean sea to the River Jordan and i
the erection of a water power plant at
its base.
The work of the society will be
planned by the problems committee,
consisting of L A. I'inck, E. Elvov
and David A. Glushak. A meeting of
this committee will bo arranged be
fore the next regular monthly meet
ing of the society, the first Tuesday
in the month, to prepare a program
to be submitted.

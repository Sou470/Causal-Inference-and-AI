districts which are not yet finished
are the ones in which trouble has
been encountered by enumeraiors
since the beginning of census taking
January 2. Considerable work will be
required before they are fully cleaned
up. In an effort to facilitate the work
of enumeration here more than half
of the enumerators are working at
night, believing they will find more
people at their homes at night than
during the day.
Majority Enumerated.
The great majority of persons in the
District have been enumerated, Mr. Mat
tingly said, but final completion of the
work here is delayed, awaiting enum
eration of the relatively few persons who
have not been seen or who do not want
to bo seen by the enumerators. Mr. Mat
tingly intimated strongly today that
there are many persons who do not want
to be counted here, as they appear to
make consistent efforts to be out of the
house when the enumerators call.
These persons are the ones who are
holding up the census here and may
hold it up for a week or ten days. The
supervisor believes that all the rjcal
I citrant districts cannot be Anally
cleaned up within a few days, but that
in all probability the work will not be
finished until the end of February.
There are some persons employed in the
government departments, he said, who
do not want to be enumerated In Wash
ington, and take care that they are not
at home when the enumerator calls.

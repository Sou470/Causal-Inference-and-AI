BANISH DISCONTENT
Money Is at Par, Price of Living
Has Been Kept Down, Normal
Production Is Found.
By WILLIAM K. NASH.
(Cablegram to The Star and rhirago

PARIS. January 21.?It is rare to
find in eastern Europe a country
which is not starving or discontented
or in a state of revolution, yet, ac
cording to a member of Premier
Venizelos' civil cabinet, Greece is not
in that condition. Her* money is
worth par, the price of living is not
exorbitant, production is normal and
the people are satisfied with their
lot. American manufacturers abound
in Athens.
Indeed, the country s so prosperous
that public works are being planned
in the harbor of Piraeus which will
cost about lUO.QOO.OOO drachmas
(S20.000.000). A radical land bill is
before parliament to expropriate
three-flfths of the great estates of
the landlords and divide the prop
erty among the peasants.
This state of affairs is due to several
causes. One is that the Greek carry
ing trade in the eastern Mediterra
nean is rapidly regaining the position
it held before the war. even though 60
per cent of its available vessels were
sunk by mines and torpedoes. Since
the armistice 150,000 tons of shipping
have been rebuilt. In the second place
the Greek exports are large. In the
third place the agricultural regions of
Thrace and Asia Minor, which Greece
has occupied by virtue of the Ver
sailles treaty, provide welcome sup
plies of food for the home market and
keep down the cost of living every
where except in Athens. In the fourth
place the confidence of the people in
the Venizelos government is so great
that, according to my friend, delega
tions come from the farthest corners
of Asia Minor just to kiss the pre
mier's hand.

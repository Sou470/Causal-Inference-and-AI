the diver at the Key bridge is no

William C. Zess, and Mr. Zess ought to
know, for he is the diver.
"All diving on the Potomac river is
done in total darkness," said Mr. Zess
today, "the diver being guided wholly
by the sense of touch."
The diver on the KeV bridge opera
tions Mr. Zess stated, receives $t! per
day for days when he does no diving
and $15 per day for days when he
works under water.
"Other divers who have helped me
on this work received $30 per day,
furnishing equipment* and one man."
continued the diver. "The War De
partment mal*s a royalty of $10 per
day on the diving apparatus fur
nished on the Key bridge work."
Mr. Zess believes that it is impos

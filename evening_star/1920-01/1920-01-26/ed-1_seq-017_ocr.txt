EVERY first mortgage bond issue which we purchase and
offer under the Straus Plan must be protected by an
ample margin of safety between the amount of the loan
and a conservative appraisal of the mortgaged property..
But this alone is not enough. Every bond issue also is sufficiently
amortized?that is. a certain portion of the bonds come due and
arc paid off each year so that the margin of safety is constantly
growing. For example?
In the typical issue shown by the above graph the origi
nal loan of $600,000 cn a property valued at $1,000,000
is reduced by 5% after the first year, by 10% after the
second year, etc., thus continually increasing the equity
and much more than providing against depreciation.
This House was a pioneer in this country in adopting the principle
and perfecting the practice of amortization. This is now gen
erally recognized as a vitally necessary safeguard in real estate
loans. Campaigns for its widespread adoption are being waged
by the United States Department of of Labor and by the savings
bank section of the American Bankers Association.

RENT COMMISSIONERS
ARE NOT YET LOCATED
The District rent commission was still
without permanent quarters today, its
business being carried on in the office
of the chairman, Capt. James F. Oyster,
at 900 Pennsylvania avenue northwest.
Capt. Oyster stated today that while
the commission is in receipt of a num
ber of complaints daily, involving rent
disputes, it is by no means overwhelm
ed. Complaints are being filed as they
are received, and as soon as quarters
and an office force are provided regular
hearings will be begun for the settle
ment of disputes between tenants and
landlords.

EIGHT KILLED IN CRASH.
Two Train Sections Collide in Can
ada When Warning Fails.
MONTREAL. January 26 Two sec
tions of a transcontinental train at
Corbeil. Ont.. yesterday collided and
eight or more persons were killed.
The collision was due to the fail
ure of trainmen to hear warning
torpedoes explode, according to re
ports reaching the Canadian Pacific
railway offices today. The first sec
tion was stalled owing to difficulty
in getting up steam in the extreme
cold, and the crew placed torpedoes
on the track as a warning to the
second section. The crew of the
second section said the explosions
were not loud enough to be heard
above the roar of the train.

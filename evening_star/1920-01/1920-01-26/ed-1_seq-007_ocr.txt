BY Cffl SYSTEM;
Method Adopted Whereby
Federal Employes Are Group
ed According to Salaries.
Solitaire, with government clerks as
the cards, constitutes the latest recre
ation of the joint congressional com
mission on reclassification of salaries
in the District.
On six tables set end to end members
of the classification staff of the com
mission today busied themselves with j
placing cards representing various I
classes in the clerical services, in j
order to arrive at a fair evaluation of
the classes of positions in each service.
Kight strings were stretched from
end to end of the six tables, dividing
the tables horizontally into eight sec
tions, corresponding with the eight
ranks of classes, from the underclerk
to the highest supervisory positions.
The lowest rank represented the
lowest paid employes, the second
higher rank the next grade, and so on
through the eight grades to the top,
in which those employes in supervis
ory positions were placed.
No \nmen Attached.
No names were attached to the
cards, the cards in each case repre
senting the claf;s specifications of the
job. showing the title, duties, quali
fications and lines of promotion. It
was significant that few employes
were placed in the lowest row.
The "cards" were shuffled into their
various positions in the horizontal
rows between the stripes, and into
the vertical rows according to which
one of the various clerical services
they represented.
No salaries were attached to the
cards, the classifiers having been or
dered by the reclassification commis
sioners to go merely on the assump
tion that the lowest row would rep
resent the lowest salary in the cleri
cal service, and so on.
The bulk of the federal employes
seemed to fall into the fourth row
from the bottom, these positions
mostly being "senior clerk." It is
believed that the great number of
clerks reach or should reach this po
sition and that here many of them
may stay for life.
Senior Clerk. Hating.
The senior clerk is usually, it is de
clared. a first rate man or woman in
every respect, knowing the job thor
oughly and being' able to fill it with
only a minimum of supervisory di
rection. These clerks constitute one
of the great working forces of the
federal government here, and upon
them much of the work of the gov
ernment rests, especially in its rou
tine aspect.
It is generally hoped that under re
classification an even greater number
of clerks will he enabled to reach
jthis rank, and that the maximum and
I minimum salary which attach to it i
jwill represent salaries upon which a
[man with a family may live comfort-)
I ably.
Scores of clerks, it is pointed out, I
must necessarily remain in the grade
I of senior clerk for life, since all em- j
I ployes cannot reach the limited few
j first supervisory positions.
Hope 1'nder Keelnsnifieatlon.

that the opportunity will be there for
lall to do so who have the ability, but
lit is realized that human nature will
not change, under reclassification or
anything else, and that there always
I will be many who are entirely high
I class men and women who must stay
in this rank.

WOOD CALLS U. S. NAVY
"FLOATING DEATH TRAP"
Tells American Legion Members
Army Must Be Big Enough
to Police Nation.
BOSTON, January 19.?Maj. Gen.
I^eonard Wood, speaking to members of
the American Legion, yesterday called
for a large navy and asserted that "all
we have now is a floating death trap."
"We want," he said, "a first-class
navy, and we want it always ready. We
cannot make it in a hurry. We cannot
have it with a divided fleet. We can
not fill ships with untrained men and
say we have a navy. It is not a navy
at all. it is a floating death trap.
"We want a large navy," he added,
"and an army large enough to police
the nation in times of peace. We need
a good navy, highly trained men, and
good ships." He also advocated a
large merchant marine.
Gen. Wood urged the adoption of a
"law and order slogan." asserting that
"it's no use talking politics these days.
There will be no politics, no confidence,
no business opportunities, no successful
or happy labor unless you have law and
order and respect for the rights of prop
erty and the rights of others."
"We are going to govern this country
by Americans," he continued. "When I
say Americans I do not mean neces
sarily people whose families have been
here many generations, but real Amer
icans, the people who have adopted and
are trying to live up to our standards
and ideals."

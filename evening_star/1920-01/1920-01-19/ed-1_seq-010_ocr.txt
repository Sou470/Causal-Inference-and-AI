DEATH BENEFIT REVIVED.
Six Months' Pay to Army Men's De
pendents Effective Again.
The Army death benefit of six
months' pay for officers and enlisted
men is again in full force and effect
by direction of the Secretary of War.
This gratuity, revived by the act of
Congress of December 17, 1919, exist
ed prior to the world war, but was
rescinded in January, 1918, on account
of the war risk insurance benefits.
According to present regulations,
when an officer or enlisted man on the
active list of the Regular Army dies
from wounds or disease not the result
of his own misconduct, his widow, or,
if there be no widow, his child or
children, or if there be no widow or
child, any other dependent, will be
paid an amount equal, to six months'
pay at the rate received by such offi
cer or enlisted man at the date of his
death.

U. S. ARMY BUILDINGS
FLOODED IN GERMANY
COBLENZ, Friday, January 16 (by
the Associated Press).?Flood waters in
the Rhine and Moselle rivers here have
reached the highest stage in 136 years,
according to official German records.
The lower parts of Coblenz along the
Moselle and Rhine are under water and
many houses occupied by Americans
have their basements flooded, but loss
of American property has been small,
owing to warnings issued two weeks
ago, when the rivers began to rise.

industry.

and terminals.

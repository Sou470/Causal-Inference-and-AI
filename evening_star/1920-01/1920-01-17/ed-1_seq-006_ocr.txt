"Sharity" vs. Charity
What Is Money?
MONEY is the pawns with which the game of the
world is played. To some it is a blessing, to
some a curse, and to all a necessity. But after all
money is the most transient possession of man.
As he plays his part in Life he is constantly changing his
pawns for the things he needs or wants. The money
that has slaved for him today is the servant of someone
else tomorrow. But the money that passes through his
hands can be made to leave an imprint for good or evil
on his character.
YOU can see men all about
you who are pinched in
body and soul through
their devotion to money, but
you never saw a man who was
not made bigger and better
and stronger and happier by
the character development
that comes from sensible giv
ing to those who have been
less fortunate in life.
To give a beggar on the
street a nickel is not always
charity?it may be only the
same kind of false pride that
makes some men attend
funerals and march in pa
rades.
Real character building
charity is a far different
thing. It means providing the
things within the limit of your
means that some other fel
low, man or woman, lacks
and is unable to secure. It
may mean giving a meal, or
getting a job. More real
charity has passed in a kindly
word in time of trouble than
money benefactions.
Whenever you do a kindly act
you profit far more than the
one you aid, for you have
broadened your vision,
strengthened your character,
and added a sounder base to
the fabric of your own suc
cess.
Charity has an unpleasant
sound. Why not call it
"SHARITY"?sharing with
others from your own sur
plus? For, after all you can
not take anything away with
you.

Dr. Meeker Differs From
Hopeful View of William

Commodity prices may be expected
j to remain at present levels for an
j indefinite length of time, according
to Royal C. Meeker, commissioner of
labor statistics. In this statement Dr
Meeker disagrees with other govern
mental agencies, which, in the last few
days, have declared that the peak of
prices had been attained and that
declines already were noticeable in
many lines.
In a statement today Dr. Meeker
declares that lower prices or a gen
eral price decline should not be con
sidered vitally necessary, because any
marked decline would be accompa
nied by equally marked business and
industrial depression.
"The sufferings already endured by
the public during the rising price era
of the last four years." Dr. Meeker'
said, "will be multiplied tenfold if
prices drop to the 1913 level within
the next seven years. A period of
falling prices always is a time of
business depression, failures and un
employment."
Speed-Ip of l*rnduetlon I rgrd.
In order to combat the present high
prices and at the same time prevent
a period of panic and business de
pression. Dr. Meeker urges that pro
duction should be speeded up to the
limit and debts should be paid?not
with more debts, but with economic
goods and services. "These processes I
should be set in motion without de
lay." he says. "It will require sev- I
eral years to readjust Industry and 1
trade on a firm basis again, and in
the meantime the I'nlted States gov
ernment should, in my opinion. Join
with the other leading governments
of the world in the creation of an In
ternational commission to study ways
and means of setting up a true and
relatively constant standard of value
to displace the present monetary
units, which are merely units of
weight, and. consequently, variable
in value or purchasing power. The
most Important constructive work j
that can be done in any Held is the I
establishment of a stable standard
of value, so that all the neople, rich
and poor, employer and employe,
bondholder and stockholder, may be
spared for all time the agonies of
changing price levels."
Dr. Meeker savs the normal sea
sonal drop, the low price level for
all commodities combined will come
either in March. April or Mav of this
year as in normal times. This view
is not held by Federal Trade Com
missioner William B. Colver. who is a
member of the government living
cost control committee.
Mr. Colver Differs.
Mr. Colver claims that the high lev
els of prices for 200 articles combined
comes In February of each year and
that the low level does not come un
til August. He claims there is no rea
son why prices should not drop appre
ciably within the next year without
:.ny sort of financial trouble, panic or
any disturbance and that the action of
the government, committee In keeping

seasonal trend of prices shows a sharp
upward tendency from August to Feb
ruary. reaching its peak in the latter

Save the Playground!!
A systematic effort has been inaug
urated by the parks committee of the
Board of Trade to secure from Con
gress at this session appropriations
to put the District's playgrounds on a
permanent basis. This will appeal to
all the people in Washington and their
voices should be unanimously raised
in support of this demand. For the
playgrounds are life-savers. They pro
tect the children from accident by
giving them spaces in which to en
gage in their games of recreation and
exercise and, moreover, they teach
them better methods of play. They
are training grounds as well as play
grounds. The 15,000 children who on
an average visited these grounds daily
daring the past year are better and
healthier for the experience. With
more playgrounds?and there is room
for at least twice as many?a greater
number of children could be accom
modated. There should be one within
easy access for every child in Wash
ington. At present the nearest play
ground is often so far away from
home that children cannot go to it
and the result is that many young
sters play in the street, in constant
peril. The bare possibility of loss of
some of the now equipped play
grounds, as set forth in The Star the
other day, has aroused the keenest
apprehension among the parents of
Washington. The play spaces are now
regarded as essentials of the munici
pal equipment, and there can be no
doubt of the force of the appeal
which will be heard in Congress for
putting this system on a permanent
basis and extending it as rapidly as
the public funds permit.

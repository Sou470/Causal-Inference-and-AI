dustrial Concerns Seeking:

large, favorable trade balance, the
labor situation satisfactory and a
gradual increase in shipping facili

ing year are bright. Many foreign
commercial and, industrial concerns
are seeking trade connections here
and the Pessoa government is show
ing a friendly disposition toward for
eign investments.
Brazilian commercial and financial
interests generally are optimistic re
garding the business outlook of 1920.
Largely increased trade in structural
steel and agricultural, industrial and
other machinery and in combustible
oils and coal Is anticipated in North
America. One of the largest and
most modern packing establishments
in South America has Just been com
pleted at Sao Paulo by a Chicago
firm and is about to begin operations.
It expects to begin the exportation
of chilled beef on a large scale early
in the new year.
Development of Brazil s vast ore
deposits is expected to begin in 1920,
under a North American syndicate
which purposes to mine the ore ana
manufacture steel.
Definite action on the governments
measures for a downward revision of
the tariff has been deferred until the
next session of congress, which will
besin in May- because of the strong
opposition of the protectionist depu
The new year witnesses work start
ed '?n a project for the relief of a
larpre section of northeast Brazil, with
a population of 2.000.000. which has
been subject to devastation by peri
odic droughts. Congress has just
approved a measure appropriating
*50 000 000 for the construction of a
great system of irrigation works in
the affected district. It is understood
that the money for this purpose will
be raised by a loaiT in the United

J. P. MORGAN CO. FORM
FOREIGN CORPORATION

New Concern Plans to Further
International Trade on
Liberal Credits.
NEW YORK, January 1.?J. P. Mor
gan Co. announced the formation of
the Foreign Commerce Corporation of
America to engage in international
trade.
The incorporators are J. P. Morgan,
H. P. Davison. T. W. Lamont, E. R.
Stettinius and Grayson M. P. Murphy,
who will resign from the Guaranty
Trust Company to accept the presidency
of the corporation.
It is understood that one of the princi
pal functions of the new international
corporation will be to forward raw ma-,
terials, machinery and manufactured
goods to Poland, Czechoslovakia and
other distressed countries of central Eu
rope on a system -of liberal credits or
on the simple basis of the exchange or
goods for goods. The new concern will
work hand and hand with the recently
organized foreign finance corporation,
of which both Mr. Morgan and Mr. Da
vison are directors.
The principal concern of the finance
corporation will be to affect an improve
ment in the demoralized foreign ex
change. At the same time the foreign
commerce corporation will not confine
its attentions to Europe, but proposes to
become a vehicle for the development of
American trade in all parts of the world.

survey made public today.
The output for the year brought
only about $18,850,000. as compared

in 1916. This decline was attributed
chiefly to the reverting of the copper
industry to normal conditions as a
result of the fall in the price of the
metal since 1916, when the war de
mand greatly stimulated production.
A depression in gold mining was also
caused by high operating costs.
Kight copper mines were operated
in Alaska in 1919. producing about
4 4.S00.000 pounds, valued at $S.r>00.000.

$9,000,000 in 1919.
Alaska still contains large reserves
of gold-bearing gravels that can be

events of the year for the future in

"Better and cheaper steamboat

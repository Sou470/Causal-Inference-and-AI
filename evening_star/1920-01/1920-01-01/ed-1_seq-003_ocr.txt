TELLS OF RELIEF IN SYRIA.
Any possible impression that there
has been or might be partiality or dis
crimination along sectarian lines
shown in the distribution of relief in
Syria is baseless, according to Mrs.
Cabot Stevens, honorary director for
Washington's Near Bast campaign.
Such can be easily dispelled, she
points out in a statement issued from
headquarters in the Woodward Building,
by reference to reports to Cardinal Gib
bons and other eminent Catholic clergy
men in America, and to an urgent ap
peal on behalf of the relief campaign re
cently issued by Rt. Rev. J. W. Shaw,
Archbishop of New Orleans.
"Owing to the unavoidable absence
of regularly organized Catholic chari
table centers." the appeal states, in
part, "the distribution which Ameri
ca has sent to these starving peoples
has been largely in the hands of non
Catholic missionaries. A distinguished
Catholic lawyer, who spent seven
months in relief work in the near
east, has been gratified to tesUfy
from personal experience, however,
that these missionaries were impar
tial in the distribution of the funds
and supplies allotted to them, and
were free from any reproach of
proselytism."

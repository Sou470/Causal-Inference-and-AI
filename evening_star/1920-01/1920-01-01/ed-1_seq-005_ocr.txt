With the Japanese, 300 years before

resolution was a solemn affair,

old furniture and old clothing: were
cast off in exchange for new, eta
Through all the ages the New Year
day has been regarded as the time of
solemn renunciation of all follies and
a renewed amendment for the future.
The Egyptians in the days of the
Pharaohs symoolized their purifica
tions with elaborate baths and fast
ings: the Persians and Phoenicians
greeted the new year with prayer to
their heathen gods.
In those ancient days the New Year
greetings assumed rather a national
character, but in the passing of the
centuries old new year's vows have
lost their formal character. "Turning
over a new leaf," is now a matter of
individual and not a national con
|cern. "The swearing off," as the term

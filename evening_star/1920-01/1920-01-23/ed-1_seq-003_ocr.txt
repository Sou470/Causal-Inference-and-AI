GREENVILLE. S. C.. January 23
William J. Bryan, when shown the
statement of Homer S. Cummings,
chairman of the democratic national
committee, declared the position of
Mr. Cummings of impartiality as re
gards candidates for the democratic
nomination for President was not suf
ficient to justify his presence at the
inaugural dinner given Gov. Edward I.
Edwards of New Jersey Tuesday
night. Mr. Bryan characterized Mr.
Cummings' presence at the dinner as
"inexcusable." and said that if Mr.
Cummings "thinks it his duty to lend
his official presence to the starting of
such a boom, he had better retire from
the management of the party long
enough to get acquainted with the senti
ment of the party and the country."
Mr. Bryan, in a formal statement,
declared that reports of the Edwards'
banquet indicated that the inaugura
tion was incidental and the starting
of the presidential boom was the im
portant thing. "With three-fourths
of the democratic senators and two
thirds of the democratic members
voting for submission and with every
democratic state ratifying the pro
hibition amendment, and with the
liquor traffic outlawed by the con
sciences of the nation, Mr. Cummings
ought to know that the Edwards
candidacy is an insult to the demo
cratic party." said Mr. Bryan.
The former Secretary of State said
Mr. Cummings knew Gov. Edwards
promised to make New Jersey "as wet
as the Atlantic ocean." and announced
after his election that he would pre
vent, if possible, enforcement of the
national amendment in New Jersey.
He declared that if Mr. Cummings had
any sympathy with the Edwards'
boom he was not fit to be the head of j
the democratic organization, and if he
was not intelligent enough to recog
nize the injury he was doing the party
by professing impartiality between
the Edwards' boom and "legitimate
candidates." he ought to relieve the
organization from embarrassment by
resigning.

statistics.

establishments for last December with
December. 1918. the largest .increase,
26.9 per cent, appeared in the manufac

showed an increase in pay rolls, men's
ready-made clothing: showing an in
crease of 96.4 per cent and the woolen
industry of 58.7 per cent.
Comparing December with November
of this year there was an increase in
the number of workers in ten of the
thirteen industries, the greatest in
crease. 18.3 per cent, being in the Iron
and steel industry. An increase in the

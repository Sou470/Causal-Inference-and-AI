It takes all kinds of weather to
make a climate in the 38th degree and
some minutes of latitude north, and
the weather of last night and today
is one of the worst specimens that are
produced in the latitude of Wash
ington. Yet, it would be useless to
make a protest against it. Weather
will have its way. You might even
write a letter to the editor deprecat
ing this variety of weather, and it
would probably do very little good.
You could complain to the weather
bureau and the scientists there would
perhaps reply that whil? they can
read the weather's mind they cannot
control its disposition. You might
bring the matter before the Board of
Trade, Chamber of Commerce or the
Retail Merchants' Association and
they could do no more to mitigate
weather like this than to pass reso
lutions or refer them to a committee.
Yon might petition Congress for re
form in the weather, but the flowers
would bloom in the spring and the
green leaves of summer would turn
gold, purple and scarlet before the
investigation would get under way.
So. as bad as the weather is, let us
put on high rubbers or skates, raise
the umbrella and wait until a street
car comes along. Of course, it may
not come along, and then the next
best thing to do is to walk home un
less a neighbor with an automobile
comes into view and hails you.
Washington in the course of the
year does not get much weather like
this. Generally weather as bad as
this happens only once in four years
on March 4. But let us not be down
hearted! The clouds will pass away,
tbe ice will tnrn to slush and run
off to the grand old Potomac and
bright and merry days will come
again!

ous as influenza. Many physicians

ing influenza unless it be a clear and

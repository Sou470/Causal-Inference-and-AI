?Joaquin Santa-Ella, chief of the pe

the Huasteca Petroleum Company,
headed by Edward L. Doheny. had in-
formed the department it was dis-
posed to agree to all the provisions
of the proposed petroleum law. This
measure will come before the next
session of congress, which will begin
September 1, 1920.
It was asserted the company had
expressed its desire to enter into am
icable negotiations with the govern
ment for the purpose of settling all
disputed points, and the company, ac
cording to the under secretary, has
decided to push new work actively.
It has solicited permission to sink a
new well on lands secured in the east
ern part of the country.
According to the interview, the com
pany had headed opposition against
the projected petroleum law, but was
not now making any representations
directly to the American government j
as to difficulties it had encountered.
Senor Santa-Ella said other com
panies would follow the example of j
the Huasteca corporation.

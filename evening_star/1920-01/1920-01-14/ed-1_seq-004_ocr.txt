In addition to a few hundred other
odd little jobs on hand the American
Red Cross is performing a new stunt
?the repatriation of the very con
siderable number of little Europeans
who, at various times, attached them
selves to the A. E. F., in the capacity
of 'mascots.' Lieut. Henry Oldys,
Silver Spring, Md., was in charge of
the work at Brest, the big embarka
tion center, where dozens of these I
youngsters eventually showed up and I
had to be left behind by their adored
Yanks, until a regular "mascot col
ony" grew up at that port.
Lieut. Oldys saw that they were
either restored to their homes or
placed in suitable Institutions, accord
ing to a recent bulletin from national
headquarters of the Red Cross in this
city. French attempts to handle the
situation, it is stated, met with little
success because the dreaded and also
despised French police were involved
in the transaction.
The nationality of these urchins was
varied, French, German, English, Yid
dish. Russian. Polish, Rumanian and
Danish being some of the languages
heard in and around the barracks
housing the special "mascot company"
into which Lieut. Oldys tactfully turn
ed the somewhat turbulent mascot
colony. Although many tried, only a
few got as far as America on a troop
ship.

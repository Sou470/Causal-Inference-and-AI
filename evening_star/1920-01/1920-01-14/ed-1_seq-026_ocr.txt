LOS ANGELES, Calif., January 14.?
Denial of charges that Jack Dempsey.
heavyweight pugilistic champion, was a.
"draft dodger" and "slacker" and de
fense of his conduct during the war was
made by Jack Kearns, Dempsey* man
ager, in a signed statement uoued here
today.
"Dempsey was not a 'draft dodger'
during the war," Kearns' statement
said. "Any one who makes such a
charge cannot support It with facts.
Did Not Claim Exemption.
"When the draft came Dempsey was
placed in Class 4-A because he was
married, and. in addition, was sup
porting a mother, an invalid brother
and a sister. Dempsey made no claims
for exemption.
"Some time later the draft board re
adjusting classifications placed Demp
sey in Class A-l. but almost imme
diately afterward put him in class
2-B. He remained there during the
rest of the war. If the conflict had
lasted a few weeks longer, it seems
a certainty he would have performed
service."
Kearns recounted Dempsey's serv
ices as a riveter in Philadelphia and
Seattle shipyards, and added:
Helped liaise SI 00,000.
"He also was appearing in fistic,
contests at benefits for the different
war charities, By this means approxi
mately' $100,000 was raised for the
various war funds."
Dempsey paid traveling expenses and
fought without remuneration, Kearns,
said, and "frequently was 'broke' as a
consequence."
"Those who are charging Dempsey
with being a 'slacker' because he worked
in the shipyards and helped to raise
nearly $100,000 for the various war
charfties,'" the statement concluded, "a^e
smudging the reputation of every other
exempted man who served the nation
in the Army at home."
Condemned In Legrton Posts.
Kearns defense of Dempsey is the out
growth of condemnatory resolutions
passed by American Legion posts in sev
eral states, including Texas, Colorado,
Indiana and Ohio, based on his failure
to enter the service. The New Jersey
athletic commission also has announced

American Legion yesterday announced
the organization has not considered

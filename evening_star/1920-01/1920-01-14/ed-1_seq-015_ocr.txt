US INSPECTOR
MEETS OPPOSITION
The first instance of opposition to
the taking of the fourteenth decennial
census came yesterday afternoon when
John Glick of 1401 Massachusetts ave
nue, census inspector in the Southeast
section, was stoned and driven from
Navy place southeast when he ap
peared in the half-alley, half-street, to
enumerate the inhabitants.
Mr. Glick was called to Navy place
by Kdith Hannah, a census enumerator
covering district No. 104, when she de
clared herself unwilling to enter the
alley on account of its forbidding ap
pearance. Knocking at the door of the
first house in Navy place, Mr. Glick
was answered by the bellicose voice of
a piant colored man, who threatened
to shoot him if he didn't get out and
get out Quick. "What do you'se
want?" the colored man shouted.
Went to Take Onan*.
"I have come to take the census,"
Mr. Glick retorted.
"I don't care what you come for,"
the colored man said, "if you don't
fret out, and get out quick, I'se swine
fill you so full of lead you won't be
able to get out." Mr. Glick told the
man he and his family would not be
included in the census, but the man
did not seem to care, as he shut the
door in Glick's face.
Proceeding to the next house, Mr.
Glick met with no response, but the
storm broke when he approached the
third house. Thoroughly aroused
and breathing defiance to the invader,
many of the inhabitants of Navy place
poured out of their homes and set to
work to repel the unwelcome visitor.
They did it with ancient but none the
less efficient weapons?stones and
sticks?and today Mr. Olick, after his
hurried exit from the alley, is nursing
a number of minor cuts and bruises
where he was hit by the missiles.
Mr. Glick reported the incident to
Capt. Mulhall of the fifth precinct and
was promised police protection in en
tering the place today.
Accompanies Knumrrator.
A policeman was sent to Navy place
this morning and went the rounds of
the houses there with Mr. Glick. No
trouble of any kind was reported and
all the inhabitants gave their reports
when asked. ("apt. Mulhall of the
fifth precinct said today there was no
trouble of any kind at Navy place
yesterday.
One woman, who wouldn't tell her
right age, was brought sharply to
terms by an enumerator yesterday
when the census taker told her that if
she wouldn't answer the question he
would put her age down as fifty. She
promptly answered the question.

SHIPPING MANIPULATIONS
BOOST POTATO PRICES
Chicago Food Director Reveals
Conditions at Chicago Interest
ing to Consumers.
CHICAGO, January 16.?Manipula
tion of shipments has caused a jump
in potato prices here from $3 and
$3.25 a hundred pounds to $5 and $5.65,
according to a statement issued today
by Russell .1. Poole, director of the
bureau of foods, markets and farm
products of the city and health de
partment.
On December 5 the statement said
potatoes were selling at the lower
figure. There was a gradual increase
until January 5, when the price
jumped 35 cents a hundred.
"We find that during this time,
due to the negligence in handling
potatoes by shippers or the railroads,
there have been S61.106 pounds of po
tatoes frozen, spoiled or wasted." said
the statement. "This is a matter of
14,352 bushels and, at the present
price of 5 cents a pound, as they cost
the consumer, that would mean $43,
055.30."
Poole said that most of the Michi
gan and Wisconsin potatoes sent to
the Chicago market have been held
by farmers and the Potato Growers'
Exchange, "which seems to me con
trary to the Sherman anti-trust law."
He pointed out that the farmer sells
to a dealer at his nearest market,
the dealer to Chicago brokers, who
handle only carload lots: the broker
to commission men, the commission
men to retailers and the retailers to
consumers. The consumer, he said,
pays the freight, all transfer and
storage charges and a handsome profit
to each of the parties to the deal.

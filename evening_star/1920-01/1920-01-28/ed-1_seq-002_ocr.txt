House Bill.
Senator Gerry of Rhode Island yester

House bill increasing- the pay of the
Navy, so that the commissioned of

resentative Kelly. These increases to

Senator Gerry', Statement.
In a statement. Senator Gerry said:

most cursory examination of naval
conditions, it is apparent that the
service is being crippled because of
the inadequate pay received by its
personnel.
"With the present high cost of living
it is almost impossible for the person
nei to live upon what they are now
receiving, with the result that the de
partment is flooded with resignations,
and the shortage has now reached
such a condition that ships are woe
fully undermanned. If this condition
is allowed to continue the Navy is
bound to be seriously crippled and its
morale impaired for the future.
Discusses the Howe Bill.
"As the House passed the bill, the

ing a special post-graduate course in
canon law at Catholic University, died

town University Hospital. The attack
lot pneumonia developed from a case
of influenza.
Father Doran was twenty-six years

Bishop Conroy at Troy, N. Y. His
brother Muurioe P. IXiran, a member
of the faculty at Catholic University,
is seriously ill of pneumonia at his
home. 1618 1st street northeast.

N Y He was graduated from 1a Salle
Institute in 1911 and from Niagara
University in 1914. Two years later
he received the degree of M. A. from

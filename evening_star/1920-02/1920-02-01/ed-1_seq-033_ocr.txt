THE STORY THE WEEK HAS TOLD
BY NEWBOLD NOYES.
THERE Is a viewpoint to be cultivated
in reading the news of the week that
makes that hour devoted by Jim and
Jane of a Sunday morning to the busi
ness of reviewing the passage of major news
items less of a task and more of a game. It
springs from the conception that news, wet
from the presses, tells in very truth a vital,
swiftly moving story. The form in which the
news is, of necessity, served to the public all
too often leaves the casual reader the impres
sion of a hodgepodge of incidents, of a crazy
quilt of events. Yet if one will search with
reasonable patience it is not hard to discover
that the story which is being told has In the
big news at least a central plot; and that a
single thread leads smoothly from each major
item to the others, till all are linked together.
The game for Jim and Jane to play together is
to follow that thread till the story the weeks
are telling may become to them, from an ac
cumulation of unrelated facts, a tale of which
paragraph and chapter and volume follow each
other In ordered sequence, each essential to
the full understanding of the others.

To those whose political philosophy

The chief reliance for clothing is

Of wool, which has come to be used in

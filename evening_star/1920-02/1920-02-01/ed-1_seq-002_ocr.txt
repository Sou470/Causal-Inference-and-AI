Recurrence of Influenza
in 33 or 66 Week Cycles,
Recurrance of influenza epidemics in
cycles 33 or 66 weeks apart were dis
cussed in a statement issued last night
by the bureau of census, based on a
study made by Dr. John Brown lee. an
Knglish physician, who advanced the
cycle theory.
"It is now exactly sixty-six weeks
since the mortality peak of the epi
demic in Chicago," the statement said.
"This also is true of New York city
and Washington. In all three of these
places influenza is epidemic.
?The periodicity suggests that we
may be dealing with infecting organ
isms which not only have the power
to reproduce themselves In a virulent
form continuously for a longer period
if susceptible persons are exposed,
but which also have the power of
developing in cycles of thirty-three or
sixty-six weeks. This recurrance of
the epidemic after sixty-six weeks cer
tainly strengthens the view that the
epidemics of 1889. 1890, 1918 and 1920
all have a common etiology."
The first application of the platoon
plan in the primary and grammar
grades of the local public school sys
tem will be made tomorrow in the
Bark View School, when the second
semester of the school year begins.
The platoon plan is a Variation of
the noted "Gary plan." first employed
on a large scale in the Gary, Ind.,
schools several years ago. It does not
mean half-?lay classes, but consists in
dividing th# classes in the school into
two groups. One group occupies the
classrooms for basic studies while the
other group is assembled in the audi
torium. At stated intervals the two
groups alternate between classrooms
and auditorium.
The plan permits the accommodation
of many more pupils with a given
amount of room, teaching force and
equipment than could b? accommodat
ed under the old method. It is de
scribed by school authorities as a
sort of emergency measure and its
permanent value is yet to be estab

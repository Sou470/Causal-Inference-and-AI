Cause* of Generator Burnont*.
A. H. MacC. asks: What causes a
generator to burn T>ut and how can
this be prevented? If one notices that
the battery is not charging what can
be done to the generator so that the
car can safely be driven to a garage?
Answer: Anything that short-cir
cuits the armature windings may do
so, for instance copper dust between
the commuator leads or a break
down of the insulation between arma
ture coils or between the armature
windings and the core. These acci
dents cause heavy, idle currents to
flow in the windings, heat them ex
cessively, destroy the insulation and
render the armature useless. The ex
cessive heat from the engine tends to
ruin the generator insulation in time.
A generator field winding may be
burned out by the battery having be
come disconnected. This throws so
much current through the field wire
that it overheats and its insulation
fails. If the battery becomes discon
nected electrically connecting the
main generator wire to the engine
frame should protect the field wind
ings.

Wife of Allied High Commissioner
Has Just Recovered From
Serious Illness.
Mrs. William N. Haskell, wife of
Col. Haskell, allied high commis
sioner in the Trans-Caucasus and
representative of the Near East Re
lief in Armenia, has returned to re
sume her work among the starving
and stricken people of Armenia, aft
er her recovery from an attack of
typhus which nearly cost her her life.
She is now in Erivan, capital of the
new Armenian republic, where she is
in charge of Near Kast Relief work,
while Col. Haskell is assisting the
infant republic to solve its govern
mental problems.
Col. and Mrs. Haskell are in direct
charge of distribution of relief to
nearly a million destitute Armenians
and about 220,000 children orphaned
in the years of warfare in which Ar
menia was pillaged and its men and
women massacred or driven from
their homes to become refugees. Mrs.
Haskell was stricken with typhus in
the course of her work in Armenia
and was taken to Constantinople. For
weoks it was thought she could not
survive.
Mrs. Haskell Is known to thousands
of refugees whom she has helped by
the title of "Queen of Armenia." She
was given this title when an Ar
menian. seeing Col. Haskell for the
first time and being impressed by
his striking appearance and mili
tary manner, exclaimed to a com
panion:
"Thank God. the Americans have
sent us a king!"
Reports from Col. and Mrs. Haskell
to the Near East Relief, headquarters
of which are at 1 Madison avenue.
New York, state that more than 800,
000 homeless and starving adults, be
sides about 220,000 orphans, are being
cared for by the Near East Relief in
the Trans-Caucasus. The working
personnel, under the direction of Col.
and Mrs. Haskell, includes thirty-six
physicians and surgeons, seventy-six
nurses, seven mechanics, fifteen in
dustrial experts, fourteen bacteriolo
gists. sixteen agricultural experts,
nineteen teachers, twenty adminis
trators, thirty-four secretaries, thir
ty-seven engineers, fifty supply and
transport workers, forty-six Army of
ficers anfl 176 orphanage and general
relief workers.
Funds for the support of these
workers, as well as money with
which to purchase food, clothing,
seeds, farm implements and general
supplies, are furnished by the Near
East Relief, in behalf of which a
campaign to raise money begins in
the United States today, to continue
to February 22. Campaign headquar
ters in Washington have been opened
at 819 15th street. in charge of work
ers of the Washington committee of
the Near East Relief.

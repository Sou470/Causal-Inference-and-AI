U. S. WORKERS REDUCED
BY 1,067 IN JANUARY
3,832 Leave Service and 2,765 New
Clerks Appointed; War De
partment Leads in Decrease.
A net reduction of 1,067 in the
number of government employes in
Washington was made in January, ac
cording to reports sent to the Civil
Service Commission by the various
departments.
The actual number separated from
the service in the month was 3,832, but
2.765 new clerks were appointed. These
figures furnish proof that there is still
an enormous "turnover," or coming and
going of workers in the government
service.
The biggest reduction was In the
War Department, where the force was
cut by 635. The Treasury Depart
ment was a close second, with a net
reduction of 621. Other reductions
were reported as follows: Navy De
partment. 247; Labor, 144 and Agri
culture,
The biggest increase for the month
was in the Department of Commerce,
where the force was increased by 423.

the parliamentary bodies. Save for
the "advice and consent" of the Sen
ate in appointment, the members of
the President's cabinet have no direct
relationship with Congress. They may
be summoned as witnesses in matters

The Constitution's only reference to

summon his "cabinet" in session.
In these circumstances, with the

chief of state. Informal conferences
could have been held by the depart
ment heads in their own offices -or at
their own homes without evoking any
criticism, but they would not have
been so effective in restoring public
confidence in the stability of the gov
ernment. Thus in following an exam
ple set during the illness of Presi
dent Taylor, when the cabinet* met
with the chief's chair vacant, Secr
etary Ionising rendered a valuable
service to the country.
No possible harm could follow the

There was no concealment of the fact

On the contrary, as just indicated,

regard the cabinet meetings as the

Needed: Plain Common Sense.
Despite hysteria manifested in some
quarters, neither Europe nor America
is at the brink of a financial crash.
There is no warrant for panic, but
there is evety need for plain common
sense and ordinary business prudence.
It is no time to rock the boat.
In another column of today's Star
is published an article, based on in
formation derived from authoritative
and official sources, showing that Eu
rope will be able to obtain in this
country commercial credits sufficient
to meet all her real needs for Amer
ican goods and explaining the methods
by which this will be accomplished.
There are to be no more direct loans
by this government to European gov
ernments, but the War Finance Cor
poration is prepared to lend a billion
dollars to American exporters, which
will enable European buyers to get
the long-time credits necessary to the
work of reconstruction. Still larger
credits aftf available through estab
lished Banking channels, and the sale
in this country of foreign-issued and
foreign-owned securities will help still
further to finance the requirements
of the striken peoples.
America is prepared to extend to
the European nations all the help they
really need. What has been cut off is
help that is not vital to their recov
ery and whieh it is better they should
not have. There is no cure for the
war-sickness of Europe except the cur
tailment of imports and increased pro
duction of goods for export. When
they curtail thei# imports and increase
theif exports, necessarily there will be
reduction in the volume of America's
expoTt trade to Europe. Production
in this country will slow down, prices
are likely to drop, and there will be
lessened excess profits and fewer in
flated incomes to bear the burden of
taxation. The readjustment is not go
ing to be without its pains and dis
comforts, but it will not be a major
operation.
There is need both in Europe and
in America for stout hearts and steady
nerves. The world is very, very sick, j
but mortification has fiot yet set in. I
The patient will not die?unless the
doctors and the nurses kill it.

Sanitary Colors.
Because hospital cots and tilerl bath- I
room floors and nurses' aprons are
white, we have all grown Arm in the j
belief that there is something intrin
sically sanitary about white as a color.
Of course, the idea underlying this
belief is that one can see the least
spot of dirt on white. On colors it is
sometimes invisible.
But there is no other reason why
white should be considered a sanitary
color. And anybody who has lain for
a long time in a white cot. on a white
pillow, covered with a white coverlet,
and gazed at white furniture, white
wall and chairs, and a white-clad
nurse, will admit that white is a mo
notonous color. It tires the eyes when
the sun glares on it.
A doctor in the west who was re
cently planning a hospital thought
about this problem of color. Blue, she
knew, had a soothing effect on the
nerves. That fact had been scientifi
cally proved. So she decided to. intro
duce a blue color scheme in her hos
pital. Blue tiles were substituted for
white, blue walls were painted, blue
"nameled furniture was used. And in
stead of a white hospital, she opened
a blue one. The results of the blue
atmosphere are proving that she was
quite justified in her innovation.
.Now all this goes to prove that
there is no reason why we should con
sider a while-tiled kitchen or bath
room or a white-painted nursery any
more sanitary than one tiled in blue or
red or green. If the tiles are kept
equally clean, the colored ones are i
quite as conducive to lu?alth as the j
white ones.
The fact that white is a tell-tale
color still remains, of course, so where j
shiftless servants are concerned, it is
a wise color to choose. And for clothes
white is still the best color for babies'
frocks, and nurses' aprons and grown
people's underwear, for it can be
boiled without danger of fading. A
nurse dressed to match a green nurs
ery, nicely tiled and enameled, would
soon present a sorry, yellow-gray ap
pearance. for strong soap and boiling
water would quickly rob her refresh
ing uniform of4ts charm. But white
looks snowier each time it comes from
the laundry?if it is properly washed
and ironed.

AVOIDING "ONE SIDED" BRAKING
Don't Do All Your Holding Hack "With One Wheel.
Does your foot brake act equally upon each roar wheel? This is an
important question at all times, but especially so in icy weather, when the
liability of side slip is great, for unequal brake action tends to produce
skidding. The same query may be raised regarding- the emergency brake,
but not quite so pertinently perhaps. Very likely your brakes are provided
with equalizers, that are supposed to divide the braking effect exactly, but
it is not safe to depend upon them. They do act to divide the force, which
is applied by the pedal or lever to the mechanism that operates the bands,
j hut this does not necessarily mean that they divide the braking effect, f<?r
if one band is greasy, glazed over or otherwise deficient in "taking hold"
I power, of if the band operating toggles and cams of one brake operate with
difficulty, while those of the other operate freely, there may still be a vast
difference between the holding power of the two brakes, even though the
same force is applied to them. The only sure way of determining equality
of braking action and thus insuring safety, the full retarding action, title
to the entire rear weight of the car and uniform rear tire wear, is to make
I periodical tests. This is readily and quickly done as follows: .lack up
iboth rear wheels, block the front wheels, start the engine slowly and put
'in high gear. Then have some one push the foot brake pedal very gently
land gradually forward and note the result on the rear wheels. The one
I which slows down first is the one which is doing the more braking. Perhaps
an even better way is not to run the engine or put the* car in gear, but to
have the assistant gradually apply the brake and see which wheel first
completely resists turning by hand. This is th^ one that is doing more of
the holding back. The emergency brake requires the same kind of test.
When one brake is found decidedly weak, it may require treatment of its
b. nd lining, the shortening of its pull-rod, the oiling or adjustment of its
mechanism, or the replacement of parts which may have become seriously
worn.

No decision was arrived at in yester-.
day's meeting of the heads of railroad
unions and officials of the railroad

employes, and the negotiations last
night promised to extend well into
next week. It was understood several
"important differences" remained to
be considered following yesterday's
conference, the fourth of the series.
It was said some union officials were
chafing under the delay, having ex
pected a definite proposal yesterday.
Director General Hinea was unable to
offer anything definite because he
could not complete the plan to be of
fered the union heads. It was under
stood the union leaders had not re
ceived the final plan of the director
general up to a late hour last night.
Cannot Become Insistent.
Representatives of the employes,
however, apparently feel they cannot
press their case too strenuously, as
they were invited to the conference
on the initiative of the director gen
eral. He was under no obligation to
call them to Washington, it was said.
As he had called the conference, the
majority of the union heads feel that
the spirit of fairness should not be
abused.
The exact proposals which have been
submitted by both parties to the con
ferences have been xealously guarded.
Railroad administration officials said
they were not prepared to give to the
public a concrete cxprAsion, and em
ployes' representatives maintained
silence, pointing out that Mr. Hines
might easily have dodged them until
after March 1, when the roads go
back to private control, if he so de
sired.
Prew Their Cause.
Many local unions have communi
cated with their representatives here,
urging that the continued high cost
of living be kept persistently before
the government officials.
Some leaders, however, are reluctant
to call up that argument continuous
ly at this time because of the pos
sibilities in the present situation of
foreign exchange, it was declared.
Those few believe a decided drop may
come soon and they prefer to await
the outcome of the new condition.

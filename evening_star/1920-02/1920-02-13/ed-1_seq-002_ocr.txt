SUNDAY OBSERVANCE
BILL TO BE INTRODUCED
A Sunday observance bill for the
District of Columbia has been pre
pared by Representative Henry W.
Temple of Pennsylvania and will be
introduced today or tomorrow. The
bill makes it unlawful for any one to
work or to employ any one to work on
Sunday, except for charitable and nec
essary purposes. It specifically for
bids the keeping open of theaters,
base ball parks, dance halls and bowl
ing alleys. All persons who work at
necessary jobs, which cannot be laid
aside on Sunday, the bill provides,
must be given a day of rest during
the week. Persons who are believers
in a religious creed other than the
Christian and who observe some other
day than Sunday as a day of rest are
exempt under the act.
Mr. Temple said today that his sole
purpose in introducing the bill is to
see that every one has a day of rest at
least once a week.

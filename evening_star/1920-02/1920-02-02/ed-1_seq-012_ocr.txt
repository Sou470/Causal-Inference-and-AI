ASSISTANT MANAGER
First Step Toward Co-Ordinating
and Unifying Community
Service.
As the first definite step toward co
ordination of varied lines of com
munity service into a unified service
which shall reach every part of the
countrj-, the Red Cross announced to
day from its national headquarters
the appointment of Fred C. Croxton
of Columbus, Ohio, as assistant gen
eral manager of the Red Cross in
charge of service organization. Aid
in the study of community problems
and how best the specialized services
of the Red Cross may be utilized in
their solution is the fundamental ob
ject of the newly organized depart
ment which Mr. Croxton will head.
The new assistant manager served
both as federal food administrator
for Ohio and as vice chairman of the
state* branch of the Council of Na
tional Defense. He is an industrial i
expert and has also served the federal
government in various important ca
pacities.
The first step in development of the
new plan of service organization has j
been the detachment from the depart- j
ment of civilian relief of the bureaus
of field supervision, education and I
rural organization. In addition to
these three bureaus there will be
created in the Red Cross a bureau of
survey, a bureau of records and sta
tistics and a bureau of campaigns.
The lines of work already undertaken
in different chapters include health
education, disaster preparedness, home
service, first-aid instruction, junior
membership and various other types
of recreational, educational and eco
nomic activities.

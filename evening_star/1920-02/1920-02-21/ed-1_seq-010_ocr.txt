ANSWERS TO QUESTIONS 1-10.
1. In early history, the notable family of Jacob, consisting of himself,
his twelve sons and their descendants, were for a time absent f.om
their own land and dwelling in the land of Egypt. Exodus. 1 .1.
Jacob's name had been changed to "Israel," hence his descendants
are known as the "Israelites." Genesis. 32.28.
2. The children of Israel were fruitful; they increased abundantly, am!
multiplied, and waxed exceeding mighty, and the land was filled
with them. Exodus. 1.7.
3. The new king said unto his people: Behold, the people of the chil
dren of Israel are more and mightier than we. Come on. let us deal
wisely with them; lest they multiply, and it come to pass, that, when
there talleth out any war. they join also unto our enemies, and fight
against us. and so get them up out of the land. Exodus, 1.9-10.
4. The new king issued an edict concerning the birth of any Hebrew
baby: if the new born infant was a boy, it_was to he-killed by being
cast into the river; if a girl, it was to be saved alive. Exodu*. 1.16-22.
5. The baby Moses, destined to bceorne a world-wide character, was
miraculously saved from death at the time of the execution of
Pharaoh's cruel edict. Exodus, 2.1-10.
6. Moses was (1) a Levite by birth; (2) was a goodly child; (3) was
hidden three months in the home of his parents; (4) was later placed
in an ark made by his mother, and left among the bulrushes; (5) was
tenderly watched by his sister, stationed nearby; (6) was found by
the king's daughter: (7) was trained during the early years of his
life by his mother who was~hired as his nurse; (8) was adopted and
named by the king's daughter. Exodus, 2.1-10.
7. Although reared in the midst of luxuries, Moses.gave his loyalty to
his own people, the Hebrews, who were of the working class.
Exodus. 2.11-12. (The Xew Testament states that Moses refused to be
called the son of Pharaoh. Hebrews, 11.23. 24.)
8. His mother, though only a hired servant, was evidently a great factor
in molding the future character of the man Moses. Exodus, 2.9.
9. In the effort to secure justice tor his own people, Moses unfortu
nately killed an Egyptian, and hid the body in the sand.
10. To escape the anger of the king. Moses fled to the land of Midian,
and, while dwelling there,'was one day sitting by a well, evidently,
to rest. Exodus, 2.15.

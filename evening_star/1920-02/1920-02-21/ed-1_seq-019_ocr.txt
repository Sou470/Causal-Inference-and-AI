on the contrary, in all the leading in

banker is inclined to be pessimistic,

Market Movement I>l*eu??ed.
The market movement of the past
week, so far as it has given judgment
between these two attitudes, has dis
tinctly favored the side of the busi

the talk of industrial depression and
who have been frightened by the com
parisons that have been drawn with
years of panic have sold out their
securities at a tremendous sacrifice,
and are now surprised that the mar
ket has rebounded and has recovered a
very considerable part of the losses of
the past month.
In an analysis of what has hap
pened it may be said that these
widely published statements by men
in high places in the financial and
political world are chiefly responsible
for the losses which the public has
sustained. The industrial situation
is absolutely different frojn that of
the past occasions where depression
and panic have occurred. The es
sential features of these past crises
have been, first, an overproduction of
goods and, second, an oversupply of
labor. Now we have a condition ex
actly the reverse. Instead of over
production there is underproduction.
instead of unemployment there is a
labor scarcity which is all the time
becoming' more acute.
Measures for Credit Situation.
In order to improve the credit
situation it may be necessary to take
more drastic measures than have yet
been adopted. Putting up the offi
cial discount rate from 4% to 6 per
cent has accomplished nothing'.
Neither would a further advance in
the federal reserve rate to 6% Per
cent, as has been discussed. These
higher rates would simply be paid
and the extra charge for money
be passed along to the consumer in
the shape of higher prioes, the same
as all other items of increased pro
duction cost. The only way in which
liquidation of commercial loans
could be brought about would be
through an outright refusal to re
new these loans when they fall due.
If this radical course -were adopted
it would naturally reduce the volume
of business in the country, but i.
would not lessen the soundness of the
business position so long as there is a
clear case of demand exceeding sup
ply. For this reason we believe that
the business man's point of view as
contrasted with the banker's point of
i view is the more trustworthy one to
follow in the present situation.

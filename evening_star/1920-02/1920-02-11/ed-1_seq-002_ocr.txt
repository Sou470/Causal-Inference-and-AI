A "key position" for any given group

The reclassification commission does

It has been found that practically

It was early found by the re

what are regarded as the lesser po
sitions or with the higher positions
would lead to confusion and difficulty
all around. It was not found feasi
ble to begin with the employe having
the least duties and usually the
smallest number of qualifications, nor
was it found feasible to begin salary
determinations with the positions re
quiring the greatest number of quali

number of duties attached to it
Salary figuring from either of these

ers that the thing could not be solved
in that way. The object in salary
fixing is to arrive at equity between
the various classes of employes. There
are 1,700 classes in all, these cutting

ice" is the thing, and the service in
cludes as many different classes as

Arrive at Solution.
Solution of the problem, the modus
operandi, came through the use of the
"key position." This method of sal
ary determinations allows the mem
bers of the reclassification commis
sion to fix first of all the salaries of
the great bulk of employes in any
one service.
This is a job in itself requiring the
highest concentration, backed by all
the acquired knowledge which the

of employes doing similar work in va
rious cities of the country outside the
government service.
But once the "key position" for any

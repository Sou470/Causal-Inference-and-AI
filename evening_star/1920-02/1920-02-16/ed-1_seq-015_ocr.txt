ACCUSES COLLEGE GIRLS

Factories and Create Unrest
Among Fellow Employes.
Reports that a number of graduates
of women's colleges throughout the
country are creating discord among
workers in the various industrial
centers are being thoroughly investi
gated by officials of the Department of
Justice, it was learned today.
Miss Edith C. Strauss, director of
the division of women's activities in
connection with the Department of
Justice's campaign to reduce the high
cost of living, said today that reports
of the activities of college women to
sow seeds of discontent in the fac
tories have been coming In to her of
fice for some time.
"According to these reports," Miss
Strauss said, "woman graduates have
been getting jobs in factories, where
they soon find fertile soil for the dis
semination of their propaganda of
unrest. When they have finished
their task in one factory they get
positions in others. It seems to have
become a fad for some college-bred
women to imagine they have a mis
sion to perform?that of spreading
radical doctrines among the workers
of the country, and the Department
of Justice is going to thoroughly in
vestigate their activities."

A Sign of Spring.
Winter thunderstorms are not of
extraordinary rarity in this climate,
and that of Saturday night is to be
taken as a manifestation of good omen.
It was followed, as frequently happens,
by a sharp drop in temperature.
Weather-wise ones, indeed, prepared
for the chilly northwest wind as soon
as they heard the rumbles following
the flashes Saturday night. The de
scent of the mercury was rapid and
severe and brought a bit of the cold
est weather Washington lias experi
enced this winter. Now tho forecasts
of the long-range prophets tend toward
an early spring. In ordinary circum
stances the winter cannot last more
than three or four weeks longer. Some
of this city's severest visitations, how
ever, have occurred in this period just
before the buds appear on the trees.
It is to be expected that there will be
at least one more sharp bit of winter
after the moderation of the present
low temperatures. The hope of all is
that it will not bring much snow.
Washington does not care much for
snow and looks ardently forward to
the days when the parks show their
first soft greens and the birds begin
to sing.
Spanish theaters threaten to close
in protest against taxation. Bull
fighting seems to be the only form of
entertainment which can be relied on
in that region to defy the high cost
of enjoyment.
The suggestion made by Col. House
some time ago that it would be well to
tranquillize proves to be one of those
"one best bets" that everybody missed.
A woman's party to fight both the
republican and democratic parties
would bring the eternal triangle most
interestingly into politics.
It needs no bulletin from Dr. Gray
son to convince the public that the
President is robust and energetic.
A higher cost of railroading can
scarcely fail to have an influence on
H. C. L. in general.

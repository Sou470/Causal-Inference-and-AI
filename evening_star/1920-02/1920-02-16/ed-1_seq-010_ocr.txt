PAUL F. MYERS.
Paul P. Myers, chief clerk of the
Treasury Department for the past two
years, today was appointed deputy
commissioner of internal revenue, the
appointment to take effect immediate
ly.
Wilmer G. Piatt, for the past year
assistant to the controller of the
Treasury, was named Mr. Myers* suc
cessor.
Native of PeiifrlTUte.
Mr. Myers is a PennsyIranian by
birth. He is a native of York Spring.
Pa., born there in 1887. In the fall
of 1909 he entered Princeton, and be
fore his graduation in 1913 had won
distinction as an orator and debater
after several engagements on debat
ing teams against Harvard and Yale.
Coming to the Treasury Department
shortly after his graduation, he was
appointed 011 a committee to draw up
income tax regulations and later was
appointed to the income tax division
of the internal revenue unit. On
April 1, 1915. he was made chief clerk
or the bureau of internal revenue. He
was appointed executive attorney for
the bureau by Secretary McAdoo on
November 1. 1917, and succeeded
James L. Wiimeth as chief clerk of
the department when the latter was
placed in charge of the bureau of en
graving and printing on December
10, 1917.
Since his occupancy of the chair of
chief clerk, the department has grown
from a personnel of 7,500 to 35,000 in
this city and from 10,000 to 40,000 in
the outside field.
Mr. Myers has been active in wel
fare work. He established the cafe
teria service at the Treasury Depart
ment and organized co-operative buy
ing clubs for the department, which
are now accommodating 6,000 mem
bers.
He also placed relief rooms in all
buildings under the Treasury De
partment.

Department, succeeding Mr. Myers.
The ceremonies took place in the of
fice' of the chief'clerk, in the pres
ence of a number of officials of that
department.
Mr. Piatt was originally appointed
to the Treasury Department from
Lawrenceburg, Ind.. on March 4, 188S,
in the office of the auditor for the

all of the clerical grades of that of
fice. In 1913 he was made assistant

vision and assisted in the organiza

when it was a new feature.
In 1916 he was appointed chief
clerk of the office of the controller of
the Treasury, and in July. 1919, was
made assistant to the controller, a
position which he has occupied up to
the present time.
He is a pioneer resident of Takoma
Park, Md., having located there in
IS 88. In civic matters he has
been keenly interested in the wel
fare and progress of Takoma Park
and has served as president of the

cumbent. He was instrumental in
organizing and was the first master
of Takoma Lodge, No. 29, P. A. A. M.,

other local organizations.

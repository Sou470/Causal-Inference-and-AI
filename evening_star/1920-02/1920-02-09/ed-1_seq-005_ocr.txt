IN UNITED STATES GROWS
Statistical Summary of Industry
for 1919 Made Public by Fed
eral Trade Commission.
Production ef news print paper in
the United States reached a higher
point during 1919 than in the preced
ing two years, according to the sta
tistical summary of the paper indus
try tor the year, made public today
by the Federal Trade Commission.
American production of news print
paper for 1919 totaled 1.374,517 net
tons. The 1918 production was 1,
260,285 net tons.
Production reached its highest point
in October. 1919, when 125,216 tons
were produced. Shipments for the
year totaled 1.378,556 tons, and 15,369
tons were on hand at the end of the
year.
Production of standard news paper
for the year was 1,227.180 net tons,
a little more than that of 1918 and
less than that of 1917. The peak of
production was reached in October,
when 110.902 tons were manufactured.
Shipments of standard news for the
year totaled 1,230,498.
Total production for all grades of
paper was 6,190,361 tons. There were
220,589,829 pounds of news print paper
exported from the United States in
1919. Imports were 1.255,462,866
pounds. News print paper is the only
grade of which the United States is
a heavy importer. Practically all of
the tonnage, the value of which
amounted to $43,674,094 for 1919, is
imported from Canada. The value of
the exports of news print amounted
to 110.100.229 in 1919. The value of
the exports of news print amounts to
less than 25 per cent of the value of
the news print imported.

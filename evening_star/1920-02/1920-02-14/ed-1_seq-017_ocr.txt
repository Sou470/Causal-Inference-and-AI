WALTj STREKT. NEW YORK. Feb
ruary 14.?Recovery ,went further on
the stock exchange this morning, in
conformity with the requirements of
a proper upturn following the wind
up of prolonged and extensive decline.
There was no news, but overnight re
flection of yesterday's happenings
tended to increase confidence in the
market position and to inspire a bet
ter feeling also regarding the change
in outside conditions.
The recovery in the exchanges con
tinued with demand sterling up to
3.46 by the end of the lirst hour.
This with the continued improve
ment in the commodity markets and
the definite casing of call money made
up a combination of incidents which
was very reassuring.
Trading was not particularly active
and much of the buying came again
from the short interest. But there
was also a quiet absorption of stocks
in a cautious way by people who had
sold out higher un and had been
waiting for the first signs x>f a turn
to get in again. The minor oils,
which had been the original leaders
of the decline a month ago. were the
logical leaders of the recovery.
Strrl Advance More Moderate.
The short interest in Middle States
Oil appeared to be having its trou
bles and, with this stock up nearly
20 points frojn its recent low. n'ti'-e
buying was drawn into Transconti
nental Oil. Sinclair, Union Oil and
others of the same group.
The advance in the Steels was more
moderate, but the rebound was ex
tremely rapid in stocks like Baldwin
locomotive. Crucible Stc-l. St'sde
baker. Marine Preferred and Ameri
can international, which had been
most under pressure and had tln-re
fore accumulated the largest short
account. Again, stocks in which Ix>n
don is interested, like Royal Dutch
and Shell Transport, were conspicu
ous.
Think Worst Over.
There was a tendency in Wall street
brokerage opinion this morning to
feel that while the rally in the mar
ket which started yesterday had been
very pleasing that it had not yet
run its course and that a further
upturn in prices could be looked for^
However, there was one house which
did not change position and even with
what most people figure as the turn
for the better having arrived this
firm stayed by Its guns and was still
advising its customers to sell stocks.
Another firm said: "Yesterday's ral
ly in prices was indeed very Impres
sive and as the short interest is large
the recovery should extend further
today. It is beginning 10 look as If
the worst has been seen for the pres
ent. Stocks are now so low that we
believe such recessions as come from
time to time should be availed of for the
purchase of stocks."
Rally Mot Far Enough.
Another of the writers of brokerage
opinion, who goes more thoroughly
into the market situation, has the fol
lowing to say: "The market rally yes
terday was good so far as it went, but
It did not go far enough after the
size of the break. We think it will go
farther today. Bears have made a lot
of money, most of them have not col-,
lected it, although the big ones have
and are reported long of stocks, and
with profits and nerve left they are
not apt to be disturbed by a five-point
rally ill leading stocks. They will be
tempted probably to sell more stocks,
which -will make up for irregularity
to the recovery, and they are apt to
accelerate it by covering when lead
ing speculative issues are up ten
points rather than five. After the
March. 1907, break the full recovery
came in two days. In 1917 it took two
weeks, but, whether two days or two
weeks, the recovery should go consid
erably farther."

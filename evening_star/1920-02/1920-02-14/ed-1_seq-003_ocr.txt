WILL TREAT EX-SERVICE MEN
Hospitals to Receive Them Under
War Department Orders.
A more liberal policy for the hos
pital treatment of discharged sol
diers. sailors and marines has been
adopted by the War Department.
Under the law they are civilians and
usually are not admitted to Army
hospitals for treatment except in
cases of emergency, like any citizen
not in the public service might be.
Under the war risk insurance act
any soldier, sailor or marine honor
ably discharged since April 6, 1917,
who sufTers from disability contract
ed in the service, is entitled to rea
sonable treatment on account of the
war risk insurance bureau, in either
a public health service hospital, an
Army hospital (if there is room for
him) or in a local civilian hospital.
Orders have now been issued for
the immediate reception of such pa
tients at Army hospitals on presenta
tion of an honorable discharge and
information showing that their exist
ing sickness was due to or aggra
vated by military service, without
awaiting the usual routine of action
on applications by the war risk In
surance bureau.

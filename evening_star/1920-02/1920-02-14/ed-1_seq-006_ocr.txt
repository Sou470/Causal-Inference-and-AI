No Night School Funds.
An effort to keep the night schools
open pending the completion of the
appropriation of the deficiency fund
needed to continue their operation
has failed, because the District Com
missioners are prevented by the law
from anticipating appropriations,
however assured they may be. This
is most unfortunate. It was hoped
that the deficiency bill would have
become a law in season to. keep the
schools in continuous session, and,
when that hope faded, that a way
might be found' to prevent closing
next Monday, in view of the virtual
certainty that the pending bill will
contain the item of $26,000 required.
Now, unless the unexpected happens
and the Senate passes the bill and the
House accepts the Senate's amend
ments and the measure is rushed to
signature by Monday night, the schools
will then close, with the exception of
that at the Business High School,
which, owing to a difference in sched
ule, can run until Tuesday night.
The assurance that the funds al
lotted to the night schools were in
adequate was given to Congress some
weeks ago. It was known, in faet,
before then that the funds were in
sufficient. So great a cut had been
made in the estimates when the cur
rent appropriation bill was enacted
that it was practically certain when
that bill became a law that ttie night
schools could not complete the year's
work. The enrollment was heavy and
required the employment of the maxi
mum number of teachers. Economies
were impossible. The ease simply
stands at this, that Congress did not
grant enough money to do the work
that was plainly in sight to be done,
and the deficiency was assured from
the start.
It will be calamitous to permit the
night schools to close abruptly in the
middle of the season. Yet there seems
now to be no escape. That they will1
resume work when the deficiency fund
is available is some consolation, but
it does not lessen the loss that will be
suffered through a suspension, per
haps of two or three weeks. The best
that can be done is to regard this
stoppage of the school work in the
light of a vacation, and to hope that
the students will be able to pick up
their tasks upon the resumption of
the sessions, without impairment. In
asmuch as these students are going to
night school in preparation for active
work in broader fields than those they
now occupy, this vacation will repre
sent a money loss to them, which,
however, must be borne philosophical
ly as one of the incidentals of an im
perfect system of public appropria
tion.

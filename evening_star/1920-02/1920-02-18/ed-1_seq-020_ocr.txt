TOMB OF WASHINGTON
The Southern Society of Washing
ton will observe the 188th anniversa
ry of the birth of George Washington
by a celebration at the tomb of the first
President at Mount Vernon at 3
o'clock Monday afternoon.

rangements consists of the past
presidents of the society. Gen. Wil
liam C. Gorgas, Claude N. Bennett and
C. C. Calhoun. By permission of the
regent of the Mount Vernon Ladies'
Association, the society will place a
wreath on Washington's tomb. Sev
eral addresses of tribute and devotion
will be made.
Those who are to go by automobile
will meet on the Ellipse at 2 o'clock
in preparation for immediate start.
Menfoers are urged to bring visitors
and to extend the invitation to the
public generally. Several trains will
leave on the Washington-Virginia
railway at 12th and E streets between
1 and 2 o'clock.
Motion pictures will be made of the
society at Mt. Vernon.

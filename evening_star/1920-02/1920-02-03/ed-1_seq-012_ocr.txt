EVENING OF FOLK SONGS
AT CENTRAL HIGH SCHOOL
Society of Fine Arts Sponsors Pro
gram by Walter L. Bogert,
Baritone.
The Washington Society of the
Fine Arts gave its attention to fttk
songs and art songs last night at
the Central High School, with "Walter
L. Bogert. baritone, of New York as
genia! and well qualified expositor.
Mr. Bogert drew from Irish, French,
Russian, Hungarian and Scotch
sources in making out the first half of
his program, and secured a marked
variety of style. The high lights of
the seriae were perhaps the Greek lul
laby on an exotic oriental scale, with
an augumented step and the Hunga
rian song entitled "Far and High," a
version of the stirring melody made
famous by L,iszt in more than one of
his compositions.
Mr. Bogert prefaced his singing
with a few judicious remarks in defi
nition of folk songs in general and in
elucidation of certain of the selected
examples, and showed his versatility
by presenting the Greek and Russian
songs in their original language, as
well as by accompanying himself at
the piano in a happy and effective
manner.
The closing portion of the program
was devoted tc art songs, and here,
as if to compensate for having roamed
so far afield ill the folk music, the
singer confined himself to songs by
American composers or by their Brit
ish ujusins. "Now Sleeps the Crimson
Petin" received a particularly warm
welcome. Mr. Bogert's singing was
of fine quality throughout the even-
ing, the phrasing sustained on an j
excellent breath control and the dic
tion clear and spirited.

The Flu

Last year I bad the well
known flu. and now I have the
flu once more: 1 lie in bed and i
say, "Kerchoo," and other words
I have in store. My dome of
thought is full of ache, and pains
through all my system wend,
and busy druggists' strive to
make the pills the sawbones
recommend. 'Twas war that
brought this punk disease that
threatens now my useful life; I
and Wilhclm, with his war lord
wheeze, was at the bottom of
the strife. 1 cannot think of any
ill in all this sorry world so wide,
that can't be charged to old ex
Bill?and now they say he won't
be tried. Ah, woe is me! This
tinhorn sport won't reap the
punishment that's due; he'll not
appear in any court for having
ushered in the flu. If I should
spring some punk disease that
made your pills of no avail, how
long before the cops would seize
my frame and put the same in
jail? This lack of justice makes
me blue, my anguished breast
with wrath it fills, and so I cry,
"Kerswoosh! Kerchoo!" and
take another mess of pills.
WALT MASON.

WM. E. FOWLER BUYS
"TUFTON" IN VIRGINIA
Pays $155,000 for Property Once
Part of Jefferson's Estate,
"Monticello."'
William E. Fowler of the banking
firm of William E. Fowler Co., of
this city, has purchased the old Vir
ginia estate ki\own as Tufton. near
Charlottesville, it was announced to
day, the price being about $155,000.
Mr. Fowler said today he intends to
make the place his summer home.
Tufton once was a part of Thomas
Jefferson's estate, Monticello, and is
located in a small valley at the foot
of the eminence on which the latter
place stands.
It contains about 763 acres, of which
about 600 acres are under cultivation.
The house, containing ten rooms, was
built about 1838, the old stone kitchen,
a detached structure, having been
built by Jefferson at an earlier date.
The kitchen is almost completely cov
ered with a dense growth of luxuriant
old English ivy. The house stands
on rising ground about 150 feet above
the Rivanna river. Mr. Fowler said
today his purchase included all live
stock on the place. Extensive, altera
tions and improvements are to be
made to modernize the house and
grounds, it was stated.
COLLEGE OF FORESTRY
OFFERS NEW COURSES
Dry Kiln Engineering' and Similar
Grading Subjects of
Study.
The importance of dry kiln en
gineering in conection with forestry
instruction is realized by the New
York State College of Forestry at
Syracuse, and a course in this subjert
is offered to begin March 1. A recent
graduate of the College of Forestry
who specialized in dry kiln work has
been given a job paying $7,200 a year
in India on a three-year contract.
Another course to be offered for the
same term at Syracuse is to give spe
cial training in timber grading. This
is becoming of increasing importance
with the high prices of lumber now
existing, for the timber grader is able
to save his employer large sums by a
technical knowledge of various kinds
and grades of lumber. The applica
tions so far received for these courses
come from men now engaged in the
lumber industry, and their employers
are paying the expenses of those who
already registered for the two courses.

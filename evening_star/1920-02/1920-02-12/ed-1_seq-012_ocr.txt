Rely on Common Sense in
Reconciling Disagreement
Within and Without.
Two major difficulties face the joint
congressional commission on reclassifi
cation of salaries in the District in
fixing salaries for the 107.000 federal
employes in the National Capital.
Difference of opinion among the
commissioners themselves as to proper
salaries is the first difficulty. The sec
ond is the natural trouble experienced
in-endeavoring to steer a fair course
between those in Congress who may
think salary recommendations too high
and those among the employes who
may regard recommendations as too
low.
Common sense, however, is being
used largely by the commissioners in
their endeavors to get at the salary
schedules for the 1.700 classes of em
ployes". and it is declared that neither
difficulty noted will be at all insur
mountable.
Common s??p netted On.
"When we get down to brass tacks
on the salary problems, as we are do
iuu now." said a commissioner today,
"we find that irood, old common sense
is our best reliance, after all."
Salary schedules being worked out
each day by the commissioners in
session at the Capitol are only tenta
tive to date. Thus divergences of
opinion will be allowed for, and when
all the schedules are thus set up
the commissioners will go back over
them and "pull them together" for
the standpoint of "relativity."
"Relativity," as used in scientific
classification, simply means equity as
between classes. Thus, endeavor will
be made, anil is being: made. not. only
to give proportionate salaries to the
various workers in a common line of
work, but also to give one class of
workers, such a? school teachers, fair
salaries in relation to equally fair
salaries to stenographers.

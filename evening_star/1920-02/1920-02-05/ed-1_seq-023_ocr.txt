OYSTER DISHES.
Creamed Oysters.
Creamed oysters offer a great deal
of nourishment, for in addition to the
oysters there is nourishment of the
milk or cream, the butter and the
flour. This is an especially good dish
to give any one who is overtired or
any one who needs extra nutrition
that can easily be assimilated. Try
it when you are fagged out and see
if it does not make a new woman of
you. Make a thin, white sauce after
the regular recipe?two tablespoons
butter, two tablespoons of flour to a
cup of milk or cream, according to
the desired richness, using even
tablespoons.
Put the butter in a saucepan and
melt; stir in the flour and then add
the milk, stirring all the time until
the mixture is smooth and creamy.
Season with one-quarter teaspoon of
salt and a litle pepper. Add a little
chopped parsley. Clean as many oys
ters as you want to eat and cook
them in their own liquor until they
are plump and beginning to curl. Utt
them out of the pan into the hot
white sauce and serve on pieces of
toast. The above proportion of white
sauce is sufficient for a pint of oysters.
One good point about cooking oysters
in this way is that you can make
the white sauce a day or two before
you want to use it. Then reheat it
while the oysters are being cooked.
Sralloped Oysters.
There arc a number of good ways
of scalloping oysters. Perhaps this
one may be new to you. Proceed just
as for regular scalloped oysters, but.
instead of bread or cracker crumbs,
use tqacaroni (three-quartets of a
<mp of one-inch pieces to one pine of
oysters) which has been cooked until
tender in salted water, drained and
rinsed with cold water. Do not make
more than two layers of the maca
roni and the oysters and sprinkle
crumbs an?J butter over the top. Add
the liquor from the oysters or a cup
of milk and bake half an hour.
Oyster Pie.
Twenty-five oysters, one cup of
finely minced celery, one cup of
finely diced potatoes, one-half cup of
oyster liquor, one cup of milk, two
tablespoons of flour, one teaspoon of
finely minced parsley, salt and pepper
to taste.
Place a layer of potatoes and cel
ery in baking dish, then a layer of
oysters, lastly a layer of potatoes and
celery; make a sauce with butter,
flour and milk and oyster liquor, add j
seasoning and parsley, pour in dish
and cover with crust made of pie
pastry, bake in hot oven for forty
five minutes.
Oysters Roasted la Shell.
Oysters roasted in the shell are
fine, provided the oysters are fine,
too. Scrub the shells well with a
brush and cold water; place in a pan
with the deep half of the shell down.
Put in a hot oven and serve oysters
in the deep half, sprinkling with salt
and pepper and placing a bit of but
ter on each oyster. Leave them in
the oven only until they are opened
and thoroughly hot.

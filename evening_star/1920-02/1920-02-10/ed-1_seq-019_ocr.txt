WILL ASK COURT RULING
ON LIQUOR DISPOSITION
IT. S. District Attorney Laskey
Wants to Know What to Do
Regarding: Seizures.
The District Supreme Court will
shortly be asked by United States At
torney Laskey to pass on the ques
tion of the disposition of a large
quantity of liquor held by the police.
After a conference with the Depart
ment of Justice Assistant United
States Attorney Mason, who is in
charge of the subject, is considering
today just what method to pursue in
bringing it before the court.
"Of course there is nothing to pre
vent claimants to any portion of this
liquor," said Mr. Mason, "beginning
replevin proceedings for the return
of the "wet" goods, but there are so
many different phases of the matter
that We are trying to shape up a
proposition that may dispose of the
entire matter. As soon as we reach
a decision we will not delay in- plac
ing it before the court."
The chief difficulty that stands in
the way of th? return of the intox
icants to former owners is said to be
the provision of the Volstead enforce
ment act which would seem to pro
vide for the destruction or sale of any
liquor seised in any proceedings for
violation of law or where owners have
not filed claim to the property within
sixty days of the passage of the act.
Edwin B. Hesse, property clerk of
the police department, will hold on
to the whisky in his possession until
the court has heard and determined
the question to be raised by Mr. Las
key or until a deputy marshal, under
replevin proceedings, shall take the
liquor out of his custody.

raised dots.

she transcribed to Braille work.

LEXINGTON, Ky., February 10.?
Dawn today found Lexington an arm
ed camp.
Squads of armed soldiers who saw
service at Chateau Thierry, were pa
trolling the streets or guarding all
approaches to the city to prevent a
recurrence of yesterday's bloody mob
.violence that cost the lives of five
persons and caused injury to a score
of others.
"W illiam Lockett, negro slayer of
ten-year-old Geneva Hardman. a
schoolgirl, was confined in a steel
cage in the Fayette county court
house here, awaiting removal either
to the state reformatory at Frank
fort or to the prison nr Jiddyville.
Reports last night that 1,500 moun
taineers were en route to Lexington
to lynch Lockett caused the com
manding officer to throw cordons of
troops across every road leading into
the city, and to place otner detach
ments at every strategic point within
the city. So extensive were the plans
to prevent a further uprising that at
10 o clock last night the brigadier
general announced that the situation
was under control and that no fur
ther trouble was expected.
Gen. Marshall announced during the
forenoon that the situation was eo
well in hand that about 200 men of
the 1st Division would be returned to
Camp Taylor. Arrangements were
made to move them late today. The
seventy-Bye members of the state
troops which repulsed the mob yester
day also were preparing to entrain
Tor Latonia.
Another interesting announcement
during- the forenoon was that of Com
monwealth's Attorney John It. Allen
that no prosecution of members of the
mob was contemplated by the author
ities.
Protection tor Prlaoner.
No attempt will be made by the au
thorities to remove Lockett from the
steel cage until danger of further
violence has passed, it was an
nounced.
Hrig. Gen. Marshall recommended
today that Lockett be kept in Lex
ington until it was entfrely safe to
take him away, either to Frankfort
or to Eddyville prison. That, he said,
might be within twenty-four hours
a ,week- Plans were formulated
this forenoon by the military au
thorities to maintain custody of the
negro.
Gen. Marshall reiterated this fore
noon that he had sufficient force to
cope with any disorder, but said he
regarded possibility of further riot
ing remote. Squads of soldiers, on
duty all night, reported to military
headquarters this morning that there
had been not the slightest sign of
disorder since patrols were estab
lished.
It was an attempt to lynch I,ockett
yesterday that caused the rioting,
t'nder guard of several hundred state
troops, the negro was brought from
the state penitentiary to this city,
matched to the courthouse, found
guilty of murder and sentenced to
die in the electric chair March 11.
Shortly after sentence had been
passed a mob of 100 or more men
charged the entrance to the court
house. bent upon lynching the negro.
The troops fired rifles and machine
guns into the crowd. The mob wa
vered and then dispersed, leaving the

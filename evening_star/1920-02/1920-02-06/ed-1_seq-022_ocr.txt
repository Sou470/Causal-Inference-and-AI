During this survey transcripts of
the pay rolls were obtained for the
week or half month preceding the
visit from 153 establishments. Of
these 46 per cent were in hotels, 48
per cent were in restaurants, 6 per
cent were in hospitals and 0.6 per cent
were in apartment houses.
Three Factor* Influence.
Three important characteristics ofi
the hotel and restaurant business
were found to influence the money
wage received by woman employes.
First among these is the unsteadiness
of employment. Of the 153 women on
the pay rolls, 68 or 44 per cent worked
less than a full month. Of the 127
women on the pay rolls of two other
hotels 49 or 30 per cent worked less
than a full half month. "Assuming
that the same number in this latter
group would have failed to work the
full pay period in the other half
month, 38 or 77 per cent of this
group would have worked less than a
full month." says a statement issued
by the department.
"Such irregular attendance and con
stant shifting from job to job makes
earnings considerably less than rates
of pay. and this difference must be
borne in mind in considering the
analysis of the wage rates."
Another factor influencing wages is
the tips received by maids, elevator

IN AMERICANIZATION
Secretary Olson of Missionary Asso
ciation Hakes An
nouncement.
Plans for a $25,000,000 campaign to
educate foreign emigrants to the
United States in the principles of
Americanism were announced today
by David E. Olson, secretary of the
International Christian Missionary
Association, who is here to inaugurate
the drive in the east.
At present the institution has build
ings erected or in the course of erec
tion at Minneapolis, Minn.; Seattle.
Wash.; Eugene, Ore.; San Francisco.
Calif., and St. Louis, Mo.
With the proceeds from the cam
paign it is intended to equip and es
tablish schools for foreigners at vir
tually every large port of entry in
the United States. The plans com
prise the addition of New York city.
New Orleans and Quebec. Canada, to
the cities already housing colleges
for foreigners with the purpose of
educating them in the system of gov
ernment of this country. Seven thou
sand foreigners would be able to re
ceive an education annually if the
campaign is successful, Mr. Olson said.
The schools would be conducted
along Christian lines, but would ba
non-sectarian. The Bible would be
taught to the students, with other les
sons in English language and gram
mar, history and geography.
The present plan'is to give the en
trants a work day of four hours, and
in return a period of study and board
and rooms. In this manner those
reaching the country illiterate would
be able to go out into the business
world with y*aflicient education to
guarantee immovability against the
lectures of the radical socialists and
bolshevisits.
Mr. Olson stated that 75 per eent of
the industries of the United States
were in the control of emigrants or
aliens, and that if every one of these
left his position at the same time as
his fellows, the country would face a
situation where the industries of the
country would not only be crippled,
but paralyzed.
"The country is conducting its
playhouses on smoldering volca
noes." declared Mr. Olson today. "The
situation is admittedly serious, but
will not be recognized until the
American people are brought face to
face with the results. The emigrant

am an emigrant myself. I love this
country and I want every emigrant
coming here to have at least as good
a chance and, if possible, more of a
chance as myself."

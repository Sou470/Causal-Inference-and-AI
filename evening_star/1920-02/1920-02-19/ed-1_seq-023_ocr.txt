Beef for the Invalid.
It is essential for the person who
has to care for any invaJid to know
how to secuVe raw beef juice. Take
a small piece of rump or upper round
and warm this slightly. Sear over
both sides under the broiler or in a
very hot frying pan. This will start
the juice. Then squeeze this juice out
through a beef press, or. if this is not
at hand, in a potato ricer or lemon
squeezer. Stand the cup containing
the juice in warm water. Add a little
salt and serve at once.
To scrap" beef, take the same qual
ity of beef and scrape with a sharp
knife, cutting out the red part of the
meat and leaving the white fiber,
which sometimes is difficult for a
weak stomach to digest. Make a
small oat of this red, scraped beef,
broil the pat and serve on a square
of toast, garnished with a little pars
ley. celery, cress, lettuce or toast
points, the object being to make the
beef, attractive.
]f the invalid must have raw beef
a good way of preparing it is to cut
slices of bread very thin, spread but
ter over and a little lettuce, then add a
little raw scraped meat on top of the
lettuce, put on another slice after
seasoning with butter, salt and pep
per, and cut the sandwiches into
dainty shapes.

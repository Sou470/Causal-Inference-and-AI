LOCAL POLICE LAUNCH
NATIONAL ASSOCIATION;

Organization Formed Here Will Be
Entirely Independent of Old
Labor Affiliations.
With the permanent organization of
members of the local police force ef
fected at a meeting- of the National
Association of Police, Local, No. 1, of
the District, the nucleus of what it is
hoped will become a nation-wide po
licemen's organization was shaped last
night.
A six-day week, an eight-hour day.
discontinuance of reserve duty and
the fixation of regular sick leave and
annual vacation are sought. Among
other objects are the placing of mem-|
bers of the force upon their semi-mili
tary basis, thus eliminating them from
classification with civil employes; the
continuance of the present bonus and
other reforms for the betterment of
the service.
Oldrtra Chosen.
Officers elected were: C. A. Strobel.
No. 9. president: D. R. Howsley, No. 6,
vice president; C. C. Wise, No. 7, finan
cial secretary; C. H. Warder, No. 9, re
cording secretary; J. H. Lee. No. 1,
treasurer. A committee on legislation
and a board of trustees will be named
at the next meeting. Permanent head
quarters have been established at 430
9th street.
The organization is the remnant of
the original policemen's union, which
held affiliation with the Central La
bor Union and the American Federa
tion of Labor, and was disbanded
when the Commissioners and Congress
raised the objection that policemen
should not as a matter of public pol
icy be connected'with a labor organ
ization.
Open Mfftinn Ordered.
Announcement was made that the
new organization is not a labor union
and is not affiliated with organized
labor, directly or indirectly. This
point is emphasized strongly by the
promoters. Meetings will be held on
the first and third Tuesdays of each
month and will be open to the Dis
trict Commissioners, the superin
tendent of police and the interested
public.
A special effort will be made to or
ganize similar local associations
among policemen of all large cities.
The secretary reported 250 paid-up
members with eighteen members
ready to take the obligation.

D. C. and W. R. E. Exonerated in
Death of J. William Shaffer
in Traffic Accident.
The District of Columbia and the
Washington Railway and Electric
Company were exonerated yesterday
by a jury in Circuit Division 1 before
Justice Hitz of responsibility for the
death of J. William Shaffer, October 2,
1916. The jury returned a verdict in
favor of the defendants in a suit to
recover $10,000 damages, brought by
Gertrude Shaffer, as administratrix of
her husband's estate.
Theaccident occurred on the Bla
densburg road at the District line,
where the railroad trolley wire poles
are placed in the middle of the road.
An automobile, in which Shaffer was
riding, collided with the pole throw
ing the passenger to the roadway
and killing him.
The plaintiff charged negligence in
having the pole in the center.of the
roadway not sufficiently lighted. The
defendants claimed the pole was
placed in accordance with an act of
Congress chartering the railway com
pany and asserted that the accident
happened from negligence of the
driver of the automobile.

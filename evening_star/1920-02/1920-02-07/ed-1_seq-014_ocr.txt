UNION WAGE SCALES
SHOW BIG INCREASES
Union wage scales in the general
trades in 1910 averaged 55 per cent
higher than in 1913; 17 per cent higher
than in 1918, and 72 per cent higher
than in 1907, according to a summary
prepared by the bureau of labor sta
tistics.
The regular hours of labor per week
in 1919 were S per cent less than Jn
1918 and 8 per cent less than in 1907,
the summary says. The 1919 regular full
time weekly earnings, under these rates,
were 48 per cent above 1913 and 61 per
cent above 1907.
The figures apply only to persons em
ployed at time rates and not to piece
workers, and include the bakery, build
ing, stone, metal, mill woodwork and
printing trades, and also chauffeur* and
teamsters, freight handlers, laundry
workers, theater hands and waiters.

To successfully raise flowers from
bulbs your first step is to buy your
bulbs from a reliable establishment.
Place Easter lily bulbs In low, broad
pots, put plenty of charcoal at the
bottom of the pots, and cover the
bulbs with sifted coal ashes and
rough, well dried manure; water
sparingly. These bulbs are somewhat
subject to insect attacks, thus differ
ing from many other bulbs. Watch
them closely, and, if necessary, use
tobacco dust or one of the prepara
tions for bug extermination. The
bulbs of these lilies endure a much
higher temperature when brought to
the light than other bulbs. There are
people living in apartments who keep
lily bulbs under their beds in a cool
room, protected from the light with
newspapers and a lin^d valahcve.
Plant lily of the valley bulbs very
fclosely. letting the tips peep out.
They like either leaf mold or plain
coarse sand. After planting, water
thoroughly with warm water or a
liquid fertilizer. Put them immedi
ately in a very warm, dark place and
remove to the light when the flowers
begin to form, which will be in about
three weeks.
Hyacinths may be given liquid fer
tilizer two or three times a week
after they are brought to the light.
Other bulbs may be given a fertilizer
only once a week. After the blooms
are thoroughly formed on hyacinths
they will not need a fertilizer. Hya
cinths can also be raised in water in
bowls or hyacinth glasses. Pebbles
and charcoal should be put in the bowl
to support the bulb, and the bowl
should be filled to the base of the
bulb with warm soft water, and then
placed in a dry dark closet until the roots
have made a big growth. If the water
in the glass becomes foul or dimin
ished, replace it with tepid water.
When well started the bulbs should
be brought gradually to the direct
sunlight, where they should have
plenty of fresh air without being
draft.
A new way to grow a hyacinth is to
place the bulb in the center of a good
sized sponge, set it in a pretty dish
and keep the sponge wet constantly.
After the bulb is well started, scatter
some grass seed over the surface of
the sponge.
When your bulbs have stopped
blooming, put them into a cool, dark
place and water them occasionally,
just enough to keep them from dying,
until the leaves dry up. Once ih two
weeks is sufficient. In the autumn
these bulbs may be planted outdoors,
and in time will bloom again, but
they cannot be forced another year.

Athletic authoriites at the Univer
sity of Virginia are to meet tomorrow
night at Charlottesville for a discus
sion of the future policy in intercol
legiate sports. The meeting is to take
the form of a general debate, in which
all opinions are to be aired and dis
cussed pro and con.
Specific nature of the matters to be
taken up have not been made known.
but it is recognized that the alumni
have been bringing powerful pres
sure to bear on the authorities at
Charlottesville to bring about certain
definite changes, one of which is to
adopt the professional coaching sys
tem to take the place of the present
graduate system. Also considerable
sentiment has been expressed against
the first-year rule, it being stated that
Virginia should not handicap itself by
holding to a rule which is not en
forced by most of its opponents.
Just what will be done at Virginia
is not known, but there have been
rumors time and again of proposed
changes in policy, but none has ac
tually been productive of real results.

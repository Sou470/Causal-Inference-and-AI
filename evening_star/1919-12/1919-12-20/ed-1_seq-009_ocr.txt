in Washington a blind bowler.

in Masonic Temple, is totally blind,

sense of sound and feeling- He read

almost impossible by taking to bowl
ing. Forman uses his cane to place

left, measuring the distance to get
as near as possible the center of the
alley.
For a man so handicapped he has
excellent control of the ball. Of
course he rolls some balls down the
gutter, but people with two good eyes
do that. Forman rolls a fairly good
game of duck pins, averaging along
about SO. but he has matie as high as
two spares in one gatne. for a total
of 103. After the first ball is rofled
some one must call the numbers of the
pins left up. so he may judge the di
rection.
When it is realized that a member
of a local league team recently rolled
a game of twenty-one with two good
eyes wide open, and Forman rolled 103
with both eyes entirely closed, it will
be well recogmxed that both performed

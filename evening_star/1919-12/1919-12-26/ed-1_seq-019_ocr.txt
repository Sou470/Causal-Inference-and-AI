are now three large triangles and two
smaller ones. Take two of the largo
pieces to form the bottom of the cor
set cover, with the other large piece
fitting at the center of the top. Place
a small piece at each side. Cut a
paper pattern first and put together
and you will instantly see how it goes, j

oblong piece when finished. Edge the

To Make Handkerchief*.
It is good sewing experience for a
girl to make her own handkerchiefs.
All young girls love pretty handker
chiefs and it is possible to have twice
as many if they are made at home.
Making handkerchiefs is good pick-up
work for any girl and is interesting.
Neat patterns are of very sheer ma
terial delicately turned on the edges
and plain hemstitched or trimmed
with very narrow lace. Colors* either
In polka" dots or as narrow borders,
are used on most of the new handker
chiefs. Some have colored centers
with white polka dots and others have
colored 4ots on the white centers with
a very narrow border in color. Some
others have tiny scallops, edged in
blue, pink, lavender or green and still
others have a sprig of flowers em
broidered in one corner as well as the
scalloped edge. Handkerchief linen is
obtainable in a variety of pretty
shades, which are very attractive
when set off by a finishing touch in a
darker shade.
Fine sheer handkerchiefs are easily
made. Instead of hemming the edges,
they should be rolled French fashion.
To do this, dampen the thumb and
first finger of the left hand and roll
as finely as possible as you continue
to sew. The lace edging or insertion
that you trim with may be sewed on
with "the same stitch that sews the
roll. In other words, the roll and
edging are sewed at the same time.
This makes a prettier, more delicate
finish, than a hem, as it is almost in
visible. Such handkerchiefs may be
trimmed as simply or as elaborately
as you wish. These handkerchiefs,
with the rolled edges may be whipped
around and back again with colored
thread to form tiny crosses for a bor
der and further ornamented by hem
stitching worked in a deeper color,
inserted parallel to the edge. Two
iin<-s, or double hemstitching, may be
inserted parallel ir you wish to give
more color and character to the de
sign. A simple initialing in color at
'one corner may finish the handker
chief.
To make one dozen gentlemen's
handkerchiefs requires three yards
of linen, one spool of thread and
two skeins of embroidery silk. Hem
stitch them by hand and work an
initial in one corner of each.
When washing delicate handker
chiefs, especially if they are turning
yellow, put a couple of drops of
peroxide of hydrogen in the rinsing
water. This is the method that is
used bv cotton manufacturers to
bleach cotton cloth to a pure white
from the raw state, and is therefore
harmless. Another method is to fJet
them soak overnight in water in
which a little cream of tartar lias
been dissolved. Instead of dampen
ing handkerchiefs in the usual way
before ironing, try this method: In
two quarts of tepid water put five
drops of bluing and a small piece of
lump starch. Pour in a basin. In
this mixture dip each handkerchief
separately, thoroughly wetting it and
then squeezing it as dry as possible.
When all the handkerchiefs have been
treated in this way spread them out
smoothly on a clean cloth or towel
until they can bo ironed.

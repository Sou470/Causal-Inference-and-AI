BY PREMIER NITTI
Denies Absurd Reports, Such
as President Being Head
of Capitalistic Plot.
BY F.DG?R A\SKI. MOWUKR.

Chicago Daily New*. Copyright, 1918.
ROME, December 22 (delayed).?
The inopportune republican demon
stration produced in the chamber
of deputies Saturday by the speech
of the socialist Sig. Modiglianin at
tacking the sovereign and calling for
the immediate realization of a re
public saved the Nitti ministry
by a small majority. Yesterday when
it seemed that nothing -could do, so
the so-called constitutional parties,
backed by the Catholics for once,
overcame personal SQuabbles and am
bitions and rallied around the mon
archy. The institution was saved
largely through the personal mas
tery of Premier Nitti over parlia
mentary psychology.
Speech Full of Common Senae.
Foreign Minister Scialoja's exposi
tion of failure Saturday weakened the
government's position and Premier
Nitti failed to help it much yester
day. Yet liis speech was fun of
common sense. He defended the al
lies and especially the United States
from the absurd reports which made
President Wilson the head of a vast
capitalistic plot to gain possession
of the eastern Adriatic for American
financiers. M. Nitti said indeed and
truly that the United States often
erred in judging of the political sit
uation on the Adriatic, but defended
the motives which made President
Wilson veto the Italian schemes.
Premier Nitti took credit for having
avoided shedding Italian blood over
Fiume. He denied that the possession
of Fiume was an Italian war aim, and
declared that Italy would be victorious
even if Fiume should be abandoned.
Nevertheless, he said Fiume remained
an aspiration dear to Italian hearts.
The premier confirmed the stories con
cerning the increasing desire of the
Fiumani to accept the government's
point of view. Four-fifths of the popu
lation voted last Thursday to accept
government control. The government
pledged itself not to reduce the claims
expressed in the so-called Tittoni
schemes. Ijeut. Col. d'Annunzio. with
the minority, may still refuse to accept
the plebiscite, but his situation is criti
cal.
Kpoch In Italian Democracy.
Yesterday, moreover, marks an epoch
in the history of Italian democracy.
Not only was the monarchy saved by
a small vote, but the premier himself
promised to present a bill transferring
the constitutional power of declaring
war and peace from the sovereign to
parliament. Two other motions for the
immediate abolition of the censorship
and the publication of war documents
were rejected by small majorities. Never
before has democracy come so near
prevailing in Italy. Premier Nitti for
mally promised to abolish the censor
ship when the Fiume question was set
tled. and expressed hope, more or less
sanguine, that the allies could be per
suaded to accept the Tittoni project
without American consent. The gov
ernment. he said, was firmly determined
to reach a solution.

Associated Press).?Police and county
authorities are scouring the country

criminals who were freed in a jail de

shot early yesterday morning.
Detectives today began searching
through a number of cottages at Point
Place, a suburb, where the men are
thought to have hidden themselves
after being taken there in an automo
bile by their deliverers.
The men at large are Kdward Mee
han, alias D. W. West, safeblower and
convicted murderer; Frank Whitey
Howard, alias Albert Johnson, safe- i
blower; Albert Loach, silk robber and
burglar, and Leo Mitchell, held on a

Four men are under arrest suspected
of being concerned in the delivery.
Two of them were taken a few hours
later returning from the Point Place
district in an automobile. Two others
were picked up in the underworld on a
I tip that they were going to the city
prison to release the others arrested.
The four are held under heavy gua.nl,
as the police expect an attempt to res
cue them.

arrested here for the murder of James j
Manion in a resort, was found guilty of
manslaughter and sentenced to twenty
years.
Loach was arrested here September
10 last for the robbery of a silk store.
Mitchell has served several terms in
prisons in different parts of the coun

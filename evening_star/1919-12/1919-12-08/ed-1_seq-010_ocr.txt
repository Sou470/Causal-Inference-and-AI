PRESBYTERY PROPOSES
TRUCE OF STRIKES PLAN
The proposal that a truce of strikes
shall be arranged for the period of
three to six months, with the under-j
standing that the status quo shall not
pe changed to the disadvantage of'
labor in that time, was indorsed today J
by the Presbytery of Washington at 1
a meeting at the New York Avenue
Presbyterian Church. The proposed
truce, as indorsed by the presbytwy,
is for the purpose of providing a
favorable opportunity for national,
state and city government and other
forces to bring down the cost of
living and suppression of profiteering
while production is maintained, and
for the friendly conferences of repre
sentatives of industry and of the
public with a view to establish a more
satisfactory relation of capital and
labor.
The presbytery states in the reso
lution that the proposal was original
ly suggested by leaders of the rail
way brotherhoods, and afterward ap
proved by the employers' group in the
first industrial conference in October.
The resolution will be signed and for
warded to the President and Congress
and also to the industrial peace con
ference. The presbytery urges that
other religious and industrial and pa
triotic bodies follow its example in
approving the proposal.

end to the present coal shortage.

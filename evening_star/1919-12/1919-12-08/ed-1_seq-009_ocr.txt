1. Anybody can improve the personal

2. The elements of the p. a. are car

3. The p. a. can be improved by (1)
Intelligence, which means finding out
where you are deficient, and learning

fidence you can succeed, and (3) Per
severance, for what ypu aim at is the
formation of a habit, and habits are of
slow growth.
4. Carriage. Stand straight?head
up, shoulders back, chest out. abdomen
in. Don't slouch. Look alert. Look
people in the eyg. Act a* if you had
confidence in yourself, whether you feel
it or not. Don't look, like an apology.
5. Walk. Don't shuffle. Avoid man
nerisms. Take short steps and stand
straight. Don't walk like a horse.
clumpety-clump, but like a cat.
6. Expression. Think agreeable
thoughts, and they will shine in your
face. Have a colic mind and you will
have a colic face. What you want
charm, and you can have charm by ex
pression even if you have no beauty;
while beauty without expression is?out!
Your face, after all, is but a sort of
inovlriK-picturt- screen; your thoughts

thoughts and feelings that constitute
the charm of your expression.
7. Complexion. The best complexion,
for you, is the one nature gave you.

to improve that is?good health. Diet,
exercise and intelligent attention to
your general health are better than all
the lotionr, creams, powders and salves
in the world.
S. Features. No matter how peculiar
or discordant your features may be,
there is one medicine that will make
them so attractive that everybody will
love them. It is good humor. Pig
eyes, snub nose, loose lips, large ears,
long neck, low forehead?whatever your
deformity, if only good cheer, a genial
soul, fun and a kindly heart are behind
them, they will become agreeable.
9. Clothes. Th$ ideal clothes are

manners is thoughtfulness of others.

ness.
In short, the place to begin the task

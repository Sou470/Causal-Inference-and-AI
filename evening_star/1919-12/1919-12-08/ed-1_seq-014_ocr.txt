BRUSSELS. Belgium, December 4
(delayed).?Other nations besides the
United States desire amendments to
the pact of the league of nations.
The unofficial congress of league so
cieties, which has just finished its
sessions in Brussels, has put forth a
number of resolutions which urge
amendments to the covenant. Tire
Italians demand compulsory arbitra
tion and the British and French to
gether want an international court
of justice to be embodied in the
league on equal terms with the as
sembly and executive council.
The French consider indispensable
the creation of an international mili
tary staff with power to enforce the
decisions of the executive council by
the use of armies, or else, they say,
the council will be flouted right and
left, as is now the experience of the
Paris peace conference and as was
formerly the experience of the peace
tribunal at The Hague. The dele
sates from all the nations unite in
demanding gradual disarmament.
These resolutions show popular dis
content in England. France, Italy and
other countries with the league of
nations as it is now constituted. The
difference between them and the
United States is that they are willing
to sacrifice some of their ideals and
accept tl-.e pact as it stands and strive
for amendments afterward, whereas
the American Senate will accept no
compromise at all. All of America's
demands must be satisfied at once or
she will not enter the league.
Besides adopting these resolutions
the congress of Brussels decided to
constitute itself into a permanent, or
ganization. At periodic intervals here
after a congress will meet in other
capitals of Europe. A general council
will be appointed to regulate tlie ex
ecutive affairs. Here in Brussels a
permanent bureau will be established
w here any one from any-corner of the
world may gather information about
questions relating to the league. I

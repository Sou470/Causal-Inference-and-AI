BILL UP IN THE HOUSE
Prospect for Passage Today of D. C.
Measure Introduced by Chair
man Mapes.
The bill introduced by Chairman
Mapes of the House District commit
tee, at the request of District officials,
to establish standard weights and
measures in the District is being de
bated in the House this afternoon. The
prospects are that the measure will
be passed today.
Representative Gard of Ohio *nd
Representative Blanton of Texas, both
democrats, delayed action on this
measure.
Btu Explained by Mr. Mapea.
Chairman Mapes explained that the
bill is a redraft of the present weights
and measures law for the District with
new provisions to require inspection
and regulation of weighing and meas
uring devices, the inspection of which
is now required, and to establish
standard containers for the sale of
fruit, vegetables, and similar com
modities.
The bill does not create any new
positions and does not change the
salary or status of any employe of the
District government.
The general purpose of the Dill,
Chairman MapeB pointed out, is to
regulate the sale of food and other
commodities in the District so as to
be fair. Just to both vender and pur
chaser by such provisions as will, as
nearly as possible, enable the one to
know exactly the amount he sells
and the other to know the amount
he reoeives.
Safeguards for Public.
Provisions of the bill not now cov
ered by law which will serve to safe
guard the public are: Prohibits short
numerical count; provides specifically
that the sale of commodities by
weight shall be net weight; estab
lishes a standard loaf of bread; pro
vides for the sale of ice by weight
only; establishes standard containers
for the sale of fruits and vegetables;
regulates the sale of coal in small
quantities; regulates the sale of wood;
specially regulates the use of gaso
line measuring pumps; regulates the
sales of oysters and fish; regulates
coin in the slot machines; provides for
delivery of sales tickets with purchase
when demanded; provides punishment
for charge of excess weights on
freight and express; abolishes charge
of fee by the District for inspection
of weighing and measuring devices.

ton. president of the International Trade
Union Federation, and secretary of the
General Federation of Trade Unions in
Great Britain, commenting today on the
international labor congress at Wash
ington, from which he has just re
turned, said:
"Two impressions I held prior to niy
departure for the United States were
confirmed, namely, that the time for
the conference was inopportune, and
that the nations which fought for the
right of self-determination with regard
to their politics, would demand the same
right with respect to industrial matters.
"The holding of the conference while
the United States was still embroiled in
domestic difficulties may account for the
attitude generally adopted toward it,
which was one of indifferent detach
ment, instead of active hostility. It was
alleged by some that Great Britain had
the best of America over the peace
treaty. They feared Great Britain
sought to secure industrial advantages
by precipitating %n industrial conven
tion which might conceivably reflect
upon American methods or handicap

Write The Star Asking Names;
Letters Will Be Turned Over to
Volunteers. Who Will Aid.
Several kind-hearted women of the
city and nearby suburbs have written
to The Star asking if they could bt
furnished with a list of names of
"shut-in" patients at Walter Reed
Hospital to whom they might send
Christmas remembrances, books or
other suitable presents.
There are many boys at the hospital
who have friends or relatives who will
remember them on Christmas day, but
there are several hundred boys who
are far from home and many of them
have no relatives and few friends.
Many come from small towns where
they may have lived but a few years
of their short lives and are very
likely to have been forgotten. For
such as these the Red Cross will do a
splendid work, but there is opportu
nity for other volunteer help. If other
kindly disposed persons wish to make
the day happy for some unknown
young friends they may write to The
Star and their letters will be turned
over to the willing volunteer women
who visit tiie different wards and art*
familiar with the needs and wants of
the various boys.
These women will be only too glad
to supply the names or see to the
proper distribution of presents that
may be sent to the Red Cross or to
The Star office. In other words, they
will see to it that boys who have lost
their legs in France and have not yet
become accustomed to their artificial
feet are not given ice or roller skates,
and that an accordion or banjo is not
given to a boy who has lost a hand.
If with each present there is a per
sonal note or a Christmas card, it will
be all the more appreciated. Who
wants to help?
Enormous War Risk Salaries.
To the Editor of The St?r:
I have noticed repeatedly articles in
your paper in regard to the conditions
and salaries in the war risk bureau,
most of which are misleading to the
public. In almost all the articles men
tion is made of the enormous salaries
paid; in a great many instances $1,800
and $240 bonus to ordinary clerks. It
is well known by the employes of the
bureau, if not by the newspapers and
also some senators and representa
tives in Congress, that a great many
of the employes receive the enormous
sum of $60 per month, some $70 and a
vast number $90. and also that instead
of receiving the $240 bonus about
one-half receive $120 and tlie remain
der none whatever. If any are receiv
ing the $240 there is evidently some
discrimination made by those in pow
er. I am sure the employes of the bu
reau would appreciate the correction
of the erroneous impression given the
public by any such articles that have
recently been brought to their atten
tion through the articles published as
above mentioned.

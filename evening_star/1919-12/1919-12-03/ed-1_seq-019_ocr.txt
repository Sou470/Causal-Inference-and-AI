DROPS OVER 50 PER CENT

Soft coal production throughout the
nation did not reach 50 per cent of
normal last week, according to fig

The pinch of the coal shortage in all re
gions promised, according to officials,
to force adoption of drastic measures to
avert an actual famine.
A month's "vacation" by bituminous
coal miners has brought about con
ditions like those prevailing during
the war, but -war-time restrictions
have not altogether checked the drain
on fuel reserves. Little 'hope was
held out for early production of
enough coal to permit the mainte
nance of the present inadequate re
serves, under supervision of the rail
road administration's central coal
committee.
Although regional coal commit
~e8\*"orki?S under instructions from
Washington, have been allocating coal
sparingly, further and more drastic
restrictions have been agreed upon
by the central committee. These will
be applied whenever regional com

will not be made public just at this

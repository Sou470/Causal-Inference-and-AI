meeting. As remedies for deficiencies

teachers, higher pay, larger congres
sional appropriations, bigger schools,
improved sanitation and hygiene and
better physical instruction.
Representative Alapes of Michigan,
chairman of the House District com
mittee on appropriations, has indorsed
the plan to better the school system.
Mrs. Goldberg says "higher salaries
will make possible the release of over
sixty teachers absolutely unfit for
service, whose place it is wise to fill
with high-grade women. In these days
the especially trained minds are go
ing into fields where they receive
adequate pay."

better conditions by petitioning Con

down Pennsylvania avenue carrying a
banner to advance the cause, "and 1
am sure many other women would
join me," said Mrs. Goldberg.

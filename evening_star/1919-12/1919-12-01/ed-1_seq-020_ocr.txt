Editorial
We wonder if men who
carry canes know that
the custom or habit, or
whatever you call it, did
not come by accident.
It is an idea copied from
the British army, where it
was introduced to keep
the men from putting
their hands in their
pockets.
The scheme worked to
perfection the men
couldn't carry a cane and
keep their hands in their
pockets at the same time,
and, as carrying a cane
was desirable, they kept
their hands out of their
pockets.
It is true that the best
way to break a bad habit
is to substitute one that
is more attractive?more
desirable?more fashion
able.
Just now we are in
need of new industrial
habits to take the place of
those that grew out of
the war. When they are
found we'll get our feet on
solid ground again.

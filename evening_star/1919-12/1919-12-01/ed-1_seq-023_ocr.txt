TO BUILD FIVE COURSES
2,200-Boom Hotel Planned for
American Golf Club on
Long Island.
The most ambitious golf project yet
proposed in this country is to be be
gun shortly near Roslyn, Jx>ng
Island, by a group of New York finan
ciers. It is reported that A. L. Hawse
of Richmond, well known to Wash
ington golfers through his playing in
tourneys here, is one of those con
nected with the project.
On a tract of land 5,000 acres in
extent on Long Island five golf
courses are to be built about a cen
tral hotel and golf club. The name
of the new club will be the American
Golf Club.
Memberships, it is understood, will
be sold on a life basis to 2.500 golfers
at $1,000 each, and it is proposed to
secure an additional 25,000 sustaining
members paying yearly dues. The
hotel will have 2,200 rooms. Alto
gether it is a stupendous proposition,
and if it succeeds should be the mecca
of American golfers. It is under
stood that the golf courses are to be
laid out under the supervision of
Walter J. Travis and that they will
all be seeded and laid out by next
August. Present plans have it that
the hotel and all five golf courses will
be opened during the season of 1922.
The land is described by one who is
familiar with it as being ideal golf
country, free from high hills, and of
sufficiently rolling character to fash
ion five very fine golf courses.

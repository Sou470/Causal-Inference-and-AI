No Flaw in District Titles.
Title claims have been filed to large
tracts of land within the District by
discoverers of an alleged flaw in the
titles by which those areas are held
by their present owners. At the rate
of $1 an acre virtually the entire busi
ness portion of the capital has been
covered by these clai>s, under a
statute of 180T, which Vforbids posses
sion by any person of any land ceded
to the United States which has not
been previously sold, ceded or leased
by the United States.
No concern need be felt by the pres
ent "owners" of the lands in question.
80 far as their titles are good in pres
ent law and they have paid their
taxes they are perfectly secure in pos
session and ownership. The law of
1807 does not apply to the lands trans
ferred by Maryland to the United
States in the formation of the federal
district known as the District of Co
lumbia. By that cession the state of
Maryland did not cede may land to.
the United States. It simply trans
ferred to the federal government the
sovereignty vested in those lands, just
as in the recent cession by Denmark
of the ^Virgin Islands to the United
States all that passed was the sover
eignty, and not the title to specific
.areas, which remained undisturbed.
Such lands as were owned by the state
of Maryland at the time of the pas
sion passed to the United States di
rectly, as in the Virgin Islands the
lands actually owned by the Danish
government became the property of
the United States. Private ownership
was not in any manner disturbed.
The promoters of this scheme to take
over enormous areas and values for

appointed. It is to be hoped that they

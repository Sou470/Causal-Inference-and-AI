TO TELL OF MABNE BATTLE.
Gen. U. G. McAlexander Will Be
Honor Guest at Press Club.
Brig. Gen. U. G. McAlexander, who
as colonel of the 38th Infantry, 3d
Division, won the title of "Rock of
the Marne" in the famous second bat
tle of the Marne. will be the guest
of honor at the National Press Club
next Friday night, at which time he
will give a first hand account of one
of the most glorious pages in Ameri
can military history.
Maps and battle plans not hitherto
made public will be shown -and dis
cussed by this officer, who in his mili
tary career has won the distinguished
service cross, the distinguished serv
ice medal, the croix de guerre with
two palms, and the Italian war cross.
Gen. McAlexander, a native of Min
nesota. first saw service in the In
dian campaigns of 1S90 and 1891. later
fighting the Spaniards in Cuba in
1S98 and the insurrectionists in the
Philippines in 1900. He went to
France with the 16th Infantry. 1st
Division, and after commanding the
18th Infantry of the same division for
a time was placed in command of the
38th Infantry. Shortly after the sec
ond battle of the Marne he was made
brigadier general and given com
mand of the 180th Infantry brigade,

Amy of Hold-Oats Expected'
The plaint of the hold-out is being
heard in the land. Already stories of
excessive demands by diamond ath
letes for their services are seeping in
from the various places where *he
players are hibernating for the win
ter. and fudged by the tenor of their
demands this year's crop of "dissatis
fied" talent promises to be the largest
in the history of the sport. The per
formers have been all fed up on
stories of prosperity that were heard
on every hand at the recent'base ball
meetings in Xew York. According to
the information disseminated nearly
every club made money, and some of
them scads of it. The players are
bound to insist on being declared in
on the prosperity, and for fat chunks
of it. too. The owners undoubtedly
are planning general increases for the
deserving toilers on their rosters, but
the stars and those who consider them
selves entitled to this classification,
which means nearly all of them, will
be seeking thousands where hundreds
are offered, and a merry correspond
ence war is In prospect when con
tracts are sent out next month.
All indications point to the cost of
base ball soaring next season, not only
in thi major leagues, but in the mi
nors as well. The salary lists of play
ers have almost doubled in three years
in the Eastern League, where the sal
ary limit was $1,800 in 1918. Last sea
son it was boosted to $2,500 and next
year it wiU'be 83.500. General raising I
of salary limits throughout the minors
is regarded as necessary, especially in
the class AA organizations. Last sea
son the International League operated
under a salary limit of 83,500, while
for next year this has been increased
to $5,000, and similar action is ex
pected in the American Association and
the Pacific Coast League.
Few Players Are Sl?aed.
In the majors few players are signed
for next year. The practice of offering
long-term contracts was done away
with just prior to the war. the mana
gers having found that in many in
stances it caused the athletes to take
things easy and also caused dissatis
faction among the other players
This alone is conducive to increas
ing the hold-out army, but the play
ers next year probably will find them
selves more handicapped in pushing
their demands than last season, when
operations with the minors had ceascd
and the supply of talent was limited.
Next year it is expected the majors
and minors will be working under a
new agreement of some kind, and
unless a hold-out is a real star he
may find his place filled with a
youngster If his demands are too stiff
More young players probably will
be tried out at the spring training
camps next March than ever before
for there is no limit on the time
American League clubs may spend in
the south, and the national leaguers
have taken off the ban and legalized
six weeks as the period for condi
tioning:.
Altogether the season of 1920 prom
ises to be the biggest, best and most
prosperous in the history of base ball
Only the factional scrap in the Amer
ican League now threatens general
serenity and it is believed this will
be ironed out before the pennant cam
paign gets under way.

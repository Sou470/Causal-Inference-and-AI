MISS A. M. WINCHESTER DIES.
Was Bureau of Education Kinder
garten Specialist for Six Years.
Almira M. Winchester, specialist in
kindergarten education in the bureau of
education for the past six years, and
formerly a prominent official in the In
ternational Kindergarten Union, died
this morning at Providence Hospital,
following an operation.
Miss Winchester was a native of St.
Catherine's, Ontario. Canada. She en
tered the United States government
service from New York, where she had
been educational "director of the Na
tional Kindergarten Association. Prior
to that she had been director of kinder
gartens and head of kindergarten train
ing schools in Fort Worth, Tex., and
elsewhere. She was the author of
numerous bulletins and reports issued
by the government on kindergarten edu
cation, and at the time of her death
wa& engaged in extensive kindergarten
organization work.

A Way to Stop Speeding.
To the Editor of TTic Star:
Manv of the traffic accidents are
caused' by reckless driving while striv
ing to pass street cars in order to avoid
being held up at the corner or at e
car-stop station. Any close observer of
traffic will notice daily these dangerous
spurts of speed. The most practical and
most easily enforced remedy is to adopt
a law or regulation to prevent an auto
mobile from passing a street car. except
when the latter has stopped on account
of a tie-up of the line. Such a law
would divert considerable traffic from
crowded streets. Laws against speed
ing are all right when you can enforce
them; but it takes an officer on a mo
tor cycle to do that.
Chauffeurs would not dare spurt by
a street car If there were a law against
it They would be easily caught.

service, lack of ocean bottoms, and

IN DRIVES OPPOSED
"Public Nuisance," Rhode Is
land Avenue Citizens' As
sociation Is Told.
Characterising the canvassing of the
schools of Washington to help swell va
rious fund drives as "a public nui
sance," the Rhode Island Avenue Sub
urban Citizens' Association, at the regu
lar meeting of the organization last
night, voiced its protest against such
practices. The meeting, which was held
in Sherwood IVesbyterian Church, was
presided over by William S. Torbert,
president of the association.
Emmet Midkiff, a member of the asso
ciation, declared that the children of
Washington have been in many In
stances "forced to contribute" to the
various funds with which the city "has
been inflicted in the last few weeks."
"Demerits for Failure."
Teachers, he said, pass out "demerit
marks" freely to those pupils who fail
to bring In their quota of the drives.
Mr. Torbert said the practice is un
doubtedly a burden on the children and
parents, but, he added, it is likewise a
burden on the teachera He said the
teachers would be the first to rejoico if
the board of education would place a
ban on canvassing in the public schools.
Without discussion, the association
reiterated its opposition to any change
in the fiscal relations between the Dis
trict government and that of the Unit
ed States. The organization also went
on record as favoring an additional
water supply for the city.
Waldo Warland Stevens, who spoke
on better protection for the District, de
clared men are hesitant about accepting
jobs on the police force when they know
the courts are slow in convicting male
factors. He cited several cases where
policemen were shot and killed, and the
criminals' trials were delayed.
Calls for Speedy Justice.
"The only way to obtain an ade
quate police force and cut down the
present crime wave appreciably." Baid
Mr. Stevens, "is to urge that speedy
justice be meted out to all offenders.
The police of Washington have done
nobly. They deserve our support, but
they have been woefully handicapped.
They will not stay in the police de
partment when the courts delay trial
in the cases of men who snuff out the
lives of their comrades."
The Rhode Island Avenue Suburban
Citizens' Association is now the eighth
largest association of its kind in
Washington, President Torbert an
nounced. Two hundred and twenty
five new members were taken in last
night, bringing the total membership
up to 560. An effort will be made to
boost this figure to 600 within the
next month.
A number of Improvements in
streets and sidewalks were asked by
members. The District Commission
ers will be asked to lay a sidewalk
between Girard and Hamlin streets,
in ..0th street, and to re-surface
Brentwood road between South Da
kota avenue and the District line
Plans for an entertainment, to be!
given late in January, were discussed.

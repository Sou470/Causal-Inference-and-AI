G. D. MILLER IS NAMED
FOR CUSTOMS POSITION
Will Succeed Capt. John T. Vance
in Post of Dominican
Receivership.
GEORGE DONALD MILLER.
President Wilson has named George
Dohald Miller of 3327 P street north
west to be deputy general receiver
of Dominican customs, to succeed
Capt. John T. Vance of Lexington,
Ry., who tendered his resignation a
few days ago. The post is a lucrative
one and the appointment does not
require confirmation by the Senate.
Mr. Miller has been on a visit to. his
I home, In Washington, and will sail
shortly for Santo Domingo.
For the past six years Mr. Miller
has been connected with the Domini
can customs service in the capacities
of Inspector, assistant auditor and
secretary, and his experience In all
branches of customs work and knowl
edge of Spanish tits him for his new
post.
The Dominican customs receiver
ship was created under the American
Dominican convention of 1907, when a
129.000.000 loan was negotiated to re
lieve the Dominican Republic of its
financial difficulties.
The customs receipts amount to
about $5,000,000 annually, part of
which is applied in monthly install
ments to liquidate the fiscal debt,
through the Guaranty Trust Company
of New York.
C. H. Baxter of New Jersey is the
general receiver of Dominican cus
toms. The personnel of the customs
service is composed mostly of Do
minicans, with Americans in the ex
ecutive positions. The central office
of the customs service is located in
the capital, Santo Domingo City.

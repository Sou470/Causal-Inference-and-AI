Thefts of groceries, clothing, shoes,
etc.. from consignments in transit by
freight and express to this city have
reached such proportions in recent
months, according to assertions of
many Washington merchants, that the
aggregate value of the goods stolen
reaches far into the thousands of dol
lars. Hitter protests were made today
by merchants at the inability of rail
road and local detectives and police to
locate the thieves and put an end to
the pilfering, which, it was frankly
declared, has become an actual factor
in the mounting cost of living.

One retail merchant today said that
about two weeks ago a consignment

freight from a factory ill Pqnnsyl'

the shoes stolen. The tops of the pack
ing cases had been removed sorfie

removed from each case, after which
the cases had been nailed up again.
Even consignments of low-priced
jewelry sent by express, it was de
clared today, have been opened in
transit, part of the contents removed
and the packages resealed. In only a
few instances, it was declared, have
thieves been located and arrested for

Merchants are making many com

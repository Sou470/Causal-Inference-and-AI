400 feet.
Crags and pinnacles, which had been
features of the volcano landscape for
two years, toppled into the depths,
and the famous fire lakes were con
verted into molten maelstroms.
The lava pillars, according to ob
servers, fell back rapidly, preceded by
outpourings of molten lava over the
floor of the great crater and accom
panied by a succession of earthquake
shocks. Great avalanches crashed
from the walls of the pit of Kala
mauma the "house of fire,** releasing:
torrents of imprisoned molten lava
which plunged into the cavern below. I
The sudden release of deep-seated
gas pressure, due to the recent violent
eruption of the neighboring volcano

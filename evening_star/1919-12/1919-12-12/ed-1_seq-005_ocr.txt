Wednesday morning following an
operation for stomach trouble.
Judge Robinson, as he was familiarly
known, came to Washington from
Missouri fifteen years ago as a mem
ber of the board of pension appeals.
He was born in Coschocton. Ohio, May
18, 1848, and was educated in the
schools there and at Ohio Wesleyan
University. He served during the
civil war with Company I, Ohio Vol
unteer Engineers, enlisting at the age
of sixteen years.
Shortly after the close of the war
he moved to Unionville, Mo., where
he practiced law fon thirty years, tak
ing a prominent part in politics of
the state.
He was a member of Unionville
Lodge, No. 210, of the Masons, of
which he was past master for five

CHINA EXCHANGE HITS ARMY
House Takes Up Gold Depreciation
There Affecting Pay.
Plans to adjust the pay of Ameri
can troops stationed in China to meet
j the depreciation of American gold
there were taken up today by the
House military committee.
"China is the only place in the
world where the exchange rate is so
against us," said Representative An
thony, republican, Kansas, acting
chairman of the committee.
The War Department informed the
committee that American gold was
being accepted from the troops only
at a rate of 61 cents in Chinese silver
on the dollar.

PREDICTS GEN. WOOD
WILL BE G. 0. P. NOMINEE
Senator Moses of New Hampshire
Declares That Political History
Is Repeating Itself.
Senator Moses of New Hampshire,
the native state of Maj. Gen. Leonard
Wood, in a statement today declared
that the general would be selected as
the republican nominee for President
when the national convention meets
in Chicago. He said:
"Political history is repeating itself.
In every quarter of the Union a
forced crop of favorite sons is ap
pearing in the vain hope of stopping
the progress of the movement to make
Leonard Wood the next President.
Neither 1916 nor 1908 has receded so
far into history as to cause thought
ful republicans to forge the futility
of an allied movement against a can
didacy which is truly national in
character.
"The Wood organization is complet
ed under the leadership of John T.
King, national committeeman from
Conecticut. The preliminary field
work has been attended to in sub
stantially every state. Under the di
rection of William Cooper Proctor of
Ohio (he Wood League is now being
organized to supplement the other ac
tivities of Gen. Wood's friends and
to correlate the whole endeavor to
make him the. nominee.
"Of the success of that endeavor
there can be no doubt. Not since
June, 1913, has there been so numer
ous and so widely representative a
company of republicans assembled as
that which assembled here this week
in connection with the meeting of the
national committee: and the testimony
from every quarter is tubstantiaJly
and incontrovertible as to Gen. Wood's
present and increasing strength.
Clearly, it is Wood against the field,
and the field is preparing the usual
campaign which such a situation in
dicates. It need not surprise the
general's friends to find that the crop
of favorite tons is immediately to be
followed by a crop of fallacious rumor,
innuendo and possible defamation. But
the situation is well in hand. South
Dakota is not a sporadic incident in
the campaign. It is the forerunner of
other successes yet to come."

j TWO-PLATOON SYSTEM IN
SCHOOLS TO BE TRIED
The platoon system will be inaug
i urated in the local public schools on
February 1, at the Park View white
j school and the Mott colored school, Su
i perintendent of Schools Thurston an
1: nounced today.
Although the plan is being tried out
primarily to make it possible to accom
modate more children until additional
buildings can be erected, school officials
regard the system as a progressive
move in educational methods.
The platoon "plan does not mean two
shifts of half-day classes Under the
i platoon plan tha. children' continue to
receive a full day of instruction.
I j The plan is briefly as follows: One i
1' group of classes uses the classrooms
for the basic subjects, such as gram- j
j mar and arithmetic, while another
group is assembled in the auditorium
for physical culture, music, or any of
the other subjects which can be taught
II to several classes at a time. At stated
times during the day the two groups j
change back and forth from the audi
torium or other special subject rooms
to the regular classrooms. In this way
it is possible to accommodate many
more children.

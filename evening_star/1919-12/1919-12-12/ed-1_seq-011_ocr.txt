ROBERT J. THOMPSON SAILS.
Former Consul Resigned in 1915 on
Account of War Attitude.
GENOA, Tuesday, December 9.?Rob
ert J. Thompson, formerly American
consul at Aix-la-Chapelle, who resigned
his post in 1915 while on a visit to the
United States and later returned to
Europe to reside at The Hague, sailed
today for New York on board the steam
er Canoplc.
At the time of Mr. Thompson's resig
nation it was stated that he asserted
his action was due to his dissatisfaction
with tH? American government's atti
tude toward Germany in the war. After
his return to The Hague the Overseas
News Agency, the German propaganda
bureau, sent out a wireless dispatch
saying that Mr. Thompson had sub
scribed $125,000 to the German war loan
then being negotiated.

Stephens, the former domestic serv
ant who waa recently elected to a seat
on the borough council of Bermondsey,
a London suburb, saxs that municipal
work Is the first step In the direction
of the house of commons. In an inter
view she spoke with quiet confidence
of her future arrival in parliament and
gave many instances of how work
could be saved for women. She told

the way to reform. My first-hand
knowledge of housework gave me
many Ideas to base my political plans

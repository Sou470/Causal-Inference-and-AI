Organization Reveals
Activity Here.
In the early days of the war, before
the government undertook the hous
ing problem in Washington, the Dis
trict Council of Defense directed 27.
685 war workers to homes that had
rooms for rent, according to the final
report of the council, just published.
The report shows that during- Feb
ruary. March and April of 1918, when
the influx of government employes
was at its height, the Council of De
fense was able to keep approximately
3.000 rooms on hand to house the new
comers.
The report also shows how the
people of the District helped to con
serve the nation's food supply during
the war by the development of home
gardens under the leadership of the
Council of Defense. In 1917, the re
port shows, food valued at $360,000
was raised in Washington. In 1918
the amount was increased to more
than $1,000,000 as a result of improve
ment in the soil and broader knowl
edge gained by the people during the
first vear.
Other activities connected directly
and indirectly with the prosecution
of the war which were furthered by
the council were: Recruiting for the
military service, organizing the boys
working reserve army, forming of a
committee of four-minute men who
spoke throughout the city in the ni
terest of war movements, mobilizing
mechanics to work in the shipyards.
Americanizing of aliens and the chil
dren's, year campaign conducted by
the Department of Labor to improve
the health of the youth of the coun
try,
The report was forwarded to the
Commissioners by William H. Bald
win. who served as chairman or the

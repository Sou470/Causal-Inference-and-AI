ASKS MOKE PAY IN CENSUS.
Director Needs $2,500,000 Extra
for Statistics Takers.
The fourteenth decennial census has
been hit by the high cost of living.
Enumerators cannot make a living wage
on the old per capita basis and pro
vision must be made for a per diem
wage or the payment of traveling ex
penses, Sam L. Rogers, director of the
census, said in asking Congress for
$2,500,000 in addition to $15,000,000 al
ready granted for the expenses of the
census.
The additional funds sought would be
used to increase the enumerator's pay
over the proposed basis of 4 cents for
each inhabitant; 30 cents for each farm
or industry and 50 cents for each irri
gation or drainage enterprise.

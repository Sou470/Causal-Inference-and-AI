SOCIETIES PLANS WORK

The league of the Red Cross societies i
which was formed in Paris some months
ago is being rapidly organized for in

ish, French, Italian and Japanese Red
Cross societies. The following national
societies have recently become mem
bers: Argentina, Australia, Belgium,
Brazil, Canada, China, Cuba, Denmark,
Greece, Holland, India, New Zealand,
Norway. Peru, Portugal, Rumania, Ser
bia, South Africa, Spain, Sweden and
Venezuela. I
In the departments of public health
and hygiene, bureaus will be organized
to deal with the subjects of child wel
fare, tul>erculosis, malaria, preventive
medicine, venereal diseases and nursing.
The recent work of the interallied
medical commission sent by the Red
Cross L#eague to investigate the pan-
demie of typhus in Poland, and the re- j
suit of this work in its recommenda
tions as to the advisability of estab
lishing sanitary conditions to suppress
the spread of typhus into adjacent ter- I
ritories, is a practical illustration of
the efficiency of this great humane
work.

last year's figures by 27 per cent, ac
cording to the report.
Sugar exports for the ten-month pe
riod were valued at nearly $97,000,000,
while the value of the sugar imported
was about ?4S1,424,000, according to the
report.

chiefly re-imported sugar, bought in the
West Indies and sent to this country

TRAFFIC VIOLATION
CASES SHOW BIG DROP
There were only forty-two cases of
traffic violations on the docket of the
District of Columbia branch of the
Police Court today, compared with
250 cases last Monday. While the de
crease of 208 cases may have been
partly due to the campaign conducted
by the police against traffic violators,
officials of the Police Court believe
that the snow was responsible.
There were twenty-two cases of
ordinary violations, in which the de
fendant forfeited from $2 to $5; there
were two esses where the defendant
was charged with driving while in
toxicated, the parties in each case
forfeiting $50; one case where a de
fendant was charged with collision
and forfeited $5: three cases where
the defendants failed to observe the
fifteen-foot rule to stop in the rear
of street cars loading and unloading
passengers; one forfeited $5, and one $15
and one 140. There were twelve cases
of speeding, in which the defendants
forfeited $10 each. Eight cases
charged intoxication, in which the de
fendants forfeited $10.

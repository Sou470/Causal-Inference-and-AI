Here is a little advice to owners of motor
cars:
It is in violation of the police regulations to
leave an automobile on a street unattended unless
it is securely locked. Automobiles so locked are
not usually stolen. Cars with the same style of
starting key should be equipped with special locks
if they are intended to be protected against theft.
Section 4. article XXVI, says:
"Each person to whom these regu
lations refer shall be provided with
a lock suitable to lock the starting
lever, throttle or switch by which the
vehicle is set in motion, and no per
son shall allow any motor vehicle
operated by him or her to stand or
re-main unattended on any of the
streets, avenues, roads, alleys, parks,
parkways, or other public places,
without having first locked the lever,
throttle or switch by which the ve
hicle may be started."

How motor trucks are reducing
transportation and distribution costB
and wherein lies their value as auxil

of the topics-to be discussed at the
conferences by transportation experts
at the national motor truck shows to
be held at New York and Chicago next
month. The New York motor truck
show will be held January 3-10, and
the one at Chicago will be held Jan
uary 24-31.
Transportation and distribution of
food and materials, it is said, is
cheaper by trucks than by conven
tional carriers, except on what is
known as long hauls. This has led
lo the development of a large num
ber of heavy haulage companies
operating fleets of trucks on sched
ules. Many of them cover as high as
150 miles on regular routes and have
contracts with other trucking com
panies to forward goods. The east is
honeycombed with motor truck com
panies of this type, and manufactur
ers have for some time been shipping
long distances by truck. It is not Un
common for a manufacturer in Spring
field.. Mass., for example, to ship Ave
or ten tons of his product to Balti
more. the goods being forwarded
through different trucking companies
whose radius of operation is about
100 miles. Despite these long dis
tances the truck operators are able
to quote a better price than the rail
roads and still make money, because
the costs of pick-up and deliver to
the railroad and from the latter to the
consignees are eliminated by the
truck.
Decrease Traaaportation Coat.
With the increase of good roads?
highways constructed to sustain large
capacity trucks?the cost of transpor
tation and distribution by trucks will
decrease. Use of trailers carrying a
load several times the capacity of the
truck is another factor tending to de
crease truck transposition costs.
This is a conditiomiiametrically op
posite to that which will obtain when
the railroads are returned to private
owners, for new equipment and in
creased wages spell higher costs for
this method of transportation.
It is pointed out that the utility of
the motor truck flupt made itaelf con
spicuous during the freight conges
tion in 1917. -when the trucks hauled
about 1,200,000,000 tons of materials
and merchandise that the railroads
could not handle at a saving of
$360,000,000. computed on a ton-mile
basis, under the cost of transporting
the goods by horses, which would have
been necessary had the truck not
stood ready.
Cheap transportation is a direct con
cern of the consumer, and it is expect
ed that the general public will be
well represented at the transporta
tion conferences. Sessions will be
held in the afternoon and evening on
each day of the show, so that visitors
to the passenger car exhibition,
which will be held at Grand Central
Palace during the same week, may
have an opportunity of visiting the
motor truck exhibition during either
the afternoon or evening session.

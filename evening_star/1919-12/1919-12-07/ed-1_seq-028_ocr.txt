DOUBLE-HEADERS FOR HARVARD
FOOT BALL TEAM NEXT SEASON
CAMBRIDGE, Mass., December 6
The double-header is to appear on
college foot ball schedules next sea
son. The Harvard foot ball team in
its first week end of play will meet
two college elevens and may take on
two more for its second date. Fred
W. Moore, graduate manager of ath
letics at Harvard, said today that the
games as proposed would be of four
periods of eight minutes each.
The idea, according to Mr. Moore, is
based on the fact that the principal
value of early season games is to de
termine the quality of Individual play
ers and to obtain as much practice
for the team as possible. The smaller
colleges usually have little practice
for early games and long periods are
a hardship on their men, especially
when Harvard and other large col
leges are able, because of the large
number of playere, to send fresh men
in to play in late periods. The short
periods are intended to save the
strength of the small college men and
the two games will give Harvard an
opportunity to try out all its., players,
Mr. Moore said.
Bowdoin College has been invited
by Harvard to play one of the games
on the opening date and the Univer
sity of Maine has been invited to play
one of the games on the second date.

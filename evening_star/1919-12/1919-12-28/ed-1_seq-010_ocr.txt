LITHUANIA RECOGNIZED.
French Action Follows That Taken
by Britain and Italy.
The independence of Lithuania has
been recognized by France, following
similar action by Great Britain and
Italy, according to information to the
State Department from Paris.
The French government has estab
lished full diplomatic and consular
relations with the new nation.
It is declared In official quarters
here that the United States does not
now contemplate recognition of Lith
uania because of the fear that rec
ognition would promote dismember
ment of Russia, to which this govern
ment is opposed.

SPANIARDS PARTICULAR.
Question of Diet Added to Problems
of Wages and Hours.
Industrial troubles in Spain have
the question of diet added to the
problems of wages and hours, accord
ing to a report at tho Department
of Commerce yesterday.
Before making a contract to cut a
crop of sugar cane Spanish worktnen
recently made the following demands:
A daily wage of 10 pesetas ($1 75).
three heavy meals, breakfast of eggs
and meat, dinner of soup and meat
stew, supper of bacon, green vege
tables and "gazpacho"; hours of work,
from sun to sun, with two hours for
siesta, or noonday nap; forty minutes
for each meal, three smoking periods
of twenty minutes each and one for
thirty minutes and a bed with two
mattresses.

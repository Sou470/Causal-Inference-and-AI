WINS SUIT AS TO AUTO
STOLEN FROM GARAGE
Cumberland, M4-. Physician Fully
Recompensed. Machine Being
Recovered and Sold.
An interesting suit has recently
been decided in Maryland, which
might prove of interest to local pro
prietors of garages and owners of
automobiles. The suit in question
was instituted by a physician of Cum
berland. Md.. against a garage.
The physician left his car at the
garage on December 13, 1918. Return
ing two days later, he presented his
ticket for the car. but the garage
owners were unable to produce it.
Later it developed that the car had
been driven out of the garage by an
unknown person. The machine was
filled with whisky and driven to
Georgia. Several months later the
car was recovered and returned to
Cumberland It was sold for J2.100.
In his suit the physician alleged
negligence on the part of the garage
proprietors on the ground that they
had permitted the car to leave the
premises without presentation of tie
proper check and also because proper
steps had not been taken to identify
the person claiming the machine.
For the garage proprietors it was
contended that they had not been
negligent and that notices were post
ed in the garage disclaiming any lia
bility for the theft. The verdict was
for the difference between the value
of the automobile and expenses in
curred by the physician in its re
covery and the proceeds of the sale,
which amounted to $1,818.

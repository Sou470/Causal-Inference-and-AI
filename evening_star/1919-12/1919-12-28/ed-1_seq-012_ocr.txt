MILITARY HONORS GIVEN
AT FUNERAL OF ALCOCK
MANCHESTER. England, December
27.?Funeral services were held here
today for Capt. Sir John Alcock. the
first aviator to make a non-stop air
plane flight across the Atlantic, who
died at Rouen France, as the result
of injuries received when his plane
crashed December 18 near Cottevrard,
in Normandy. Thousands of persons,
including numerous army officers and
airmen, attended.
The route from the church to the
cemetery was lined with admirers of
the late aviator here and full military
honors were rendered at the grave.
Airmen acted as pallbearers. As
the funeral ceremony ended buglers
sounded the last post.

Cathedral here today for memorial
services held in tribute to Capt. Sir
John Alcock, whose funeral was held
in Manchester today.

FRENCH OF NORTH FAVOR
1919 INCOME TAX ONLY
LILLE, France, Friday, December 26.
?The members of the Association of
War Sufferers of the Department of the
North at a meeting here today adopted a
resolution declaring that, while they
recognize It Is the duty of even' French
man to fulfill his legal obligations, the
members of the association consider
that the administration has no right to
demand any declaration of incomes ex
cept one concerning income for 1919, and
in the case of 1919 incomes it is only
right that there should be a delay until
the financial situation of each taxpayer
becomes clear.
The resolution points out that the in
vaded regions, instead of receiving in
comes during ther period of occupation,
were obliged to borrow money; that
laws concerning the collection of an in
come tax never have been promulgated
in the invaded regions, and. therefore,
they should not be asked to make decla
rations for the years of German occu
pation and should receive a long delay
for 1919.

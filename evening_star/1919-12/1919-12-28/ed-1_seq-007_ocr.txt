15,271 OVERSEAS MEN
IN U. S. ARMY HOSPITALS
Excellent Health Reported Among
Troops Here, With Few Cases
of Sickness in Germany.
According to the surgeon general of
the Army, there are still 15,271 over
seas patients under treatment at gen
eral and base hospitals in this coun
try. The commissioned strength of
medical, dental, sanitary and veteri
nary corps on active duty is stated at
3,206, in addition to which there are
7,768 officers in the reserve corps. Ex
cellent health conditions are reported
among troops in the United States,
with only a few sporadic cases of
epidemic diseases. There were lateT?
a few cases of diphtheria, measles, in
fluenza and pneumonia among the
troops in Germany.
Improvement is reported in health
conditions in the American forces in
Siberia, there having been a falling
off in the number of cases of pneu
monia and influenza. There were
twenty-three deaths from pneumonia
in the Siberian command during the
week ended December 5. An out
break of influenza in the second week
of November sent 117 cases to the
hospitals, whereas the admissions on
that account for the week ended De
cember 5 were but 17.
The British steamer Princess May,
Jamaica, for New York, which sent
out wireless calls for assistance, has
arrived at Delaware breakwater.

Stringent Regulations Adopted by
Treasury to Close Last Chance j
for Contraband Shipments.
Stringent regulations were issued yes- i
terday by the Treasury to prevent In-
toxicants from entering the country on j
ships arriving from foreign ports. J
Liquors properly listed as sea stores i
must be placed under seal by the board-
ing officer of the customs service and
kept sealed during the entire time of
the vessel's stay in port. No part of
such stores may be removed from under J
seal for any purpose while the ship is
in United States waters, the regulations
state, which denies to foreign crews the
ration of wine served with meals on
some ships under other flags.
Excessive liquor stores, no longer be-
ing dutiable, are subject to seizure.
Liquors properly carried may be re- i
turned to a foreign port on the vessel's
changing from the foreign to the coast- j
ing trade, or may be transferred under
supervision of the customs officers from
a vessel ir. foreign trade, delayed in
port for any cause, to another vessel of
the same line or owner.
Customs officials are not greatly con- J
cerned over the possibility of smug
gling large stores of liquors into the
country and the new orders are believed
to close the last hole through which
intoxicants might reach domestic con
sumers. With all liquid refreshment
under seal, it was said, there woi'ld be
small opportunity for "bootlegging' to

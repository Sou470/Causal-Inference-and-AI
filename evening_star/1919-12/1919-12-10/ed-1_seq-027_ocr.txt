MAY HIT CANADIAN COINS
OTTAWA. December 10.?The high
price which bar silver has been com
manding in the metal markets of the
world may necessitate a reduction of
the fineness of the alloy from which
Canadian silver coins are struck, it
was authoritatively .stated here today.
With bullion prices at the present
level there is too great a tendency
to withdraw coins from circulation
and melt them for commercial uses.
It was said. So great has been the
demand for silver coinage of late
that the belief is strong that a con
siderable quantity of it Is being con
verted into bullion. The Canadian
mint here has been kept busy to meet
the demand for silver coinage and i
minting is not a prolitable business
with bar silver so expensive.
To tMBen the temptation to With
draw Canadian coins from circula
tion to be melud and so!:l for bullion
it now is proposed to reduce the per
centage 01 pure silver in them. Just
what standard may be adopted has
not been indicated. The change can
not be made, however, before the end
of the year, because the Dominion
cannot issue coins of two different
standards of fineness in the same year.
The fact that Canadian silver coins
sell at a discount of 5 cents in New
York is an additional factor in en
couraging the melting process, and it
is believed here that a good deal of
coin has be*n exported there. Cana
dian coins contain 92per cent pure
silver, while those of the Tnited

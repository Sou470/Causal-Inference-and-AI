COMMISSIONER BROWNLOW
OUTLINES RULES FOR LIGHTS
Commissioner Brownlow today issued the following statement
to the public regarding coal conservation measures to be taken in
the District of Columbia:
The order of the federal fuel administration governing the i
use of heat, light and power was issued under authority of la-w
and has the force of law. The power of the Lever act is behind
it, and all local authorities will co-operate with the federal agencies
in securing prompt abedience. Cases of violation will be reported
by the police to the United States district attorney.
It is required that retail stores, except food and drug stores, j
shall limit their use of light to six hours daily. The movement
for the uniform store hours of 12 noon to 6 in the afternoon has 1
the hearty support of the Commissioners, since it will insure a
uniform observance of the order. Most cities have adopted these
hours. Baltimore today went on a schedule of from 11:30 a.m. to
5:30 p.m. The new hours should be in effect tomorrow, Thurs
day, December 11. Food and drug stores must reduce their light
ing one-half.
The use of electric light for illuminated signs and for show
window displays cannot be permitted under the regulations. Some
streets last night were ablaze with lighting that cannot be con
tinued under the order of the federal authorities.
Lights in office buildings must be extinguished at 4 p.m. This
i rule is in effect today, and all persons should take notice from the
announcements in the newspapers, as it is impracticable_ for the
j federal authorities to serve individual notices upon the citizens.
The situation demands the co-operation of all citizens. 1 am
confident that compliance will be secured by voluntary action and
that it will not be necessary to institute prosecutions under the
Lever act.
Washington shoppers must do all their buying between the
hours of 12 o'clock noon and 6 p.m., beginning tomorrow. This
was the decision of the Merchants and Manufacturers' Associa
tion at a special meeting in the Raleigh Hotel today.
Complying with the order of Dr. Harry Garfield, federal fuel
administrator, issued Monday, and the order of Commissioner
Brownlow, made public today, to conserve coal, all retail stores in
Washington, except _tao4- and, drug stores, tomorrow will begin
opening for business at noon and will close at 6 o'clock sharp.
NO UNNECESSARY LIGHTS.
During the six-hour period no outline, sign, show window or
other unnecessary lights will be permitted in the stores. Heat is
also to be cut down, the temperature permitted being not more
than 68 degrees. Use of electric power will be permitted only for
absolutely necessary purposes.
Clerks and other employes in the stores will be required to
have their luncheons before reporting for work at noon daily, and
the entire six-hour period is to be devoted to the transaction of
business.
It is expressly stipulated that food stores and drug stores do not
come under the provisions of the order, save as to the use of un
necessary lights, show-window lights, unnecessary heat or un
necessary use of electric power.

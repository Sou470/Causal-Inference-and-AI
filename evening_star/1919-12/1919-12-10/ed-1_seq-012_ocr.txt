Centralization of all matters of
transportation into one service under
the W ar Department is recommended
by Brig. Gen. Frank T. Hines. chief of
transportation service, in his annual
report made public today. "The idea
of central transportation control."
says he. "has not been carried to its
logical conclusion in the United
States in that the Motor Transport
[t'orps exists today as an independent
I agency of transportation and the con
trol of military railroads in the the
ater of operations is a matter under
j the control of the chief of engineers.
It is believed that economy in admin
istration. efficiency in operation, and
greater co-ordination justify beyond
any question of doubt all transporta
tion for the War Department being
combined into one service alon^ sub
stantially the following lines: A trans
portation corps, to be charged with
complete supervision over all matters
of transportation for all branches of
the War Department. Unquestionably
the successful movement of men and
war materials throughout the United
States and overseas was made pos
sible only bj> reason of a centrally
controlled transportation system. In
view of the fact that the United States
Kailroad Administration will be dis-
solved by January 1. 1920. and that
in all probability the railroads will
be returned to private ownership on
that date, it is recommended that
steps be taken at this time to pre
pare some arrangement whereby an
agency will be established at ^rash
ington. D. C.. to represent all the

GUARD HERE PUT AT 238!
A War Department statement shows
that the present enlisted strength of
the National Guard of the District of
Columbia is 238, being 30 per cent of
its authorized enlisted strength of
801. The present enlisted strength of
the entire National Guard of the
United States is stated at 35.S27,
being 23 per cent of the authorized
enlisted strength of 153,285. The guard
of Minnesota ^nd Texas is slightly
above the authorized strength of those
states.
Figures for authorized strength are
based primarily on the appropriation
by Congress for the fiscal year 1920.
Some states have requested larger
guard organizations than government
funds provide for, agreeing to defray
the additional expense themselves. In
these cases the authorization has been
increased.
November 1 the actual strength was
29 per cent of that authorized, while
on December 1 it was only 23 per cent.
That decrease is primarily due to the
fact that since November 1 the au
thorized strength has been increased
from 126,109 to 153,285, or by more
than 27,000. There was, however, a
falling off in actual strength of 212

COMMUNITY TRUSTS
This is the name now given to charitable trusts. Modern
thought is beginning to focus rapidly on this class of trusts. Such
trusts are not created without throwing around them all possible
protection for future faithful administration. Since January 1.
19-14 twenty-two such trusts are known to have been established
in the United States. In every case the trustee has been a trust
company.
Expert knowledge and thorough organization are needed to ar
range properly the terms of a community trust and to execute
efficiently its provisions. It is therefore natural that The Washing
ton Loan and Trust Company should-be selected.
An illustration of the efficiency of such trusteeship by
this company is found in that notable charity, The John
Dickson Home of Washington.

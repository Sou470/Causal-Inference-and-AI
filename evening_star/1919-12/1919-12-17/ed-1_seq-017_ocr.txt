cusses President's Pro
posal With Senators.
One clause in the President's plan
for the settlement of the coal strike
accepted by the miners would bring
about an Increase in the miners'
wages of at least double the 14 per
cent proposed by Dr. Garfield while
fuel administrator, the Senate com
mittee investigating the strike set- i
tlement was told by Tracy W. Guthrie
pi esident of the Hillman Coal and

Mr. Guthrie was called to present

wages.
Say? 20 Per Cent Are Idle.
"About 20 per cent of miners are
idle all the time, according to our
experience with twenty-three mines,"
he told the committee. "That is,, we
have to have 120 names on the pay
roll in order to get 100 men on the
job as a constant factor. Miners lffce
to celebrate all American holidays and
a lot of foreign ones. Then they have
"Mitchell day," on April 1, in honor

'rabbit day' on November 10, when
everybody adjourns to shoot a rab
bit."
Senator Walcott, democrat, of Mary
land, drew out Mr. Guthrie's view of

wages involved in the settlement by
observing that Dr. Garfield's proposal
was the same as that put foi-th by the
President.
"As I read the memorandum given

Guthrie replied, "it iB proposed to set

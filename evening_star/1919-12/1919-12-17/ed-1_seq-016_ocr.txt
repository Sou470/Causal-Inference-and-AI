FAVORS JOINT BARGAINING.
In its first annual report issued today
the women's bureau of the I>epartment
of Labor recommended recognition of
the right of collective bargaining for
women in industry.
"The genuine co-operation essential to
production," the report says, "can be
secured only if definite channels of com
munication between employers and
groups of their workers are estab
lished."
Established a year and a half ago to
meet war-time industrial conditions the
women's bureau has, since the war,
been engaged in making surveys of con
ditions and problems affecting working
women.

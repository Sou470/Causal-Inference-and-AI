HOOVER DISMISSES
SAN FRANCISCO, December 24.?
Rumors that he would be a candidate
for the presidency were dismissed by
Herbert Hoover when he returned
here yesterday from the second in
dustrial conference at Washington
called by President Wilson. "I have
no political aspirations." said Mr.
Hoover, "and am perfectly contented
with my present work, that of pro
viding* three million European babies
with milk and healthy food."
Mr. Hoover said he believed the
situation in Kurope was improved
over last year, although Finland,
Poland. Czechoslavakia, Hungary and
Austria still are in a critical condi
tion. He recommended signing of the
peace treaty as soon as possible, and
said he believed the peak of indus
trial unrest in this country had been
reached.

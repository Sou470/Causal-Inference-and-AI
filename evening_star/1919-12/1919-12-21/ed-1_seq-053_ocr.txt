LADIES' TEAK IN DRILL.
Soon Expect to Hake Appearance
Before State Societies.
The All-States .Ladies' Drill Team,
made up of young- women from the
various states for the purpose of
physical culture and general military
drill work, drilled two hours last
night at the Thompson School Com
munity Center. The drill was under
the direction of Mrs. S. H. Severe.
The team has been organized since
early fall, and their training has ad
vanced to such a stage of perfection
that they are about, ready to make
their appearance before the various
state societies of the city.
Their initial appearance will be the
evening of January 2, before the
Pennsylvania State Society at the
Thompson School Center gymnasium.
As the winter progresses the team
will appear before the various state
societies.

Mrs. Ella E. Brooks, wife of Capt.
N. M. Brooks, a resident of this city
for forty-seven years, died Friday at
her residence, 224 A street southeast.
She had been ill less than three weeks.
Vr>. Brooks had been prominent In
Presbyterian work since 1874. when
she joined the Metropolitan Presby
terian, Church of Washington. She
was a charter member of the Woman'i
Missionary Society a fid its treasurer
for thirty-five years. Although active
in all the work of the church, Mrs.
Brooks was probably best known as a
Bible teacher, having at the time of
her death an organized class of twen
ty-five women of mature years, known
as "Mrs. Brooks' class."
Funeral services will be held to
morrow afternoon at 2 o'clock at her
residence Dr. Albert Evans will of

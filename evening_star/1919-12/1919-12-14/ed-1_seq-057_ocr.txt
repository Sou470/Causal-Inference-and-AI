Seating Your Guests.
Most women nowadays are syste
matic about the running of their
households?at least, they are sys
tematic if they are successful. So
most of them have several systems of
classification and indexing connected
with household accounts and provision
ing. To be sure, there Is the danger,
though it is small, of letting the
means for systematizing housework
obscure the end. and there are a few
scientific housekeepers who carry
their work a little too far. But their
work is, nevertheless, in the right
direction, and other women can learn
much from them.
Aside from the methods that have
been devised by the women who look
upon housekeeping as a scientific as
well as a business experiment, there
are various other methods and con
veniences for the busy housekeeper.
In the shops there is a little seating
pad. It is oblong, made of leather.
and it has around its edge various
little pockets that represent the
chairs about the dinner table. So the
hostess, puzzling over the seating of
her guests, does not have to scribble
down names on a sheet of paper
around an Imaginary table and erase
them orer and over ag9tn until she
gets the right arrangement. Instead,
she writes each guest's name on a
card and shuffles the cards about in
the little pockets until she has Just
the arrangement she wishes. Seating
guests at dinner has much to do with
the success of the dinner; and doubt
less there are many gyests in this
world who could wish their hostess
the possessor of one of these con
venient little seating pads.
In the household where there are
servants of sufficient number and In
telligence to look after such things
the hostess, after she has planned the
seating of the guests, can slip the
place cards into the proper pockets
and hand the seating pad over to
maid or butler.

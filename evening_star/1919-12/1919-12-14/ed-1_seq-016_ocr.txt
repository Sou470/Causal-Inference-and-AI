Because of the wide range of cloth
ing quality and styles the subcommit
tees do not believe a price list for
such articles would be practicable, for
a price that would represent a bar
gain for one class of goods would be
profiteering if charged for another
class.
Instead, close watch is being kept
of wholesale prices and invoices of
goods bought by local merchants, and
it is planned to organise a large
group of volunteer consumers, who
will turn in all their sales slips to
the committee, so that comparisons
can he made with wholesale prices
and any instances of overcharging
uncovered. Specific charges of
profiteering also will be unco%-ered.
When his attention was called to
reports that the Chicago fair price
committee had fixed the prices for
suits. shoes, overcoats. dresses,
hosiery, underwear and other articles
of clothing in that city, Mr. Wilson
said yesterday that he did not be
lieve an arbitrary price could be
set for such article* that would be
fair to all branches of the trade, and
that only comparison between the
wholesale and retail prices of each
article, or articles very similar, could
form the basis of a prosecution that
would stand the test of court action.

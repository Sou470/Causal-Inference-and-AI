NEW WAR SERVICE
HOME FOR THE K. OF C.
The Costello building-, at the south
west corner of 6th and G streets, has
been leased for a term of years by the
local branch of the Knights of Colum
bus, to be used as a war service
home. It is understood that the or
ganization will expend something like
J10.000 on the building in the way of
dormitories, reading rooms, pool
rooms. shower baths and lecture
rooms. The building is four stories
and contains approximately 12,000
square feet of floor space.
This activity, now housed at 606 E
street, known as the Knights of Co
lumbus Hall, will revert to the local
bodies of the Knights of Columbus,
where the meetings of the various
branches of the organization were
held prior to that building being used
for the war activities.
It is expected that the necessary re
pairs and additions will be completed
by January 1.

Demands the Right to Vote.
To the Editor of The Star:
Having purchased a home and be
come a bona* fide citizen of Washing
ton. I feel that I have a right to join
in the demand for the right of "self
determination." or the plain rights of i
citizenship accorded ail who live out-
side the District of Columbia.
While Washington is a beautiful j
city in which to live, the thought of
being disfranchised and put in a class
with Chinamen, lunatics, convicts, etc.. i
when It comes to exercising- the
greatest prerogative of American citi
zenship, "detracts greatly from the
pride and Joy one feels in living here.
it will not do to say that a man
can claim citizenship in the state
whence he came and vote there,
especially if ho is not connected with
the government, because his desire
to vote to promote the welfare and
happiness of the community in which
he lives Is the strongest incentive
for the ballot.
You cannot reconcile the right of
the government to' tax a man and
compel hiin to fight for his country
with a denial of the highest privilege
he is capable of enjoying.
The District of Columbia has as
many people and is just as important
as some states that have two sena
tors and two or three congressmen.

Government Pay Scale Chaos.
Flagrant instances of the salary in
equalities that prevail in the govern
ment pay scale have been brought to
light from time to time during the
researches of the reclassification com
mission, but none quite so glaring as
those affecting the telephone operators
of the various departments. It ap
pears, as shown by a news stoTy in
The Star of Friday, that there is no
uniformity whatever in this respect.
I'hone operators doing precisely the j
same kind of work are paid all the way
from $720 in the State Department to
$>,400 in the Treasury Department and
the Shipping Board. Not only is there
this gross disparity, but the workers
in the departments and bureans long
est established appear to be getting
less than those who work for the newer
e*>tablishments.
This situation strikingly illustrates
the need of reclassification and ad
justment. The same differences, per
haps not as marked in some cases,
but possibly more so in others, pre
vail respecting the clerical forces.
Men and women in one of the public
offices are getting much less than those
doing precisely the same grade of work
in' others. Promotions are more rapid
in. one branch of the service than in
another. Longevity counts for some
thing here, and for nothing there. Ex
perience is rated as valuable to the
government in one bureau and is re
warded accordingly, but counts for
nothing in others.
In vifew of the definite demonstra
tions so numerously given in the
coarse of the hearings that the gov
ernment's pay scale is grossly uneven,
as well as inadequate, it is confidently
expected that the commission will rec
ommend the adoption of a system of
payments that will take into account
the value of the work done, the ex
perience and skill of the workers, the
element of tenure of office, and, in
short, establish a scientific and busi
nesslike pay system.
This new system should be put into
effect as quickly as possible. The
work of the commission, involving an
immense labor, should not be pro
tracted for so long that conditions
will meanwhile have changed, necessi
tating new calculations and surveys.
It is vitally important to the welfare
of the government service as wejl as
of the workers themselves that the

no more delay than is required to

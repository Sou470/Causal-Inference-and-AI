Reformation is a matter of switch
ing.
After running along one track we
switch to another.
That is all there is to any chansre
in a man or his institutions: it is the
method in political revolution, busi
ness readjustment or religious con
version.
It is the trick of changing over
from one habit to another.
We all run in ruts. We are lik*
railroad trains, not like automobiles,
for we run on tracks.
Our mental wheels are flanged, and
if we get off the track, and don't get
oh another, we usually are ditched
Tragedy means the victim has got
off one track, but has not succeeded
in getting on another track.
Now. when we want to break a bad
habit, say drinking, or idling or
gambling, or profligacy, or ugly tem
per. or petulance, or any such thing,
one trouble is that, while we se?
clearly the track we want to go from,
we do not form a definite idea ol the
track we want to go to.
It is not so much ceasing the use
of alcoholic stimulants we should
keep in our minds, not this negative
idea, but the pleasure, comfort, health
and vigor that come from consuming
wholesome and rational nourishment.
Many a man ha& fully made up his
mind to quit booze, but merely sticks
there "all dressed up and nowhere
to go."
Nature abhors a vacuum
The soul abhors a "don't."
Show us what to do*
And it is important, not only to
have another track ready upon which
to go when we leave the old one.
but also to attend to the switch
That is to say. in supplanting a had
habit with a good one the attention
should be concentrated upon the net
itself of changing-.
For instance, in breaking yourself
of fits of ungovernable temper or or
sulking, it is not enough to u>e >our
imagination to depict to yourself how
much better you would appear if you
had poise and self-restraint, and if
you remained calm, unruffled and
cheerful under all provocations; von
must also use your imagination, and
your will and judgment as well, to
picture for yourself your right course
of action at the very moment of temp
tation.
It is not only how to act a ve?r
from now. or when you get old. that
you want to know and to practice,
but how to act at the particular junc
ture of time.
Tn other words, you must learn how
to throw the switch deftly, how to
get suddenly from the old road and
habit to the new without running
off the track.
If-you want to change successfully.
onrentrate upon the act of changing,
think of that, study that, practice
tb:?r. and do not waste all your moral
(enthusiasm in thinking how well you
are going to get along after the chance
has been made.
